# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: OFFICE OF MANAGEMENT AND BUDGET

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

OMB is the largest EOP component, established 1921 as Bureau of the Budget (reorganized 1970). With ~$132M budget and 510 FTEs, it prepares the federal budget, reviews regulations via OIRA, and coordinates executive branch policy. Central to Schedule F implementation.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Office of Management and Budget
**Founding:** 1921 (Bureau of the Budget); reorganized 1970 as OMB
**Current Director:** Russell Vought (February 6, 2025)
**Location:** Executive Office of the President, White House complex

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Budget Preparation
- Circular A-11 (July): Budget instructions
- Agency submissions: September
- Budget Review Division develops President's budget
- Fiscal year: October 1

### Regulatory Review (OIRA)
- Executive Order 12866 authority
- 90-day review timeline (extendable)
- M-25-36: 28-day review for deregulatory actions
- Reviews significant proposed/final regulations

### Core Responsibilities
- Federal budget assembly and analysis
- Procurement policy oversight
- Financial management coordination
- Information resources management

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### OIRA Transparency
- Business conducted in secret; exempt from FOIA
- 2003 GAO: "Regulated parties frequently get what they want after meetings"
- 2011: OIRA altered 84% of EPA rules, 65% of other agency rules
- 2025: M-25-36 sharply reduced review periods (14-28 days)

### Schedule F Implementation
- EO 13957 (October 2020): Created Schedule F for policy positions
- Repealed by Biden (January 2021)
- Reinstated by Trump (January 2025)
- OPM approved 415 OMB employees (68% of workforce) for reclassification
- 50,000-200,000 career positions potentially affected government-wide
- 94% of 40,500 public comments opposed

---

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- Treasury: OMB sets budget policy; Treasury ensures spending authority
- All Executive Agencies: Budget authority, procurement/financial policy, OIRA regulatory review
- GAO/OPM: Financial management coordination
- Council of Economic Advisers: Budget assumptions
