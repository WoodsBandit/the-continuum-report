# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: OFFICE OF THE UNITED STATES TRADE REPRESENTATIVE

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

USTR is an EOP agency leading U.S. trade policy and negotiations, established 1962. With ~$76M budget and 258 FTEs, the Cabinet-level Ambassador negotiates trade agreements, administers Section 301 tariffs, and represents the U.S. at WTO. Central to 2025 tariff escalation.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Office of the United States Trade Representative
**Founding:** 1962 (Trade Expansion Act); renamed USTR 1980
**Current USTR:** Ambassador Jamieson Greer (February 27, 2025)
**Headquarters:** Winder Building, 600 17th Street NW, Washington, DC
**Additional Offices:** Geneva, Switzerland; Brussels, Belgium

---

## 2. BUDGET

**FY2025 Total:** $76 million
- Direct USTR: $61 million
- Trade Enforcement Trust Fund: $15 million
**Staff:** 258 FTE
**FY2024 Spending:** $120.5 million (12% of EOP budget)

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**Leadership:**
- USTR (Ambassador rank, Cabinet-level)
- Three Deputy USTRs (Ambassador rank)
  - Two in Washington, DC
  - One in Geneva (also U.S. Ambassador to WTO)
- Chief Agricultural Negotiator (Ambassador rank)

**24 Offices:** Organized by function and region
- Americas, Europe/Middle East, Southeast Asia, Japan/Korea/APEC, Africa
- Services and Investment
- WTO and Multilateral Affairs

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Trade Negotiations
- Bilateral and multilateral negotiations
- Lead responsibility for WTO negotiations (since 1994)
- President's principal trade advisor

### Section 301 Authority
- Investigates unfair foreign trade practices
- Can impose retaliatory tariffs
- Ongoing investigations: China, Nicaragua, Brazil, shipping/shipbuilding

### Tariff Administration
- Section 232 (national security tariffs)
- 2025 reciprocal tariffs under IEEPA
- Tariff exclusion processes

### Trade Enforcement
- ICTIME (Interagency Center for Trade Implementation)
- 20+ member agencies
- Monitors agreement compliance

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### China Section 301 Tariffs (2018-Present)
- August 2017: Investigation initiated
- June 2018: 25% tariffs on ~$50B goods
- Total: Tariffs on ~$370B imports (7.5%-25%)
- May 2024: Four-year review; tariffs continued
- 2024: EV tariffs to 100%; steel/aluminum to 25%

### USMCA Disputes
- Automotive rules of origin disputes
- Canadian dairy tariff quota issues
- Circumvention via China-Canada-Mexico routing
- 2026 Joint Review: Greer indicated potential bilateral deals or exit

### WTO Appellate Body Blockade (2017-Present)
- U.S. blocked 88+ appointment votes
- Concerns: Precedent assertions, missed deadlines, advisory opinions
- No operational AB since November 2020
- 130+ members petitioned U.S. to unblock

### 2025 Tariff Policy ("Liberation Day")
- April 2, 2025: National emergency declared on trade deficit
- April 5: 10% minimum tariff on almost all imports
- Country-specific: 10%-41% (higher for China, Canada, Mexico)
- Supreme Court heard oral arguments November 2025

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- Commerce: ITA market access; BIS national security trade
- Treasury: Currency provisions; CFIUS chair; G-20/G-7 participation
- State: Trade diplomacy; foreign policy alignment
- ITC: AD/CVD investigations; Section 201/337; Harmonized Tariff Schedule
- TPSC: 20+ agency trade policy coordination (USTR chairs)
- ICTIME: 20+ agencies for trade enforcement
