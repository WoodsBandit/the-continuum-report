# ORIGIN STORY: BUREAU OF INDIAN AFFAIRS

## FOUNDING DATE
March 11, 1824 (created administratively)
July 9, 1832 (Congressional legislation)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Created by Secretary of War John C. Calhoun
Legislatively established July 9, 1832 (4 Stat. 564)

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### JOHN C. CALHOUN (Creator)

#### Background
- **Born:** March 18, 1782, Abbeville District, South Carolina
- **Died:** March 31, 1850
- **Career:** Congressman; Secretary of War; Vice President (under Adams and Jackson); Senator; Secretary of State

#### Religion
- **Calvinist Presbyterian background** → **Unitarian** (founding member, All Souls Unitarian Church, Washington, 1821)
- Never formally joined a church; possibly Deist
- Retained Calvinist work ethic and bleak view of human nature

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democratic-Republican → Democrat (occasional Whig flirtation)
- **Philosophy:** Early nationalist (pro-federal); later states' rights; nullification doctrine; defender of slavery

### Constitutional Views
- Later believed Constitution was "compact among sovereign states"
- Developed nullification doctrine
- Major works: *A Disquisition on Government*; *Discourse on the Constitution*

---

## FIRST SUPERINTENDENT

### THOMAS L. McKENNEY

#### Background
- **Born:** March 21, 1785, Hopewell, Maryland
- **Died:** February 19, 1859, Brooklyn (typhoid fever, in poverty)
- **Career:** Dry-goods store owner; Superintendent of Indian Trade; first BIA head (1824-1830)

#### Religion
- **Quaker** - Influenced his approach to Native Americans

#### Politics
- Founded newspaper to promote Calhoun's presidential bid
- Dismissed by Andrew Jackson in 1830

### Views on Native Americans
- Believed "the Indian was, in his intellectual and moral structure, our equal"
- This view contributed to his dismissal by Jackson

## CONTEXT: EARLY REPUBLIC

### Why BIA Was Created
- Calhoun created bureau administratively without Congressional authorization
- Needed centralized management of treaty negotiations, Indian schools, trade, expenditures
- Congress later ratified the structure

### Earlier History
- Committee on Indian Affairs established 1775
- Originally headed by Benjamin Franklin

### Transfer to Interior (1849)
- Moved from War Department to newly created Department of Interior
- Reflected shift from military to civilian approach (at least nominally)

---

## CALHOUN'S CONSTITUTIONAL EVOLUTION

### Early Career (Pre-1828)
- Strong nationalist
- Supported federal power and protective tariffs
- "War Hawk" pushing for War of 1812

### Later Career (Post-1828)
- Strict constructionist
- States' rights advocate
- Nullification: states could declare federal laws unconstitutional
- Drew from Jefferson and Madison's 1798 Kentucky and Virginia Resolutions

---

## TRAGIC HISTORY

### BIA's Dark Legacy
- Forced relocations (Trail of Tears)
- Reservation system
- Boarding schools ("Kill the Indian, save the man")
- Allotment policy (Dawes Act) - broke up tribal lands
- Termination era (1950s-60s)

### Modern Era
- Self-determination policy (1970s-present)
- Tribal sovereignty recognition
- Trust responsibilities
- Now led by Native Americans

---

## MISSION

### Trust Responsibilities
- Manage 55 million acres of trust land
- Serve 574 federally recognized tribes
- Provide services: education, social services, law enforcement

### Tribal Relations
- Government-to-government relationships
- Honor treaty obligations
- Support tribal self-governance
