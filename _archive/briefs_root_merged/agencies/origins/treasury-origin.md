# ORIGIN STORY: DEPARTMENT OF THE TREASURY

## FOUNDING DATE
September 2, 1789

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Treasury Department established by Act of Congress, with Alexander Hamilton appointed as first Secretary

---

## KEY FOUNDER: ALEXANDER HAMILTON

### Background
- **Born:** January 11, 1755 or 1757, Charlestown, Nevis (British West Indies)
- **Parents:** Rachel Faucette and James Hamilton (unmarried)
- **Early Life:** Orphaned as child; father abandoned family 1766, mother died 1768
- **Adolescence:** Raised on Danish possession of St. Croix; taken in by prosperous merchant
- **Education:** King's College (now Columbia University), New York City
- **Military:** Served as George Washington's aide-de-camp during Revolutionary War

### Religion
- Not definitively documented in search results
- Note: Further research needed on Hamilton's religious background

### Politics
- **Party:** Founded the Federalist Party (1791) - America's first political party
- **Philosophy:** Strong central government, energetic executive, national defense, diversified economy with expanded industry
- **Constitutional Views:** Proposed senators and executive serve for life; executive have absolute veto (proposals not adopted)
- **The Federalist Papers:** Authored 51 of 85 essays defending the Constitution

### Views on Founding Documents / America
- Passionately campaigned for Constitution ratification
- Argued in Federalist Papers that bill of rights was unnecessary since federal government not vested with power over those rights
- Successfully argued implied powers of Constitution authorized First Bank of the United States
- Advocated assumption of states' war debts by federal government
- Envisioned strong financial system as foundation of national power

### Key Constitutional Contributions
- Architect of Treasury Department structure
- Devised plans for funding national debt
- Secured federal credit
- Encouraged manufacturing expansion
- Organized the First Bank of the United States

---

## SUPPORTING FIGURE: ROBERT MORRIS

Robert Morris, known as the "financier of the Revolution," was originally offered the Secretary of Treasury position by George Washington but declined. Morris recommended Hamilton for the critical role.
