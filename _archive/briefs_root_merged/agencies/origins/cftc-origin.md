## CONTEXT: FUTURES MARKET EXPANSION

### Why CFTC Was Needed
- Futures trading volume reached $500 billion annually by 1973
- Previous regulation (Commodity Exchange Authority) only covered agricultural commodities
- New financial instruments required broader oversight
- Hearings began summer 1973

### Predecessor Agency
- Commodity Exchange Authority (under USDA)
- Only regulated agricultural commodities
- Insufficient for growing financial futures market

---

## MISSION

### Regulatory Scope
- Exclusive jurisdiction over futures trading in all commodities
- Later expanded to include swaps and options
- Protects market participants from fraud, manipulation, abusive practices

### Modern Functions
- Oversees derivatives markets
- Enforces Commodity Exchange Act
- Regulates futures commission merchants
- Monitors systemic risk
