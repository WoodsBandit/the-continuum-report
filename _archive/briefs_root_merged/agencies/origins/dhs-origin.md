# ORIGIN STORY: DEPARTMENT OF HOMELAND SECURITY

## FOUNDING DATE
November 25, 2002 (Homeland Security Act signed)
March 1, 2003 (DHS began operations)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Homeland Security Act of 2002

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### TOM RIDGE (First Secretary)

#### Background
- **Born:** August 26, 1945, Munhall, Pennsylvania
- **Raised:** Veterans' public housing in Erie, Pennsylvania
- **Education:** Harvard University (honors graduate); Penn State Law
- **Military:** U.S. Army during Vietnam War; awarded Bronze Star
- **Childhood:** Attended local Roman Catholic schools; served as altar boy; excelled at debate
- **Political Start:** Parents engaged in long political discussions - mother Republican, father Democrat

#### Religion
- **Roman Catholic**
- Notably pro-choice on abortion despite Catholic faith

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Career:** U.S. House of Representatives (1983-1995); 43rd Governor of Pennsylvania (1995-2001)
- **Philosophy:** Moderate Republican

#### Homeland Security Role
- October 8, 2001: Sworn in as first Director of Office of Homeland Security (White House position)
- January 24, 2003: Became first Secretary of Homeland Security (cabinet position)
- Served until February 1, 2005

---

### GEORGE W. BUSH (President)

#### Role in DHS Creation
- Created Office of Homeland Security within White House (October 2001) as immediate 9/11 response
- June 6, 2002: Proposed creation of cabinet-level Department of Homeland Security
- Outlined four essential missions corresponding to four proposed divisions
- November 25, 2002: Signed Homeland Security Act into law

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Philosophy:** Compassionate conservatism; expanded executive power post-9/11

---

### JOSEPH LIEBERMAN (Congressional Champion)

#### Background
- **Born:** February 24, 1942
- **Died:** March 27, 2024
- **Position:** U.S. Senator from Connecticut (1989-2013)
- **Notable:** Democratic nominee for Vice President (2000)

#### Religion
- **Orthodox Jewish** - First Jewish nominee on major party presidential ticket

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democrat (later Independent Democrat after 2006 primary loss)
- **Philosophy:** Centrist Democrat; strong on national security

#### Role in DHS Creation
- As Chairman of Senate Governmental Affairs Committee, proposed forming DHS
- Co-authored legislation with Senator Arlen Specter (October 2001)
- Guided legislation through Senate
- Lieberman and Fred Thompson and Susan Collins forged "bipartisan compromise"
- Called creation of DHS an "honor" to work on

---

### PRIOR WARNINGS: HART-RUDMAN COMMISSION

#### Background
- 1999-2001: Senators Gary Hart and Warren Rudman chaired U.S. Commission on National Security in the 21st Century
- Commission warned of new terrorist threat
- Pressed for creation of federal department focused on homeland defense
- Recommendations preceded and anticipated 9/11 response

---

## POLITICAL CONTEXT

### September 11, 2001
- Al-Qaeda terrorist attacks killed nearly 3,000 people
- Exposed vulnerabilities in domestic security and intelligence coordination
- Created political imperative for homeland security reorganization

### Largest Reorganization Since 1947
- "Setting up the Department of Homeland Security involved the most extensive reorganization of the federal government since Harry Truman signed the National Security Act"
- Combined 22 agencies from across government
- 170,000+ employees initially

### Agencies Merged
- FEMA
- U.S. Customs Service
- U.S. Border Patrol
- U.S. Coast Guard
- Immigration and Naturalization Service
- Secret Service
- Transportation Security Administration (new)
- And many others

### Bipartisan Passage
- Senate passed bill November 19, 2002
- House concurred
- Signed November 25, 2002
- "Efforts to develop and pass the legislation were sometimes contentious, they were ultimately able to forge a grand bipartisan compromise"
