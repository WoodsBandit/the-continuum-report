# ORIGIN STORY: DEPARTMENT OF AGRICULTURE

## FOUNDING DATE
May 15, 1862 (established as department)
1889 (elevated to Cabinet level)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Act of Congress establishing "at the seat of Government of the United States a Department of Agriculture"

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### ABRAHAM LINCOLN (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** February 12, 1809, Kentucky
- **Early Life:** Born on a poor farm; work as farmhand gave him understanding of cultivating land
- **Philosophy:** "Once he left his farming life to pursue politics, Lincoln expressed his passion for developing better education and government recognition of agriculture and engineering"

#### Religion
- No formal church membership, though expressed Christian beliefs

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican (co-founder)
- **Philosophy:** Union preservation; government support for internal improvements

#### Views on Agriculture
- December 3, 1861 (first annual message to Congress): "Agriculture, confessedly the largest interest of the nation, has not a department nor a bureau, but a clerkship only, assigned to it in the Government"
- Called USDA "the People's Department"

---

### ISAAC NEWTON (First Commissioner of Agriculture)

#### Background
- **Born:** March 31, 1800, Burlington County, New Jersey
- **Claim to Fame:** Claimed to be direct descendant of Sir Isaac Newton
- **Career:** Established "impeccable farm" in Delaware County, Pennsylvania near Philadelphia
- **White House Connection:** Supplied White House with dairy products for several years; sent butter weekly

#### Politics
- **Philosophy:** Skilled agriculturalist, not politician
- Lincoln preferred practical farmer over political appointee
- "A master relationship builder"

#### Appointment
- July 1, 1862: Appointed by Lincoln as first Commissioner
- Previously served as chief of agricultural section of the Patent Office
- Congressional opposition: "Many in Congress felt the Commissioner position required political experience"

---

### NORMAN J. COLMAN (First Secretary of Agriculture)

#### Background
- Became first Secretary when department elevated to Cabinet level in 1889
- Appointed by President Grover Cleveland

---

## CONTEXT: THE 1862 AGRICULTURAL REVOLUTION

Lincoln signed multiple transformative agricultural laws in 1862:
- **Homestead Act:** Critical to westward expansion
- **Pacific Railway Act:** Faster transportation for farmers and products
- **Morrill Act:** Created land-grant universities for agriculture and engineering
- **USDA Establishment:** Federal department for agriculture

All four proposals enacted during Civil War when Southern opposition absent from Congress.
