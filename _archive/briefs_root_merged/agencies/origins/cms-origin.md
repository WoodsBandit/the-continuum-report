# ORIGIN STORY: CENTERS FOR MEDICARE AND MEDICAID SERVICES

## FOUNDING DATE
July 30, 1965 (Medicare/Medicaid created)
March 8, 1977 (HCFA created)
July 1, 2001 (Renamed CMS)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Social Security Amendments of 1965 (P.L. 89-97)

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### WILBUR J. COHEN ("The Man Who Built Medicare")

#### Background
- **Born:** June 10, 1913, Milwaukee, Wisconsin
- **Died:** May 17, 1987, Seoul, South Korea
- **Career:** First professional employee of Social Security Board (1935); HEW Secretary (1968-69); proposed Medicare in 1935

#### Religion
- **Jewish** - Son of immigrant parents Aaron and Bessie Rubenstein Cohen

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Philosophy:** New Deal/Great Society liberal; member of Americans for Democratic Action

### ROBERT M. BALL (Medicare's Chief Architect)

#### Background
- **Born:** 1914, New York
- **Died:** January 29, 2008
- **Career:** SSA field representative (1939); SSA Commissioner under Kennedy, Johnson, Nixon (1962-1973)

#### Religion
- **Methodist background** - Son of a Methodist minister

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Philosophy:** "Nation's most influential Democrat" on Social Security; opposed privatization

### LYNDON B. JOHNSON (Signing President)

#### Background
- **Born:** August 27, 1908
- **Died:** January 22, 1973

#### Religion
- **Disciples of Christ**

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Philosophy:** Great Society; fulfilled Truman's dream of national health insurance

## CONTEXT: 20-YEAR STRUGGLE

### Truman's Proposal (1945)
- First president to propose national health insurance
- AMA labeled it "socialized medicine"
- Defeated repeatedly in Congress

### Medicare Breakthrough
- John F. Kennedy made it campaign priority
- LBJ pushed it through after landslide 1964 victory
- Signed in Independence, Missouri with Truman present
- Truman received first Medicare card

---

## EVOLUTION

### Bureau of Health Insurance (1965-1977)
- Within Social Security Administration
- Administered Medicare Part A and B

### Health Care Financing Administration (1977-2001)
- Combined Medicare and Medicaid under one agency
- Created by HEW Secretary Joseph Califano

### Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (2001-present)
- Renamed by Secretary Tommy Thompson
- Now also administers ACA marketplace

---

## MISSION

### Programs Administered
- Medicare (65+ and disabled)
- Medicaid (low-income)
- Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP)
- Health Insurance Marketplace

### Scale
- Covers 145+ million Americans
- Annual budget exceeds $1 trillion
