# ORIGIN STORY: TENNESSEE VALLEY AUTHORITY

## FOUNDING DATE
May 18, 1933 (TVA Act signed)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Tennessee Valley Authority Act of 1933

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### SENATOR GEORGE W. NORRIS ("Father of the TVA")

#### Background
- **Position:** U.S. Senator from Nebraska
- **Title:** "Often called the 'father of the TVA'"

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican (nominal) - but often voted with Democrats
- **Philosophy:** Progressive Republican; distrusted private utility companies
- **Notable:** Supported FDR despite being Republican; TVA had no impact on his state of Nebraska

#### Prior Efforts
- 1920: Blocked proposal from industrialist Henry Ford to build private dam in Tennessee Valley
- 1930: Sponsored Muscle Shoals Bill for federal dam - vetoed by President Herbert Hoover who believed it to be "socialistic"

#### Victory
- TVA Act passage was "the victory of a lifetime" for Norris

---

### FRANKLIN D. ROOSEVELT (President)

#### Vision
- Brought idea of "public authority" from New York where he had created such organizations
- TVA Act called for governing board of three directors
- Designed as government corporation not connected to other federal departments, answerable only to President

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democratic
- **Philosophy:** New Deal - believed government could solve social and economic problems

---

### ARTHUR E. MORGAN (First Chairman)

#### Background
- **Position:** Former President of Antioch College
- **Profession:** Educator and engineer
- **Selection:** With help from Norris and other advisers, Roosevelt selected Morgan in May 1933

#### Religion
- **Religious:** Deeply believed in the Social Gospel
- **Values:** "Anticapitalist, communitarian values"
- **Criticism:** "Often accused of holding paternalistic and authoritarian positions"

#### Politics
- **Philosophy:** "Advocate of social planning"
- **Vision:** Wanted cooperative relationship between government and business
- **Goals:** Believed higher purpose of TVA was to eliminate poverty in Tennessee Valley and serve as model for national regional planning
- **Worldview:** "Ruralist" who believed farms and small-towns offered best prospect for human fulfillment; saw TVA as means of reviving declining small-town, rural culture

#### Professional Philosophy
- "Exemplified the engineering spirit of the Progressive Era"
- "Hostile to all politicians - they introduced inefficiency and corruption into his engineering projects"
- Wanted to keep rates at comparative level to avoid alienating private industry

#### Conflict and Departure
- Battle between three administrators went on from 1933 until March 1938
- Arthur Morgan was fired by FDR

---

### HARCOURT A. MORGAN (Board Member)

#### Background
- No relation to Arthur Ernest Morgan
- Former President of University of Tennessee
- Agricultural expert

---

### DAVID ELI LILIENTHAL (Board Member)

#### Background
- Lawyer and electric-power specialist
- Later became influential figure in atomic energy policy

---

## BOARD DYNAMICS

- The three directors "had very different ideas about the direction TVA could and should take"
- Arthur Morgan's vision conflicted with more pragmatic approaches of other board members
- Conflict led to his eventual firing in 1938

---

## POLITICAL CONTEXT

### Why Tennessee Valley?
- Region among poorest in nation
- Lacking electricity, modern infrastructure
- Chronic flooding
- Deforestation and soil erosion

### Controversy
- Private utility companies opposed TVA as unfair government competition
- Constitutional challenges reached Supreme Court - TVA was validated
- Became model for regional development worldwide

### New Deal Framework
- TVA represented comprehensive, planned approach to regional development
- Combined electricity generation, flood control, navigation, agricultural improvement
- Government as positive force for economic development
