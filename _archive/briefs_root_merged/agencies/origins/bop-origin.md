# ORIGIN STORY: FEDERAL BUREAU OF PRISONS

## FOUNDING DATE
May 14, 1930

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Act of May 14, 1930 (Pub. L. 71-218)

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### SANFORD BATES (First Director)

#### Background
- **Born:** 1884, Boston, Massachusetts
- **Died:** 1972
- **Career:** Massachusetts legislator; Boston Commissioner of Penal Institutions; Massachusetts Corrections Commissioner; first BOP Director (1930-1937)

#### Religion
- Not documented

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Philosophy:** Progressive prison reformer; believed prisons should "cure" offenders like hospitals

### MABEL WALKER WILLEBRANDT (Champion)

#### Background
- **Born:** May 23, 1889, Woodsdale, Kansas
- **Died:** April 6, 1963
- **Career:** First female public defender in Los Angeles; Assistant Attorney General (1921-1929) - highest-ranking woman in federal government; first female ABA committee chair

#### Religion
- Expelled from Park College for religious views; campaigned to Protestant churches against Catholic Al Smith (1928); later converted to Catholicism

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican (Progressive wing)
- **Philosophy:** Enforced Prohibition despite personally opposing it - "took her task of defending the Constitution seriously"

### JAMES V. BENNETT (Investigator; Later Director)

#### Background
- **Born:** 1894, Silver Creek, New York
- **Died:** 1978
- **Career:** Bureau of Efficiency investigator; authored 1928 report documenting prison problems; BOP Director (1937-1964, 27 years)

#### Religion
- Not documented

#### Politics
- Progressive reformer; opposed capital punishment; pushed federal agencies toward racial integration

## CONTEXT: PRISON REFORM ERA

### Before 1930
- 11 federal prisons operated without central coordination
- "Inhumane and poorly operated" system
- No unified standards or rehabilitation programs

### Bennett's 1928 Report
- "The Federal Penal and Correctional Problem"
- Documented system failures
- Led directly to Bureau creation

### Progressive Philosophy
- All three founders shared Progressive Era beliefs
- Rehabilitation over punishment
- Professionalization of prison administration

---

## MISSION

### Federal Corrections
- Manages federal prisons and detention facilities
- Operates 122 institutions
- Houses approximately 150,000 inmates

### Programs
- Rehabilitation and reentry programs
- Education and vocational training
- Medical and mental health services
