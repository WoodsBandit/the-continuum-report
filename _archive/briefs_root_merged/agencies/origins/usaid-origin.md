# ORIGIN STORY: U.S. AGENCY FOR INTERNATIONAL DEVELOPMENT

## FOUNDING DATE
November 3, 1961

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Foreign Assistance Act of 1961 (P.L. 87-195)
Executive Order 10973

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### JOHN F. KENNEDY (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** May 29, 1917, Brookline, Massachusetts
- **Died:** November 22, 1963
- **Career:** WWII Navy hero; Congressman; Senator; 35th President

#### Religion
- **Roman Catholic** (first Catholic President)

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Philosophy:** Cold War liberal; "New Frontier"; believed economic development could counter communism

### WALT WHITMAN ROSTOW (Intellectual Architect)

#### Background
- **Born:** October 7, 1916, New York City
- **Died:** February 13, 2003
- **Career:** Rhodes Scholar; MIT professor; later LBJ's National Security Advisor

#### Religion
- **Jewish** (secular) - Son of Russian Jewish socialist immigrants

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Philosophy:** Anti-communist; pro-capitalism; believed development would prevent communist expansion

### Famous Work
- "The Stages of Economic Growth: A Non-Communist Manifesto" (1960)

## CONTEXT: "DECADE OF DEVELOPMENT"

### Kennedy's Vision
- Called it "the most important international piece of legislation...since the passage of the Marshall Plan"
- Consolidate fragmented foreign aid programs
- Use economic development to compete with Soviet Union
- Create soft power to advance American interests

### Cold War Framework
- Rostow theorized nations could progress through developmental stages toward capitalism
- Proper aid could "immunize" developing nations against communism
- Development seen as alternative to revolution

## PREDECESSOR AGENCIES MERGED

## MISSION

### International Development
- Leads U.S. international development and humanitarian efforts
- Works in over 100 countries
- Focus areas: health, agriculture, democracy, economic growth

### Budget
- Approximately $50+ billion annually (varies by year)
