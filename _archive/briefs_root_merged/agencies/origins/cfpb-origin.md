# ORIGIN STORY: CONSUMER FINANCIAL PROTECTION BUREAU

## FOUNDING DATE
July 21, 2010 (Dodd-Frank Act signed)
July 21, 2011 (CFPB officially launched)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### ELIZABETH WARREN (Intellectual Architect)

#### Background
- **Born:** June 22, 1949, Oklahoma City, Oklahoma
- **Education:** University of Houston (BS); Rutgers Law School (JD)
- **Career:** Law professor (Rutgers, Houston, Texas, Penn, Harvard); bankruptcy expert
- **Later:** U.S. Senator from Massachusetts (2013-present)

#### Religion
- **Methodist** - raised; identifies as Christian
- Later attended Episcopal and Unitarian churches

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican until 1990s; Democrat since
- **Philosophy:** Progressive; consumer protection champion; anti-Wall Street

### Warren's Vision
- 2007: Proposed consumer financial protection agency in *Democracy: A Journal of Ideas*
- Concept: Agency specifically focused on protecting consumers from predatory financial practices
- Similar to how CPSC protects product safety
- Called for "Consumer Financial Product Safety Commission"

---

## THE 2008 FINANCIAL CRISIS

### What Went Wrong
- Subprime mortgage crisis
- Predatory lending widespread
- Complex financial products consumers didn't understand
- Regulatory gaps allowed exploitation
- Consumer protection scattered across multiple agencies

### Response
- 2008: Financial system near collapse
- TARP bailouts; bank failures
- Public outrage at Wall Street
- Demand for reform

---

## CREATION OF CFPB

### Dodd-Frank Act
- Named for Senator Christopher Dodd (D-CT) and Representative Barney Frank (D-MA)
- Comprehensive financial reform legislation
- Title X created CFPB
- July 21, 2010: President Obama signed into law

### Obama's Role
- Embraced Warren's concept
- Made CFPB centerpiece of financial reform
- Appointed Warren as Special Advisor to set up agency
- Could not nominate her as Director (Senate opposition)

### Warren as Organizer
- September 2010: Appointed Special Advisor
- Built agency from ground up
- Recruited staff
- Established structure
- Did not become first Director

## FIRST DIRECTOR

### RICHARD CORDRAY

#### Background
- **Born:** May 3, 1959
- **Education:** Michigan State (BA); Oxford (Rhodes Scholar); University of Chicago Law
- **Career:** Ohio Attorney General; Ohio Treasurer
- Jeopardy! champion (1987)

#### Religion
- Not documented in sources

#### Politics
- Democrat

### Appointment Controversy
- Obama nominated Cordray
- Senate Republicans blocked confirmation
- January 4, 2012: Obama made recess appointment
- July 16, 2013: Finally confirmed by Senate

## CONSOLIDATED AUTHORITY

### Consumer Protection Powers From
- Federal Reserve
- Office of the Comptroller of the Currency
- Office of Thrift Supervision
- FDIC
- FTC (some functions)
- NCUA
- HUD

### Jurisdiction
- Mortgages
- Credit cards
- Student loans
- Payday loans
- Debt collection
- Credit reporting

---

## CONTROVERSY

### Political Opposition
- Republicans sought to weaken/abolish CFPB
- Structure challenged (single director removable only "for cause")
- Supreme Court in *Seila Law* (2020): Director can be removed at will by President
- Agency continues but under political pressure
