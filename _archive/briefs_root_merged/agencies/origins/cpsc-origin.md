# ORIGIN STORY: CONSUMER PRODUCT SAFETY COMMISSION

## FOUNDING DATE
October 27, 1972 (Consumer Product Safety Act signed)
May 1973 (CPSC became operational)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Consumer Product Safety Act of 1972

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### RICHARD M. NIXON (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** January 9, 1913, Yorba Linda, California
- **Died:** April 22, 1994
- **Career:** Navy officer; Congressman; Senator; Vice President; President

#### Religion
- **Quaker** - raised in devout Quaker household

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Philosophy:** Pragmatic; surprisingly progressive on consumer/environmental issues

### Nixon's Consumer Protection Legacy
- 1970: Created EPA
- 1970: Created OSHA
- 1972: Created CPSC
- Responded to consumer movement and public demand

---

## CONTEXT: CONSUMER MOVEMENT

### Ralph Nader's Influence
- "Unsafe at Any Speed" (1965) galvanized consumer movement
- Created public demand for corporate responsibility
- Pushed for independent regulatory agencies
- Consumer advocacy became political force

### The Problem
- Millions of injuries annually from consumer products
- Thousands of deaths linked to hazardous products
- Fragmented and inconsistent safety regulations
- No unified federal approach to product safety

---

## CREATION OF CPSC

### Consumer Product Safety Act (1972)
- October 27, 1972: Nixon signed into law
- Established CPSC as independent federal regulatory agency
- Bipartisan commission of five commissioners
- May 1973: Agency became operational

### Bipartisan Support
- Conference Committee: 9 Democrats, 7 Republicans
- Broad consensus on need for product safety
- Independence from industry influence prioritized

### Authority
- Develop safety standards
- Pursue product recalls
- Ban hazardous products
- Tackle consumer product hazards

## EARLY YEARS

### Resources at Founding
- 1972 budget: $34.7 million
- Staff: 786 members
- Significant investment in new agency

### Peak Under Carter
- Staff reached 978 people (1977-1981)
- Aggressive enforcement
- Consumer protection priority

## JURISDICTION

### Products Covered
- Thousands of consumer products
- Household items
- Children's products
- Recreational equipment
- Electronics
- Furniture

### Products NOT Covered
- Automobiles (NHTSA)
- Food, drugs, cosmetics (FDA)
- Firearms, ammunition
- Tobacco
- Pesticides (EPA)
