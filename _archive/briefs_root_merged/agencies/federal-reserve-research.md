# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: FEDERAL RESERVE SYSTEM

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Federal Reserve System is the central bank of the United States, established December 23, 1913. Self-funded through securities portfolio income (not congressional appropriations), it manages ~$7.4 trillion in assets. Governed by 7-member Board of Governors and 12 regional banks, with FOMC setting monetary policy.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System
**Founding:** December 23, 1913 (Federal Reserve Act)
**Current Chair:** Jerome H. Powell (2018-present, second term through 2026)
**Headquarters:** Marriner S. Eccles Building, 20th & Constitution Ave NW, Washington DC 20551

---

## 2. BUDGET/FUNDING

**Funding Model:** Self-financed, not appropriated by Congress
- 90%+ revenue from interest on Treasury securities
- Total Assets: ~$7.4 trillion (May 2024)
- Operating Expenses: ~$6.98 billion
- Remits surplus to Treasury as general revenue
- Operating at losses since September 2022 due to rate increases

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**Board of Governors:** 7 members, 14-year staggered terms
**12 Regional Banks:** Boston, New York, Philadelphia, Cleveland, Richmond, Atlanta, Chicago, St. Louis, Minneapolis, Kansas City, Dallas, San Francisco
**FOMC:** 12 voting members (7 governors + NY Fed president + 4 rotating)

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

- Monetary policy (federal funds rate, open market operations)
- Bank supervision (~3,800 bank holding companies, 700 state banks)
- Payment systems (Fedwire, ACH, FedNow)
- Consumer protection and community development

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### 2008 Financial Crisis and QE
- QE1-3 expanded balance sheet from $900B to $4.5T
- Criticized as bank bailouts vs. legitimate monetary policy
- Boeing settlement: $2.5B (2021)

### 2021-2024 Inflation Response
- Delayed rate increases; characterized inflation as "transitory"
- Fed funds rate reached 5.25-5.5% (July 2023)
- FAIT framework criticized

### Political Pressure (2025)
- Trump demanded immediate rate cuts
- Executive orders asserting presidential control
- Vice Chair for Supervision Barr resigned

---

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- Treasury: Coordination, surplus remittance
- OCC: Bank supervision coordination
- FDIC: Joint guidance, bank oversight
- SEC: Limited coordination
