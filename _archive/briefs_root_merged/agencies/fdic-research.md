# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: FEDERAL DEPOSIT INSURANCE CORPORATION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The FDIC is an independent agency providing deposit insurance and bank supervision, established June 16, 1933. Self-funded through bank assessments with $129B+ DIF, it insures deposits up to $250,000 per depositor. Faced major crisis in 2023 with SVB, Signature, First Republic failures (~$38B DIF losses).

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation
**Founding:** June 16, 1933 (Banking Act of 1933)
**Headquarters:** Washington, DC
**Jurisdiction:** 4,517 insured institutions (June 2024)

---

## 2. BUDGET/FUNDING

**Funding Model:** No Congressional appropriations; self-funded through risk-based assessments
**Deposit Insurance Fund (Q3 2024):** $129.2 billion (1.21% reserve ratio)
**Statutory Minimum:** 1.35% of insured deposits
**Board Target:** 2.0% designated reserve ratio
**Emergency Credit:** $100 billion Treasury line (never used)
**Special Assessment (2023):** $20.4 billion to recover uninsured depositor losses

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**5-Member Board:** Presidential appointees, max 3 same party
- Ex officio: Comptroller of Currency, CFPB Director

**Major Divisions:**
- Risk Management Supervision
- Depositor and Consumer Protection
- Office of Complex Financial Institutions
- Legal Division
- Office of Inspector General

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Deposit Insurance
- Standard coverage: $250,000 per depositor per bank per ownership category
- Trust accounts: Up to $1,250,000 (5+ beneficiaries)
- Multiple categories: Individual, joint, trust, IRA, POD/ITF
- "No depositor has ever lost a penny of FDIC-insured funds"

### Bank Supervision
- Safety and soundness examinations
- Consumer protection functions
- Community investment oversight

### Failed Bank Resolution
- Receivership management
- Asset liquidation
- Depositor protection

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### 2023 Banking Crisis
**Silicon Valley Bank (March 10, 2023):**
- Third-largest U.S. failure
- DIF cost: $20 billion ($18B for uninsured depositors)
- Acquired by First Citizens BancShares

**Signature Bank (March 12, 2023):**
- 90%+ uninsured deposits
- Crypto industry overreliance
- DIF cost: $2.4 billion

**First Republic Bank (May 1, 2023):**
- Second-largest U.S. failure
- $213B assets at year-end 2022
- Sold to JPMorgan for $10.6B
- DIF cost: $15.6 billion

**Total DIF Impact:** ~$38 billion

### Systemic Risk Exception
- Controversial use to protect uninsured deposits
- Sparked deposit insurance reform debate

### Workplace Culture Scandal (2024)
- Cleary Gottlieb 234-page report based on 500+ employees
- Findings: "Toxic, misogynistic, patriarchal, insular"
- Sexual harassment and discrimination ignored
- Pattern of moving wrongdoers vs disciplining
- Created Office of Professional Conduct in response

### Crypto Policy Shift
- Pre-2025: Required notification, requests met with "resistance"
- March 2025: Reversed - banks no longer need prior approval
- Permissible: Custodial services, stablecoin reserves, digital asset issuance

---

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- OCC: Co-regulator for national banks
- Federal Reserve: Joint supervision, FFIEC/FSOC member
- NCUA: Credit union coordination
- State Regulators: Primary federal regulator for non-Fed state banks
- FFIEC: Harmonizes examination standards
- FSOC: Detects systemic financial stress
