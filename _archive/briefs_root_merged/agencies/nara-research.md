# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: NATIONAL ARCHIVES AND RECORDS ADMINISTRATION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

NARA is the nation's official record keeper, established June 19, 1934. With ~$481M budget and storage of 13+ billion archival pages, it manages presidential libraries and federal records. Faces Trump classified documents case aftermath, 2025 Archivist retaliation firings, declassification backlogs, and 30-year budget flatline.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** National Archives and Records Administration (NARA)
**Founding:** June 19, 1934 (P.L. 73-432); became independent agency April 1, 1985
**Current Archivist:** Marco Rubio (acting)
**Recent Changes:** Archivist Colleen Shogan fired February 7, 2025; Deputy William Bosanko resigned; IG purged
**Headquarters:** Washington, D.C. (National Archives Building); College Park, Maryland (administrative hub)

---

## 2. BUDGET

**FY2025 Request:** $481.1 million
- Operating Expenses: $426.3 million
- +$6M for pay raises (2.0% increase)
- $9.2M decrease from FY2024 continuing resolution

**FY2024 Enacted:** ~$490.3 million (at FY2023 level)
**Critical Issue:** Budget "flatlined" 30 years while records accumulation grew exponentially; 13B+ pages with 4.2B more expected within 15 years

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**Leadership:**
- Archivist of the United States (chief political appointee)
- Deputy Archivist
- General Counsel, Office of Inspector General, Chief Operating Officer

**Four Main Archival Offices:**
1. Federal Records Centers Program
2. Research Services
3. Office of Presidential Libraries
4. Agency Services (records management oversight)

**Supporting Offices:**
- External Affairs and Communications
- Office of the Federal Register
- National Personnel Records Center (St. Louis)
- Office of Human Capital, CIO, Business Support Services

**Presidential Libraries System:** 15 libraries/museums (presidents since Hoover)

**Facilities:**
- 33 facilities nationwide
- National Archives Building (Washington, DC)
- National Archives at College Park (Maryland)
- 16 Regional Records Services Centers

**Grant-Making:** National Historical Publications and Records Commission (NHPRC) - ~4,500 grants since 1964

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Federal Records Management
- Establishes standards for retention and disposition
- Appraises federal records and approves disposition schedules
- Inspects agency records and management practices
- Chief Records Officer oversees electronic records government-wide

### Presidential Libraries (15 locations)
- Preserves and provides public access to presidential documents
- Center for Legislative Archives for Congressional records

### National Declassification Center (NDC)
- Established by Executive Order 13526
- Processes agency referrals for declassification
- Began operations January 2010

### Electronic Records Archives (ERA)
- Authenticates and preserves electronic records
- OMB Memorandum M-19-21: Digital records management by December 31, 2022

### Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) Program
- Information Security Oversight Office (ISOO) serves as Federal Executive Agent
- Oversees classified national security information programs

### Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) Services
- Office of Government Information Services (OGIS) as FOIA resource
- Reviews agency FOIA policies and compliance

### Federal Register Publishing
- Publishes Federal Register, Code of Federal Regulations
- Administers Electoral College processes

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Trump Classified Documents Case
**Timeline:**
- May 2021: NARA became aware of missing Trump documents
- January 2022: Retrieved 15 boxes from Mar-a-Lago containing classified materials
  - 184 documents with classification markings: 67 Confidential, 92 Secret, 25 Top Secret
- August 2022: FBI search recovered additional Top Secret materials
- June 2023: Trump indicted on 37 counts (special counsel Jack Smith)

**Key Evidence:**
- Audio recording (July 2021 Bedminster): Trump acknowledged "now I can't" declassify
- Mark Meadows told special counsel no recall of Trump ordering declassification
- Documents torn up; officials recovered and taped together

### Records Destruction
- Trump officials used private communications for government business
- DOD and Army wiped phones of top officials (including January 6 texts)
- Secret Service deleted text messages from January 6, 2021

### Archivist Retaliation (2025)
- February 7, 2025: Trump fired Archivist Colleen Shogan (no cause stated)
- Deputy Archivist William Bosanko (30+ years tenure) stepped down
- NARA's Inspector General purged
- Described as "primary target of Trump's retribution tour"

### Declassification Backlogs
- NDC faces systemic processing delays
- Inadequate compliance and processing capacity

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- **All Federal Agencies:** NARA is mandatory central repository; agencies must comply with records standards
- **DOJ:** Role in Trump classified documents case; legal custody protocols
- **ISOO:** Housed within NARA with independent authority; reports to President annually
- **NSC:** Provides policy guidance to ISOO
- **OMB:** Issues records management memoranda; coordinates digital records modernization
- **GSA, GAO, OPM:** Interagency coordination on records, facilities, personnel
