# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: NUCLEAR REGULATORY COMMISSION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The NRC is an independent agency regulating civilian nuclear power, materials, and waste, established October 11, 1974 (succeeding AEC). With ~3,000 employees and ~$1B budget (90% fee-recovered), it licenses and oversees 100+ operating reactors.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission
**Founding:** October 11, 1974 (Energy Reorganization Act)
**Current Chair:** David A. Wright (January 2025)
**Headquarters:** Suburban Maryland, 4 regional offices
**Staff:** ~3,000

---

## 2. BUDGET

**FY2024:** $1.006 billion gross; $156M net appropriation
**FY2025:** $995M gross; $151M net
**Fee Recovery:** ~90% from licensee fees
**Operating Reactor Fee (FY2025):** $5.36M per reactor

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**5 Commissioners:** Max 3 same party, 5-year terms

**Major Offices:**
- Office of Nuclear Reactor Regulation
- Office of New Reactors
- Office of Nuclear Material Safety and Safeguards
- Office of Nuclear Security and Incident Response
- 4 Regional Offices

**Advisory Bodies:**
- Advisory Committee on Reactor Safeguards
- Atomic Safety and Licensing Board Panel

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Reactor Oversight Process (ROP)
- Risk-informed, tiered approach
- 7 safety cornerstones
- Resident inspectors at each site
- Color-coded findings (Green/White/Yellow/Red)

### Nuclear Waste Management
- Spent fuel storage oversight
- Transportation regulation
- High-level waste licensing
- No permanent repository operational

### Advanced Nuclear Support
- HALEU licensing (5-20% enrichment)
- SMR design certification (NuScale)
- Reduced fees for advanced reactors

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Three Mile Island (1979)
- Partial meltdown, Unit 2
- Led to sweeping regulatory changes

### Fukushima Response (2011)
- Safety review of 104 U.S. reactors
- Cost concerns: 50% power cost increase projected
- Licensing suspension petitions

### Aging Reactor Fleet
- 60+ reactors granted extensions beyond 60 years
- Component degradation concerns

### NuScale SMR Cancellation (November 2023)
- Idaho project cancelled due to cost escalation
- Commercial viability concerns

### Regulatory Capture Allegations
- Obama (2007): "captive of industries"
- Reuters (2011): "regulator and salesman"
- 2025: DOE/DOGE staff allegedly demanded "rubber-stamp" licenses
- Executive Order 14300 raised safety concerns
