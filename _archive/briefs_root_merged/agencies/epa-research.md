# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION AGENCY

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) is the primary federal agency responsible for environmental protection and human health. Operating across 10 regional offices with a FY2025 appropriation of approximately $21.14 billion, EPA enforces major environmental statutes while balancing regulatory authority with state partnerships.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** United States Environmental Protection Agency

**Establishment:** December 2, 1970

**Current Administrator:** Lee Zeldin (confirmed January 29, 2025)

**Headquarters:** Washington, D.C.

**Regional Structure:** 10 regional offices

---

## 2. BUDGET & APPROPRIATIONS

**FY2025:** $21.14 billion (including IIJA)
**FY2026 Request:** $4.16 billion (substantially reduced)

---

## 3. KEY PROGRAMS

- Clean Air Act enforcement
- Clean Water Act/Safe Drinking Water Act
- Superfund remediation (CERCLA)
- Pesticide regulation (FIFRA)
- Toxic substances (TSCA)
- Climate programs (subject to 2025 rollbacks)

---

## 4. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Flint Water Crisis (2014-Present)
- EPA received 87 complaints, took minimal action
- Whistleblower suppression (Del Toral)
- Emergency order lifted May 19, 2025

### 2025 Regulatory Rollbacks
- 31 rules under reconsideration
- GHG Endangerment Finding repeal proposed
- Power plant standards, MATS proposed repeal
- GGRF terminated ($20 billion)

---

## 5. INTERAGENCY RELATIONSHIPS

- Interior: Land management coordination
- Agriculture: Pesticide regulation (joint)
- HHS/FDA: Chemical safety
- State agencies: ~50% budget to grants
