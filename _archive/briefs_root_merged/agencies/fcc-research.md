# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: FEDERAL COMMUNICATIONS COMMISSION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The FCC regulates interstate and international communications, established February 26, 1934. Self-funded through regulatory fees (~$390M FY2024), it manages spectrum, broadcast licensing, and telecommunications policy with ~1,600 employees.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Federal Communications Commission
**Founding:** February 26, 1934 (Communications Act)
**Current Chair:** Brendan Carr (January 2025)
**Previous Chair:** Jessica Rosenworcel (2021-2025)
**Headquarters:** 45 L Street NE, Washington, DC 20554

---

## 2. BUDGET

**FY2024:** $390.2 million (regulatory fees)
**FY2025 Request:** $448 million
**Funding:** 100% regulatory fees (not appropriations)
**Fee Rate:** 34.4% of telecom end-user revenues

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**5 Commissioners:** Max 3 same party

**7 Bureaus:**
1. Media Bureau
2. Wireline Competition Bureau
3. Wireless Telecommunications Bureau
4. Public Safety and Homeland Security Bureau
5. Consumer and Governmental Affairs Bureau
6. Enforcement Bureau
7. Space Bureau (est. April 2023)

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

- Spectrum management and licensing
- Broadcast licensing
- Net neutrality regulation
- Universal Service Fund ($7.4B disbursed 2022)
- Rural Digital Opportunity Fund ($20.4B)

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Net Neutrality Battles
- Adopted 2015 → Repealed 2017 → Reinstated April 2024
- January 2025: Sixth Circuit ruled FCC lacks authority
- Death threats against Chair Pai

### Lifeline Program Fraud
- Waste, fraud, abuse concerns
- Recipient verification issues

### Broadband Mapping Failures
- Poor data granularity
- Overstated availability
- $42B+ allocated based on inaccurate maps

### 5G/China Concerns
- Huawei/ZTE equipment removal
- Security measures ongoing

### Spectrum Auction Authority
- Expired March 9, 2023
- Awaiting Congressional renewal

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- USDA/Treasury: Broadband funding coordination
