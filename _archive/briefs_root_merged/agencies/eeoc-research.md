# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY COMMISSION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The EEOC is an independent agency enforcing federal employment discrimination laws, established July 2, 1965. With ~$455M budget and 53 field offices, it investigates discrimination charges and litigates systemic cases. Faces significant 2025 policy reversals on LGBTQ+ and DEI enforcement.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission
**Founding:** July 2, 1965 (Civil Rights Act of 1964)
**Current Chair:** Andrea R. Lucas (Acting Chair January 2025; confirmed July 2025)
**Headquarters:** 131 M Street NE, Washington, DC 20507
**Field Offices:** 53 nationwide

---

## 2. BUDGET

**FY2024:** $455 million
**FY2025:** 2% increase from FY2024
**FY2026 Request:** $435.4 million (decrease of $19.6M)
**Staff:** 2,246 FTE (2024); declined from 3,390 FTE (1980)

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**5 Commissioners:** Presidential appointees, 5-year staggered terms, max 3 same party

**General Counsel:** Andrew Rogers (Acting, February 2025)

**Divisions:**
- Office of Field Programs (53 field offices)
- Office of General Counsel
- Office of Federal Operations

**Specialized Offices:** Fair Lending, Civil Rights, OMWI, Ombudsman

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Discrimination Complaint Processing
- FY2024: 88,531 new charges (9.2% increase)
- Protected classes: Race, color, national origin, religion, sex, age, disability, genetic information
- Merit factor resolution rate: 15.6%

### Systemic Investigations
- 250% increase since 2006
- FY2023: 370 resolved; $29M in benefits; 100% success rate

### Litigation Program
- FY2024: 111 merits suits; $700M recovered for 21,000+ victims
- FY2025: 93 lawsuits (10-year low during quorum loss)

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### LGBTQ+ Enforcement Rollback (2025)
- April 2024: EEOC issued harassment guidance covering LGBTQ+ protections
- January 2025: Trump EO 14168 directed rescission
- January 28: Acting Chair rolled back Biden-era guidance
- May 2025: Texas court vacated portions of LGBTQ+ guidance
- Bostock remains good law but enforcement deprioritized

### DEI Enforcement Shift
- March 2025: EEOC/DOJ warned against "unlawful DEI-related discrimination"
- November 2025: First-known subpoena for DEI policy disclosure (Northwestern Mutual)
- Chair Lucas: "No 'good' race or sex discrimination"
- Corporate response: Meta, Target, Amazon scaled back DEI

### 2025 Administration Changes
- January 21: Trump appointed Lucas as Acting Chair
- January 28: Fired Democratic commissioners Samuels and Burrows
- January 28: Discharged General Counsel Gilbride
- April 2025: EO mandates cessation of "disparate impact" enforcement
