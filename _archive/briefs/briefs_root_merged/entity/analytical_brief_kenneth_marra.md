### Professional Background

Judge Kenneth Anthony Marra was born in 1951 in Queens, New York. He received his B.A. from the State University of New York at Stony Brook in 1973 and his J.D. from Stetson University College of Law in 1977.

**Career Timeline:**
- 1977-1980: Attorney, United States Department of Justice
- 1980-1983: Private practice, Washington, D.C.
- 1984-1996: Private practice, Florida
- 1996-2002: Judge, Circuit Court of Florida, Fifteenth Judicial Circuit
- 2002-present: U.S. District Judge, Southern District of Florida

Judge Marra was nominated by President George W. Bush on January 23, 2002, confirmed by the Senate on September 9, 2002, by a vote of 82-0, and received his commission on September 13, 2002. He assumed senior status on August 1, 2017.

### February 21, 2019 CVRA Ruling

Judge Marra ruled that federal prosecutors violated the Crime Victims' Rights Act when they secretly negotiated the 2008 non-prosecution agreement with Epstein without notifying identified victims.

Key findings from the ruling (as reported in public records):

> "Under the facts of this case, once the Government failed to advise the victims about its intention to enter into the [plea agreement], a violation of the CVRA occurred."

The ruling stated that the government:

> "spent untold hours negotiating the terms and implications of the [non-prosecution agreement] with Epstein's attorneys," while "scant information was shared with victims."

### April 7, 2015 Ruling on Joinder Motion

**[ECF Doc. 1330-1](https://thecontinuumreport.com/sources/giuffre-v-maxwell/ecf-1330-1.pdf):**

> "On April 7, 2015, Judge Marra denied Ms. Giuffre's motion to join the case as Jane Doe No. 3 and denied Jane Doe No. 4's motion to join as well."

### September 16, 2019 Remedies Ruling

Following his CVRA violation finding, Judge Marra issued a second ruling on remedies:

- Denied victims' request for monetary damages from the federal government
- Found that the CVRA explicitly states: "Nothing in this chapter shall be construed to authorize a cause of action for damages"
- Ruled that Epstein's death in August 2019 rendered the request to void the plea agreement moot

Judge Marra stated in his ruling:

> "Despite Petitioners having demonstrated the Government violated their rights under the CVRA, in the end they are not receiving much, if any, of the relief they sought. They may take solace, however, in the fact that this litigation has brought national attention to the Crime Victims' Rights Act and the importance of victims in the criminal justice system."

### Eleventh Circuit Reversal (2021)

Judge Marra's CVRA ruling was subsequently reversed by the Eleventh Circuit Court of Appeals sitting en banc. The appellate court ruled:

> "as the CVRA is currently written, rights under the Act do not attach until criminal proceedings have been initiated against a defendant, either by complaint, information, or indictment"

Because the government never filed charges against Epstein in the federal case, the appellate court held that the CVRA was never triggered, reversing Judge Marra's finding of a violation.
