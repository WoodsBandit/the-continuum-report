The reasons for the designation, and for the decision not to charge, remain outside the public record.

## Alternative Interpretations

Reasonable observers might interpret this evidence differently:

1. **The absence of charges is significant.** Federal prosecutors had access to extensive evidence and witness testimony. Their decision not to charge Kellen—despite the co-conspirator designation—may reflect an assessment that charges were not warranted or could not be proven.

2. **Fifth Amendment invocations are constitutionally protected** and should not be held against an individual. Lawyers routinely advise clients to invoke these protections in civil proceedings where criminal exposure may exist, regardless of actual culpability.

3. **The co-conspirator designation may be a legal technicality.** In federal practice, "unindicted co-conspirator" can be applied broadly. It does not require the same evidentiary showing as criminal charges and does not constitute a finding of guilt.

4. **Kellen may have been a victim herself.** The documents do not address whether Kellen may have been recruited, groomed, or coerced. Her circumstances during her employment with Epstein are not documented.

5. **Employment as an assistant does not establish criminal knowledge.** Many individuals work for people who engage in illegal activity without awareness of that activity.

6. **The nature of any direct interaction between Kellen and Giuffre** is not established in the documentary record. Their connection appears to be through shared involvement in litigation rather than documented personal interaction.
