# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: TENNESSEE VALLEY AUTHORITY

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

TVA is a federally-owned public power corporation, established May 18, 1933. Self-funded with $10B+ annual revenues and 34,000 MW capacity, it serves 10 million people across 7 states. Faces Kingston coal ash disaster legacy, CEO compensation controversy, rate increases, and 2025 privatization proposals.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA)
**Founding:** May 18, 1933 (TVA Act under FDR)
**Current CEO:** Don Moul (appointed April 2025)
**Headquarters:** Knoxville, Tennessee
**Service Area:** 80,000 square miles across TN, AL, MS, KY, GA, NC, VA
**Serves:** ~10 million people

---

## 2. BUDGET/FINANCES

**Self-Funding Model:** Zero taxpayer funding
**Annual Revenues:** $10+ billion from electricity sales
**Q1 FY2025:** $2.9 billion operating revenues
**Revenue Growth:** 5.6% increase over prior year
**Bond Authority:** $30 billion cap
**Debt Securities:** Secured by power revenues; no federal guarantee

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**Governance:**
- 9-member part-time Board of Directors (currently 3 members)
- Board appoints CEO; members select Chair
- 5-year staggered terms
- Geographic representation requirement

**Executive Leadership:**
- CEO/President (Don Moul, $1.2M base salary)
- Power generation/transmission
- Environmental stewardship
- Economic development
- River/reservoir management

**Recent Board Changes:**
- 3 members fired by Trump administration (2025)
- Lost quorum April 2024; restored December 2024

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Power Generation (Largest Public Utility in U.S.)
- 34,000-35,000 MW capacity
- 16,200 miles transmission lines
- Fuel mix: 42% nuclear, 31% gas, 14% coal, 9% hydro, 4% wind/solar
- Assets: 29 hydro dams, 4 coal plants, 9 gas plants, 7 nuclear reactors
- Third-largest nuclear fleet (7 units, 3 sites)

### Flood Control
- 29 hydroelectric dams on Tennessee River
- Reduced Chattanooga flood damage from $1.7M to near-zero

### Navigation
- 13 locks maintain 11-foot channel depth
- 25,000-30,000 barges annually (40-50M tons)
- Saves $400-500M annually in transport costs

### Economic Development
- Agricultural modernization
- Reforestation
- Regional infrastructure

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Kingston Coal Ash Disaster (2008)
- Largest coal ash spill in U.S. history
- 5.4 million cubic yards released
- 1 billion gallons of sludge covered 300 acres
- Cesium-137 contamination from Oak Ridge legacy
- 220+ worker lawsuits settled ($77.5M)
- 10 coal plants with 29 ash ponds; 119M cubic yards toxic waste

### CEO Compensation
- Jeff Lyash: ~$10.5M total (FY2023), highest-paid federal employee
- Two-thirds tied to coal/gas reliability bonuses
- No renewable/decarbonization metrics
- Board voted 6-3 to deny raise
- New CEO (Moul): $1.2M base

### Rate Increases
- FY2024: 4.5% increase
- FY2025: 5.25% increase
- Combined ~10% over two years
- Average $8.61/month additional per customer
- Raises rates "without public scrutiny"

### Privatization Proposals (2025)
- Trump administration active interest
- 3 board members fired; CEO threatened
- Stephen Smith: "Never seen TVA in such destabilized position"
- Opposition: State lawmakers, utilities, unions, environmental groups
- Counter: Private utilities charge 40% more; national security risk

---

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- Federal Corporation: Wholly-owned; GAO audits; congressional oversight
- FERC: Limited jurisdiction; TVA exempt from Federal Power Act
- EPA: Subject to Clean Air/Water Acts; permitting required
- TVA Act "Fence": Restricts power sales to 1957 service area
