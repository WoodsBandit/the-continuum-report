## THE WORKPLACE SAFETY CRISIS

### Before OSHA
- 14,000 workers died each year from workplace hazards (two years before OSHA)
- 2 million workers disabled or harmed annually
- Limited federal workplace safety regulation
- State regulations inconsistent and weak

### Labor Movement Push
- Tony Mazzocchi, labor and environment activist, was "principal force behind the legislation"
- United Steelworkers and other unions lobbied heavily
- Coal mine disasters drew public attention

---

## CREATION OF OSHA

### The OSH Act
- December 29, 1970: Nixon signed into law
- Created three entities:
  1. **OSHA** (Labor Department): Set and enforce workplace safety standards
  2. **NIOSH** (HHS): Conduct occupational safety research
  3. **OSHRC**: Independent commission to adjudicate enforcement

### OSHA Operational
- April 28, 1971: OSHA began operations
- Placed within Department of Labor

## BIPARTISAN ACHIEVEMENT

### Nixon's Legacy
- Often overlooked aspect of Nixon presidency
- Same period: Created EPA (1970)
- Environmental and worker safety progress under Republican president
- Demonstrated bipartisan consensus on basic protections

---

## IMPACT

### Results Since 1971
- Workplace fatalities cut by 60%
- Occupational injury and illness rates reduced by 40%
- Transformed workplace safety culture
- Established employer duty to provide safe workplace

### Standards
- Developed comprehensive safety standards
- Hazard communication
- Machine guarding
- Fall protection
- Chemical exposure limits
- Personal protective equipment
