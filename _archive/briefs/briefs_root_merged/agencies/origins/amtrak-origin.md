# ORIGIN STORY: AMTRAK (NATIONAL RAILROAD PASSENGER CORPORATION)

## FOUNDING DATE
October 30, 1970 (Rail Passenger Service Act signed)
May 1, 1971 (Operations began)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Rail Passenger Service Act of 1970 (Public Law 91-518)

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### RICHARD M. NIXON (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** January 9, 1913, Yorba Linda, California
- **Died:** April 22, 1994
- **Career:** Navy officer; Congressman; Senator; Vice President; President

#### Religion
- **Quaker** - raised in devout Quaker household

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Philosophy:** Pragmatic; supported rail subsidization despite free-market instincts

### Nixon and Rail
- Supported government subsidization of passenger trains
- Influential in Amtrak's creation
- October 30, 1970: Signed Rail Passenger Service Act

---

## CRISIS OF PASSENGER RAIL

### Decline of Rail Travel
- After WWI: Competition from automobiles
- After WWII: Competition from airlines
- By 1960s: Railroads in poor economic health
- Passenger service losing money; cross-subsidized by freight

### Railroads Wanted Out
- Private railroads required by law to maintain passenger service
- Passenger trains hemorrhaging money
- Railroads petitioned to discontinue service
- Public and Congress wanted to preserve some passenger rail

---

## CREATION OF AMTRAK

### Rail Passenger Service Act (1970)
- October 14, 1970: Congress passed "Railpax" bill
- October 30, 1970: Nixon signed Public Law 91-518
- Created National Railroad Passenger Corporation (NRPC)
- Relieved railroads of requirement to continue passenger service

### The Deal
- Railroads could join NRPC by contributing equipment/cash
- In return: Released from passenger service obligation
- 20 of 26 railroads joined
- 6 railroads (notably Southern, Denver & Rio Grande) declined

### Name Change
- Original nickname: "Railpax"
- April 21, 1971: Changed to "Amtrak"
- Name created by Lippincott & Margulies (PR firm)
- "Amtrak" = America + Track

---

## FIRST DAY OF OPERATIONS

### May 1, 1971
- First Amtrak train: "Clocker" No. 235
- Philadelphia to New York City
- Departed Penn Station 12:05 AM
- Historic moment for American passenger rail

### Initial Network
- Consolidated from 26 railroads' passenger routes
- Many routes eliminated
- Focus on major city pairs
- Started with inherited equipment

## EXPECTATIONS OF FAILURE

### "Last Hurrah"
- Nixon administration expected experiment to be short-lived
- Washington insiders viewed as politically expedient
- Way to give passenger trains "last hurrah"
- Public demanded rail preservation; politicians delivered face-saving measure

### Surprise Survival
- Amtrak survived and grew
- Now integral part of Northeast Corridor transportation
- Long-distance routes maintained (though debated)
- Passenger numbers grew over decades

---

## MISSION TODAY

### Operations
- Operates intercity passenger rail in 46 states
- Northeast Corridor: Boston-New York-Washington
- Long-distance routes across country
- State-supported corridors

### Status
- Government corporation
- Subsidized by federal appropriations
- Periodically threatened with funding cuts
- Ongoing debates about expansion vs. reduction
