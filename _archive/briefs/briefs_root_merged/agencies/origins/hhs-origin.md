# ORIGIN STORY: DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES

## FOUNDING DATE
April 11, 1953 (HEW established)
October 17, 1979 (HHS created when Education split off)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Reorganization Plan No. 1 of 1953 (HEW)
Department of Education Organization Act (1979 - created HHS from HEW)

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### OVETA CULP HOBBY (First Secretary of HEW)

#### Background
- **Born:** January 19, 1905, Killeen, Texas
- **Died:** August 16, 1995
- **Family:** Married William P. Hobby, former Governor of Texas
- **Career:** Businesswoman and media executive; ran the Houston Post newspaper

#### Military Service
- First Director of Women's Army Corps (WAC) during World War II
- Achieved rank of Colonel
- Pioneering role for women in military

#### Religion
- **Episcopalian**

#### Politics
- **Party Evolution:** Democrat who became Republican
- **1952:** Supported Dwight D. Eisenhower for president
- **Significance:** Second woman to serve in presidential cabinet (after Frances Perkins)
- **Distinction:** First woman in Republican administration's cabinet

#### Role as HEW Secretary
- April 11, 1953: Sworn in as first Secretary of HEW
- Oversaw controversial rollout of Salk polio vaccine
- Resigned 1955 due to husband's health issues

---

## EISENHOWER'S VISION

#### Creation of HEW
- Eisenhower proposed consolidating health, education, and welfare functions scattered across government
- Represented "modern Republican" approach to social programs
- Maintained New Deal programs while emphasizing efficient management

---

## EVOLUTION TO HHS

### 1979 Reorganization
- President Jimmy Carter signed Department of Education Organization Act
- Split HEW into two departments:
  - Department of Education (new)
  - Department of Health and Human Services (retained health and welfare functions)

### PATRICIA ROBERTS HARRIS (First HHS Secretary)
- Served as last HEW Secretary (1979)
- Became first HHS Secretary when reorganization took effect
- First African American woman to serve in U.S. Cabinet
- First African American woman to serve as U.S. Ambassador

---

## KEY DISTINCTION: HEW vs HHS

| HEW (1953-1979) | HHS (1979-present) |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Health, Education, AND Welfare | Health and Human Services only |
| Oveta Culp Hobby first Secretary | Patricia Roberts Harris first Secretary |
| Eisenhower administration creation | Carter administration reorganization |
