# ORIGIN STORY: FARM CREDIT ADMINISTRATION

## FOUNDING DATE
March 27, 1933 (Executive Order 6084)
June 16, 1933 (Farm Credit Act)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Executive Order 6084 (March 27, 1933)
Farm Credit Act of 1933 (last day of FDR's "Hundred Days")

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### FRANKLIN D. ROOSEVELT (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** January 30, 1882, Hyde Park, New York
- **Died:** April 12, 1945
- **Career:** NY State Senator; Assistant Secretary of Navy; NY Governor; 32nd President

#### Religion
- **Episcopalian**

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Philosophy:** New Deal; believed in cooperative public-private partnerships

### HENRY MORGENTHAU JR. (First Governor)

#### Background
- **Born:** May 11, 1891, New York City
- **Died:** February 6, 1967
- **Career:** Apple and dairy farmer; editor of American Agriculturist; FCA Governor; Treasury Secretary (1934-1945, longest-serving)

#### Religion
- **Jewish** (Reform tradition) - Father was leader of Reform Jewish community in NYC

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Philosophy:** Fiscal conservative despite New Deal role; believed in balanced budgets

## CONTEXT: FARM DEBT CRISIS

### Why FCA Was Created
- $12 billion farm debt burden
- Commodity prices at 49% of pre-WWI levels
- Nearly half of National Farm Loan Associations failing
- Farmers losing land to foreclosure

### Consolidation
FCA merged all federal agricultural credit agencies:
- Federal Land Banks
- Joint Stock Land Banks
- Federal Intermediate Credit Banks
- Production Credit Corporations
- Banks for Cooperatives

---

## MISSION

### Farm Credit System Oversight
- Regulate and examine Farm Credit System institutions
- Ensure safe and sound farm lending
- Support rural communities and agriculture

### Unique Structure
- Farmer-owned cooperatives
- Not taxpayer-funded (self-sustaining)
- Provides about 40% of U.S. farm debt
