# ORIGIN STORY: DEPARTMENT OF ENERGY

## FOUNDING DATE
August 4, 1977 (Department of Energy Organization Act signed)
August 5, 1977 (First Secretary sworn in)
October 1, 1977 (Department began operations)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Department of Energy Organization Act

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### JAMES R. SCHLESINGER (First Secretary of Energy)

#### Background
- **Born:** February 15, 1929, New York City
- **Education:** Harvard University
  - B.A. (1950)
  - M.A. (1952)
  - Ph.D. in economics (1956)
- **Academic Career:** Taught economics at University of Virginia (1955-1963)
- **Publications:** "The Political Economy of National Security" (1960)
- **Think Tank:** Director of strategic studies at RAND Corporation (1963-1969)

#### Religion
- Not documented in sources

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican (appointed by Democratic President Carter)
- **Philosophy:** Cold War strategist; national security hawk
- **Style:** Described as having "abrupt" manner with colleagues

---

## PRIOR GOVERNMENT EXPERIENCE

### Nixon Administration
- 1969: Assistant Director, Bureau of the Budget
- 1971: Chairman, Atomic Energy Commission
- 1973: Director of CIA (replaced Richard Helms; served 5 months)
- 1973: Secretary of Defense

### Ford Administration
- Continued as Secretary of Defense
- November 1975: Fired by Gerald Ford

- Ford also disliked Schlesinger's abrupt style

---

## APPOINTMENT AS ENERGY SECRETARY

### Carter's Selection
- January 1977: Carter appointed Schlesinger (a Republican) as special adviser on energy
- "Schlesinger impressed candidate Jimmy Carter with his brains, his high-level experience"
- October 1977: Became first Secretary of Energy

### What He Inherited
- Consolidated energy powers of 50+ agencies
- Abolished Federal Energy Administration and Energy Research and Development Administration
- Integrated Federal Power Commission functions

### Key Initiative
- Launched Department's Carbon Dioxide Effects and Assessment Program (early climate research)

---

## JIMMY CARTER (President)

### Energy as Priority
- Made clear "energy" would be linchpin of administration
- Announced energy as central focus
- Ordered 90-day energy plan
- Established Department of Energy

### The Moral Equivalent of War
- April 1977 speech: Declared energy crisis "the greatest challenge our country will face in our lifetime"
- Called it "the moral equivalent of war"

### Congressional Reality
- Senate opposition due to temporary oil surplus
- Dislike for tax features
- Demands for natural gas deregulation
- Schlesinger later described achievement as less than "moral equivalent of war" and more like "political equivalent of Chinese water torture"

---

## ENERGY CRISIS CONTEXT

### 1970s Crises
- 1973 Arab oil embargo
- 1979 Iranian Revolution oil disruption
- Long gas lines
- Economic stagflation

### Carter's Response
- Comprehensive energy policy
- Conservation emphasis
- Alternative energy development
- Creation of DOE to coordinate federal response
