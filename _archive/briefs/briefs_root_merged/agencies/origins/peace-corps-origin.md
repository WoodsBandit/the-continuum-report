# ORIGIN STORY: PEACE CORPS

## FOUNDING DATE
March 1, 1961 (Executive Order 10924 signed)
March 4, 1961 (Sargent Shriver became first Director)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Executive Order 10924 (initial); Peace Corps Act of September 22, 1961 (permanent)

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### JOHN F. KENNEDY (President)

#### The Founding Moment
- October 14, 1960: Arriving late to speak at University of Michigan at 2:00 AM
- Found himself facing crowd of 10,000 students
- Speaking extemporaneously, challenged American youth to "devote a part of their lives to living and working in Asia, Africa, and Latin America"
- Students responded with petition signed by 1,000 willing to serve abroad

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democratic
- **Philosophy:** New Frontier - emphasized sacrifice, service, idealism

#### Motivations
- Concerned about influence of Soviet communism worldwide
- Sought to harness energy and enthusiasm of America's youth
- Wanted to counter stereotype of "Ugly American" and "Yankee imperialism"
- Theme of volunteerism fit campaign themes of self-sacrifice
- Provided way to redefine American relations with Third World

#### Religion
- **Roman Catholic** - First Catholic president
- To avoid appearance of favoritism, Peace Corps did not place volunteers with any religious agencies

---

### SARGENT SHRIVER (First Director and Architect)

#### Background
- **Born:** November 9, 1915
- **Died:** January 18, 2011
- **Family:** Member of Shriver family by birth; Kennedy family by marriage to Eunice Kennedy
- **Position:** Brother-in-law to JFK

#### Religion
- **Roman Catholic** (devout)
- Credited with convincing hesitant Kennedy to contact Coretta Scott King after Martin Luther King Jr.'s 1960 jailing - helped strengthen Black support for Kennedy

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democratic
- **Philosophy:** Liberal idealist; believed in power of public service

#### Role in Peace Corps
- Between election and inauguration, Kennedy ordered Shriver to conduct feasibility study
- Shriver recalled: "We received more letters from people offering to work in or to volunteer for the Peace Corps... than for all other existing agencies"
- "Driving force behind the creation of the Peace Corps"
- Also founded Job Corps, Head Start, VISTA, Upward Bound
- "Architect of the 1960s war on poverty"

#### Peace Corps Development
- Deployment was rapid: Volunteers began serving in 5 countries in 1961
- In just under six years, developed programs in 55 countries with 14,500+ Volunteers

## INTELLECTUAL ORIGINS

### The Peace Army Concept
- "For more than 50 years, since the philosopher William James had called for the creation of a 'peace army' in which young Americans could serve around the world in the cause of peace and justice, Americans had talked about creating such an organization"

### Senator Hubert Humphrey
- Had proposed similar legislation before Kennedy's campaign

---

## POLITICAL CONTEXT

### Cold War Framework
- Peace Corps was tool for winning "hearts and minds" in developing world
- Alternative to Soviet influence
- Soft power projection through development and cultural exchange

### Anti-Imperialism Framing
- Kennedy wanted to counter "Ugly American" stereotype
- Peace Corps volunteers would live at local level, learn languages
- Represented democratic idealism rather than commercial exploitation

### 1961 Context
- Described as "most original, stimulating and promising foreign affairs project" of Kennedy administration
- Received more volunteer letters than any existing agency
