# ORIGIN STORY: FEDERAL TRADE COMMISSION

## FOUNDING DATE
September 26, 1914 (FTC Act signed)
March 16, 1915 (FTC opened for business)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Federal Trade Commission Act of 1914

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### WOODROW WILSON (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** December 28, 1856, Staunton, Virginia
- **Died:** February 3, 1924
- **Education:** Princeton (BA); University of Virginia Law; Johns Hopkins (PhD)
- **Career:** Professor; Princeton President; Governor of New Jersey; President

#### Religion
- **Presbyterian** - devoutly religious; son of Presbyterian minister
- Faith deeply influenced his political idealism

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democratic
- **Philosophy:** Progressive; "New Freedom" agenda; antitrust reform

### LOUIS BRANDEIS (Adviser/Intellectual Father)

#### Background
- Boston lawyer; "People's Attorney"
- Wilson adviser on trust legislation
- Later Supreme Court Justice (1916-1939)

#### Religion
- **Jewish** - first Jewish Supreme Court Justice

#### Politics
- Progressive; anti-monopoly; small business champion

### GEORGE RUBLEE (Co-Author of FTC Act)

#### Background
- Lawyer who helped draft FTC legislation
- Worked with Brandeis on progressive reforms

---

## CREATION OF FTC

### The Trust Problem
- 1911: Supreme Court broke up Standard Oil
- "Trust problem" dominated 1912 presidential election
- Multiple approaches debated: break up trusts vs. regulate them

### Predecessor Agency
- 1903: Theodore Roosevelt created Bureau of Corporations
- Investigated and reported on business practices
- Had 11 years of operation before FTC

### Legislative Path
- January 25, 1912: First FTC bill introduced by Rep. Dick T. Morgan (Oklahoma)
- February 21, 1912: Morgan gave first House floor speech advocating commission
- 1913-1914: Wilson administration pushed legislation forward
- September 26, 1914: Wilson signed FTC Act

---

## FIRST CHAIRMAN

### JOSEPH E. DAVIES

#### Background
- Commissioner of Corporations (FTC's predecessor)
- Transferred to FTC when it superseded Bureau of Corporations
- Served as Commissioner 1915-1916
- Served as Chairman 1915-1916

#### Religion
- **Christian** (denomination not documented)

#### Politics
- Democrat; Wilson loyalist
- Later Ambassador to Soviet Union (1936-1938)

### Davies's Contribution
- December 1913: Submitted memorandum to Wilson recommending trust legislation
- Advocated administrative regulation with limited judicial review
- Influenced drafting of FTC Act

---

## DISAPPOINTING START

### Original Sponsors' Assessment
- Rublee, Brandeis, and most historians agree FTC "got off to a disappointing start"
- Early Commission cautious and conservative
- Courts limited FTC authority in early cases
- Mission evolved significantly over following decades

---

## MISSION EVOLUTION

### Original Focus
- Prevent unfair methods of competition
- Antitrust enforcement partner to DOJ
- Investigate business practices

### Modern Mission
- Consumer protection
- Antitrust enforcement
- Privacy regulation
- False advertising
- Competition policy
