## CONTEXT: TWO FOUNDING MOMENTS

### 1883 (Pendleton Act)
- President Garfield assassinated by disappointed office-seeker (1881)
- "Spoils system" replaced with merit-based hiring
- Created Civil Service Commission

### 1978 (Civil Service Reform)
- Post-Watergate demand for government accountability
- Carter's experience streamlining Georgia government (278 agencies to 22)
- Commission abolished; replaced by three agencies:
  - OPM (personnel management)
  - MSPB (Merit Systems Protection Board)
  - FLRA (Federal Labor Relations Authority)

---

## CARTER ON REFORM

### Views
- Called civil service "tangled web of complicated rules and regulations"
- Wanted government "efficient, open and truly worthy of understanding and support"
- Viewed reform as restoring public trust after Watergate

---

## MISSION

### Federal Workforce Management
- Recruit, hire, and manage federal employees
- Administer federal employee benefits
- Background investigations (transferred to other agencies after 2015 breach)
- Policy guidance on personnel matters

### Scale
- Serves 2.1+ million federal civilian employees
