# ORIGIN STORY: BUREAU OF ALCOHOL, TOBACCO, FIREARMS AND EXPLOSIVES

## FOUNDING DATE
July 1, 1972 (became independent bureau under Treasury)
January 24, 2003 (transferred to Department of Justice)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Treasury Department Order 221 (1972)
Homeland Security Act of 2002 (transfer to DOJ)

---

## DEEP HISTORICAL ROOTS

### Whiskey Rebellion (1791)
- 1791: Congress imposed tax on distilled spirits to offset Revolutionary War debts
- Tax was deeply unpopular
- 1794: Whiskey Rebellion erupted in Pennsylvania
- Federal government suppressed rebellion, establishing principle of federal tax enforcement

### Evolution Through Prohibition
- 1919: Prohibition Unit created in Bureau of Internal Revenue
- Agency also had jurisdiction over federal drug laws 1914-1930
- End of Prohibition led to focus on alcohol taxation

---

## KEY FOUNDER (INDEPENDENT BUREAU)

### REX D. DAVIS (First Director)

#### Background
- Headed Alcohol, Tobacco and Firearms Division of IRS since 1970
- Oversaw transition to independent bureau status

#### Religion
- Not documented in sources

#### Politics
- Career government official; apolitical in public role

### Davis's Leadership
- Shepherded organization into new era
- Shifted primary mission toward federal firearms and explosives laws
- Addressed violent crime as new focus
- Also continued collecting billions in alcohol and tobacco taxes

---

## CREATION AS INDEPENDENT BUREAU

### Pre-1972 Structure
- **1952:** Alcohol and Tobacco Tax Division (ATTD) formed within IRS
- **1968:** Gun Control Act and Omnibus Crime Control Act expanded scope
  - Empowered to enforce laws against criminal use of explosives
  - Renamed Alcohol, Tobacco and Firearms Division (ATFD)

### Independence (1972)
- Duties had become "increasingly distinct from those of the IRS"
- July 1, 1972: Became independent bureau within Treasury Department
- Reported to Treasury's Office of Enforcement, Tariff and Trade Affairs

---

## TRANSFER TO DOJ (2003)

### Homeland Security Act
- November 2002: Congress passed Homeland Security Act
- January 24, 2003: ATF transferred from Treasury to Department of Justice
- Renamed Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives
- Tax and trade functions remained in Treasury (new Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau)

---

## MISSION EVOLUTION

### From Revenue to Law Enforcement
- Original focus: Tax collection on alcohol and tobacco
- 1960s-1970s: Added firearms and explosives enforcement
- Post-2003: Primarily law enforcement agency focused on:
  - Firearms trafficking
  - Explosives crimes
  - Arson investigation
  - Violent crime

### Controversies
- Ruby Ridge (1992)
- Waco Siege (1993)
- Fast and Furious operation (2006-2011)
