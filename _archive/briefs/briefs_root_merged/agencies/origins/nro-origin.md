# ORIGIN STORY: NATIONAL RECONNAISSANCE OFFICE

## FOUNDING DATE
September 6, 1961 (established)
1992 (existence publicly acknowledged)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Classified agreement between Secretary of Defense and Director of Central Intelligence

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### DWIGHT D. EISENHOWER (President, initiated concept)

#### Background
- **Born:** October 14, 1890, Denison, Texas
- **Died:** March 28, 1969
- **Military:** Five-star general; Supreme Allied Commander WWII
- **Career:** Columbia University President, NATO Commander, President

#### Religion
- **Presbyterian** - baptized and confirmed as Presbyterian while President (1953)
- First president baptized while in office

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Philosophy:** "Middle way" moderate; internationalist; military-industrial complex warning

### EDWIN "DIN" LAND (Scientific Adviser)

#### Background
- Founder of Polaroid Corporation
- Inventor of instant photography
- Key scientific adviser to presidents
- Member of President's Board of Consultants on Foreign Intelligence Activities

#### Religion
- **Jewish** background

#### Politics
- Apolitical scientist; national security focus

### JAMES KILLIAN (Scientific Adviser)

#### Background
- MIT President
- First presidential science adviser (under Eisenhower)
- Co-proposed NRO concept with Land

### ROBERT McNAMARA & ALLEN DULLES (Formal Creators)

#### Establishment
- September 1961: McNamara (Defense) and Dulles (CIA) reached agreement
- Formalized NRO establishment

---

## CREATION OF NRO

### Origins: U-2 and Corona
- 1950s: U-2 spy plane program demonstrated overhead reconnaissance value
- Corona: First successful reconnaissance satellite program
- Both considered national assets requiring unified management

### Eisenhower's Directive
- June 10, 1960: Ordered Defense Secretary to reassess space-based intelligence
- Concluded satellites were national assets
- Should be organized under civilian agency in Defense Department

### Land-Killian Recommendation
- August 25, 1960: Presented to Eisenhower at National Security Council
- Proposed new national office for overhead reconnaissance
- Key principles: Minimize bureaucracy, civilian-led, highly classified

---

## EXTREME SECRECY

### Classification
- Enabling directive made NRO classified organization
- Even office name classified "Secret" within compartmented channels
- "The fact of" organization's existence not acknowledged until 1991
- Cover organization: "Office of Space Systems, Office of the Secretary of the Air Force"

### 30 Years in Shadow
- Operated from 1961 to 1992 without public acknowledgment
- One of most secret organizations in U.S. government
- Declassification came only after Cold War ended

---

## MISSION

### Satellite Reconnaissance
- Designs, builds, launches reconnaissance satellites
- Provides satellite intelligence to multiple agencies:
  - SIGINT to NSA
  - IMINT (imagery) to NGA
  - MASINT (measurement/signature) to DIA

### "Big Five" Status
- Considered with CIA, NSA, DIA, NGA as core intelligence agencies
- Operates most advanced intelligence satellites globally
