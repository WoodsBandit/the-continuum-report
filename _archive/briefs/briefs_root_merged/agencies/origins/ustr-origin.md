# ORIGIN STORY: OFFICE OF THE U.S. TRADE REPRESENTATIVE

## FOUNDING DATE
January 15, 1963 (Executive Order 11075)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Trade Expansion Act of 1962 (P.L. 87-794)

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### JOHN F. KENNEDY (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** May 29, 1917
- **Died:** November 22, 1963
- **Career:** Congressman; Senator; 35th President

#### Religion
- **Roman Catholic**

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Philosophy:** Free trade internationalist

### Famous Statement
- Called the Trade Expansion Act "the most important international piece of legislation...since the passage of the Marshall Plan"

### GEORGE W. BALL (Drafter)

#### Background
- **Born:** December 21, 1909, Des Moines, Iowa
- **Died:** May 26, 1994
- **Career:** Law partner of Adlai Stevenson; Under Secretary of State (1961-1966); famous Vietnam War dissenter

#### Religion
- Not documented

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Philosophy:** Liberal internationalist; free trade; European unity advocate

### WILBUR D. MILLS (Congressional Architect)

#### Background
- **Born:** May 24, 1909, Kensett, Arkansas
- **Died:** May 2, 1992
- **Career:** Youngest county judge in Arkansas; Chairman, House Ways and Means Committee (1958-1974)

#### Religion
- Not documented

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democrat (Southern)
- **Philosophy:** Fiscal conservative on some issues; architect of Medicare

## FIRST TRADE REPRESENTATIVE

### CHRISTIAN A. HERTER

#### Background
- **Born:** March 28, 1895, Paris, France (American parents)
- **Died:** December 30, 1966
- **Career:** Foreign Service; Massachusetts Governor; Secretary of State (1959-1961); first Trade Representative (1963-1966)
- **Family:** Married granddaughter of Standard Oil's Charles Pratt

#### Religion
- Not documented

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Philosophy:** Internationalist; anti-isolationist; co-founded Council on Foreign Relations

### Views
- Washington Post: "Few men in public life did more to put an end to the sterile isolationism" between World Wars

---

## CONTEXT: COLD WAR TRADE POLICY

### Why USTR Was Created
- State Department previously handled trade negotiations
- Needed dedicated office to balance domestic/international interests
- Response to European Economic Community (EEC) formation
- Trade as tool of Cold War competition

### Original Name
- Office of the Special Trade Representative (STR)
- Renamed USTR in 1979-1980

---

## MISSION

### Trade Negotiations
- Leads U.S. trade and investment policy
- Negotiates trade agreements
- Coordinates trade policy across agencies
- Represents U.S. at WTO

### Cabinet Status
- Trade Representative is Cabinet-level position
- Ambassador rank
