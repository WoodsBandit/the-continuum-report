# ORIGIN STORY: NATIONAL ARCHIVES AND RECORDS ADMINISTRATION

## FOUNDING DATE
June 19, 1934

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
National Archives Act (48 Stat. 1122)

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### J. FRANKLIN JAMESON ("Father of the National Archives")

#### Background
- **Born:** September 19, 1859, Somerville, Massachusetts
- **Died:** September 28, 1937
- **Career:** First PhD in history from Johns Hopkins; professor at Johns Hopkins, Brown, Chicago; Director of Historical Research at Carnegie Institution; Chief of Manuscripts Division at Library of Congress; founding editor of American Historical Review

#### Religion
- Not documented

#### Politics
- **Historiographical Philosophy:** Progressive historian - emphasized social/economic factors over political/military history

### Campaign
- Led 28-year campaign for national archives (1906-1934)
- Built coalition: American Historical Association, DAR, American Library Association, American Legion, Treasury Secretary Mellon, Commerce Secretary Hoover

---

## FIRST ARCHIVIST

### ROBERT DIGGES WIMBERLY CONNOR

#### Background
- **Born:** September 26, 1878, Wilson, North Carolina
- **Died:** February 25, 1950
- **Career:** Founding Secretary of NC Historical Commission; UNC professor; first Archivist of the United States (1934-1941)
- **Family:** One of twelve children; father was NC Supreme Court Justice

#### Religion
- **Episcopalian** (family associated with Timothy's Episcopal Church)

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democrat (family background; father was "Cleveland Democrat")

## CONTEXT: PRESERVATION CRISIS

### Jefferson's Warning (1791)
- "Time and accident are committing daily havoc on the originals deposited in our public offices"

### Problem by 1930s
- American historical documents "kept in poor condition, largely inaccessible, and were in danger of destruction"
- Scattered across agencies with no unified preservation
- Records lost to fire, decay, neglect

### Herbert Hoover
- Laid cornerstone of National Archives building (February 20, 1933)
- Supported archives creation as Commerce Secretary

---

## ARCHITECTURE

### National Archives Building
- Architect: John Russell Pope (also designed Jefferson Memorial)
- Designed as "temple to history"
- Houses Declaration of Independence, Constitution, Bill of Rights

---

## LATER REORGANIZATION

### Independent Agency (1985)
- Public Law 98-497 (October 19, 1984)
- Became NARA (National Archives and Records Administration)
- Previously part of General Services Administration

---

## MISSION

### Preservation
- Preserves and provides access to federal records
- Manages Federal Register
- Administers presidential libraries
- Houses founding documents

### Scale
- Over 13 billion pages of records
- 15 presidential libraries
- 44 facilities nationwide
