# ORIGIN STORY: DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE

## FOUNDING DATE
June 22, 1870 (DOJ established)
July 1870 (First Attorney General to head DOJ sworn in)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Act to Establish the Department of Justice (June 22, 1870)

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### AMOS T. AKERMAN (First DOJ Attorney General)

#### Background
- **Born:** February 23, 1821, New Hampshire
- **Died:** December 21, 1880
- **Education:** Dartmouth College, 1842 (Phi Beta Kappa)
- **Career Path:** Went south to teach after college - first North Carolina, then Richmond County, Georgia
- **Legal Training:** 1846 - accepted position as tutor in Savannah home of Senator John Macpherson Berrien (former Attorney General); studied law in senator's library
- **Bar Admission:** Admitted to Georgia bar in 1850
- **Pre-War Life:** Owned farm and enslaved eleven people
- **Civil War:** Joined Confederate Army; achieved rank of colonel

#### Politics
- **Pre-War:** Whig - opposed secession as "unwise strategy" but followed adopted state into Confederacy
- **Post-War:** Joined Republican Party during Reconstruction
- **Philosophy:** Outspoken advocate for freedmen's civil rights
- **Distinction:** "Only person from the Confederacy to reach cabinet rank during Reconstruction"

#### Character
- "Small-town Georgia lawyer"
- "Prized the stability of the law above almost everything else"
- "Resented the fact that some southerners so easily disobeyed the U.S. Constitution"

#### DOJ Achievements
- First U.S. Attorney General to head the newly formed Justice Department
- Strenuously investigated and prosecuted Klan activities
- Under his leadership, the Ku Klux Klan was "effectively ended"
- Created department's first investigative unit (later became FBI)
- In South Carolina: Arrested 470+ Klan members; hundreds more fled state
- By 1872: Klan's power collapsed; African Americans voted in record numbers in South

#### Resignation
- Not everyone in Grant administration shared Akerman's anti-Klan zeal
- Secretary of State Hamilton Fish was "put off by it"
- Criticism from powerful railroad interests
- Grant asked for Akerman's resignation in December 1871

---

### ULYSSES S. GRANT (President)

#### Role
- June 22, 1870: Signed bill creating Department of Justice
- Appointed Akerman as Attorney General
- Appointed Benjamin H. Bristow as America's first Solicitor General
- Sent federal troops to South to enforce civil rights legislation

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Philosophy:** Believed in protecting rights of freedmen during Reconstruction

---

### BENJAMIN H. BRISTOW (First Solicitor General)

#### Role
- America's first Solicitor General
- Together with Akerman, "vigorously prosecuted Ku Klux Klan members in the early 1870s"

---

## FOUNDING PURPOSE

### Civil Rights Mission
- "Created 150 Years Ago, the Justice Department's First Mission Was to Protect Black Rights"
- DOJ created specifically to handle federal government's litigation and enforce civil rights laws
- Klan prosecution was original raison d'etre

### Consolidation
- Prior to DOJ, legal work scattered across departments
- DOJ consolidated all federal government litigation under one agency

---

## HISTORICAL SIGNIFICANCE

### Reconstruction Context
- 14th and 15th Amendments recently ratified
- Ku Klux Klan terrorizing Black communities in South
- Need for federal agency to enforce new constitutional rights

### Akerman's Unique Position
- Former Confederate who became civil rights champion
- Understood Southern resistance from inside
- Zealously prosecuted former allies in Klan
