# ORIGIN STORY: OFFICE OF THE COMPTROLLER OF THE CURRENCY

## FOUNDING DATE
February 25, 1863

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
National Currency Act of 1863 (later National Banking Act of 1864)

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### SALMON P. CHASE (Treasury Secretary)

#### Background
- **Born:** January 13, 1808, Cornish, New Hampshire
- **Died:** May 7, 1873
- **Career:** Defended fugitive slaves ("Attorney General for Runaway Negroes"); Senator; Governor of Ohio; Treasury Secretary; Chief Justice of the United States

#### Religion
- **Protestant Episcopal (Anglican)** - Raised by uncle Bishop Philander Chase
- Put "In God We Trust" on U.S. coins (1864)

#### Politics
- **Party:** Whig → Liberty Party → Free Soil → Republican
- **Philosophy:** Anti-slavery constitutionalist; created slogan "Free Soil, Free Labor, Free Men"

### ABRAHAM LINCOLN (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** February 12, 1809
- **Died:** April 15, 1865
- **Career:** Lawyer; Congressman; 16th President

#### Religion
- No formal church membership

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Philosophy:** Union preservation; gradual emancipation

---

## FIRST COMPTROLLER

### HUGH McCULLOCH

#### Background
- **Born:** December 7, 1808, Kennebunk, Maine
- **Died:** May 24, 1895
- **Career:** Fort Wayne banker; came to Washington to OPPOSE national banking system; persuaded by Chase to lead it instead

#### Religion
- Not documented (New England Protestant background)

#### Politics
- **Party:** Whig → Republican
- **Philosophy:** "Original Henry Clay Whig"; hard money advocate

---

## CONTEXT: CIVIL WAR FINANCING

### Why OCC Was Created
- Union needed money to finance Civil War
- State bank notes created chaotic currency system
- 1,600 different state banks issued own notes
- Many worthless; counterfeiting rampant

### National Banking System
- Uniform national currency
- Banks required to purchase government bonds
- Created stable banking system that lasted until Federal Reserve (1913)

---

## CHASE'S CONSTITUTIONAL PHILOSOPHY

### "Freedom National" Doctrine
- Argued Constitution was fundamentally anti-slavery
- Freedom was default; slavery existed only by state law
- Fifth Amendment's due process couldn't justify slavery
- Federal government had no authority to protect slavery in territories

### Texas v. White (1869)
- As Chief Justice, declared: "the Constitution...looks to an indestructible union composed of indestructible states"

---

## McCULLOCH'S IRONIC CONVERSION

### From Opponent to Leader
- Came to Washington in 1862 to oppose national banking bill
- Met with Secretary Chase
- Persuaded that system would work
- Became its first administrator
- Later served as Treasury Secretary under Lincoln, Johnson, and Arthur

---

## MISSION

### Bank Supervision
- Charter national banks
- Supervise and examine national banks
- Regulate federal savings associations
- Ensure safe and sound banking system

### Modern Scope
- Supervises approximately 1,200 national banks
- $14 trillion in assets
- Part of federal banking regulatory framework with Fed and FDIC
