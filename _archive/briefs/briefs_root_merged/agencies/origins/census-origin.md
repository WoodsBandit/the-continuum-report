# ORIGIN STORY: UNITED STATES CENSUS BUREAU

## FOUNDING DATE
1790 (First census conducted)
March 6, 1902 (Permanent Census Office established)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
U.S. Constitution, Article 1, Section 2 (census mandate)
Act of March 6, 1902 (permanent agency)

---

## CONSTITUTIONAL MANDATE

### Article 1, Section 2
- Constitution requires census every 10 years
- Purpose: Apportion seats in House of Representatives
- Began in 1790

### First Census (1790)

#### THOMAS JEFFERSON (Secretary of State)

##### Background
- **Born:** April 13, 1743, Shadwell, Virginia
- **Died:** July 4, 1826
- **Career:** Author of Declaration; Governor; Minister to France; Secretary of State; Vice President; President

##### Religion
- **Deist** - rejected orthodox Christianity; admired Jesus's moral teachings

##### Politics
- **Party:** Democratic-Republican
- **Philosophy:** Agrarian democracy; limited government; states' rights

### Jefferson Led First Census
- Secretary of State oversaw early censuses
- First census: 6 questions
  - Name of head of family
  - Number of persons in 5 categories
- 56 pages when published
- Cost: $44,377.28
- Approximately 650 U.S. Marshals conducted enumeration

---

## EARLY ADMINISTRATION

### Temporary Offices
- Until 1902: Temporary census offices assembled for each census
- U.S. Marshals or enumerators conducted counts
- Offices disbanded after tabulation complete

### Growing Complexity
- Mid-1800s: Temporary offices under Interior Department
- Herman Hollerith's tabulating machine (1890) revolutionized processing
- Growing volume of data required skilled, permanent staff
- Retention of expertise became necessary

---

## PERMANENT AGENCY (1902)

### Theodore Roosevelt Era
- March 6, 1902: Congress created permanent Census Office
- Initially within Department of Interior
- July 1, 1902: Agency officially opened doors
- Location: Emery Building, Washington, D.C.

### First Permanent Director
- **William Rush Merriam** - Director when agency became permanent (1902)

### Move to Commerce
- February 14, 1903: Moved to Department of Commerce and Labor
- March 4, 1913: Commerce and Labor split; Census stayed with Commerce
- Remains in Department of Commerce today

---

## EXPANSION OF MISSION

### Beyond Decennial Census
- Permanent bureau allowed expanded survey-taking
- Economic censuses
- American Community Survey
- Current Population Survey
- Numerous other surveys

### Modern Functions
- Decennial census (constitutional mandate)
- Economic statistics
- Demographic research
- Population estimates
- Geographic programs

---

## CONTEXT: CONSTITUTIONAL FOUNDATION

### Why Census Matters
- Apportionment of House seats
- Electoral College allocation
- Federal funding distribution
- Redistricting basis
- Planning and research data

### Counting Every Person
- Constitution says "actual Enumeration"
- Count every person, not just citizens
- Political controversies persist over methodology
