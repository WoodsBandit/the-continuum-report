# ORIGIN STORY: SMALL BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION

## FOUNDING DATE
July 30, 1953

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Small Business Act of 1953 (P.L. 83-163)

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### DWIGHT D. EISENHOWER (Signing President)

#### Background
- **Born:** October 14, 1890, Denison, Texas
- **Died:** March 28, 1969
- **Career:** Army General; Supreme Allied Commander WWII; NATO Commander; 34th President

#### Religion
- **Presbyterian** - Baptized February 1, 1953 (10 days after inauguration; only president baptized in office)
- Parents were River Brethren (Mennonite), later Jehovah's Witnesses

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Philosophy:** Moderate; recognized New Deal programs as permanent

### Famous Statement
- "Our form of government has no sense unless it is founded in a deeply felt religious faith"

---

## KEY CONGRESSIONAL ADVOCATES

### WRIGHT PATMAN (Champion of Small Business)

#### Background
- **Born:** August 6, 1893, near Hughes Springs, Texas
- **Died:** March 7, 1976
- **Career:** WWI officer; Texas legislator; Congressman (24 terms); House Banking Committee Chairman

#### Religion
- **Primitive Baptist** (family background)

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Philosophy:** Populist; anti-big banks; co-author Robinson-Patman Act protecting small retailers

### Famous Quote on SBA
- Opposed placing SBA under Commerce Department, comparing it to "sending a rabbit for a head of lettuce"

### JESSE P. WOLCOTT (Banking Committee Chairman)

#### Background
- **Born:** March 3, 1893, Gardner, Massachusetts
- **Died:** January 28, 1969
- **Career:** WWI infantry officer; prosecutor; Congressman (1931-1957); later FDIC Chairman

#### Religion
- **Universalist** or Congregationalist

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Philosophy:** Conservative; opposed much of New Deal; supported civil rights measures

## FIRST ADMINISTRATOR

### WILLIAM D. MITCHELL

#### Background
- Business executive; former RFC official
- First SBA Administrator (October 15, 1953)
- Served briefly

### WENDELL B. BARNES (Second, Longer-Serving)

#### Background
- **Born:** 1909, Ponca City, Oklahoma
- **Died:** 1985
- **Career:** Brown University; Michigan Law; Douglas Aircraft counsel; Oklahoma legislator; SBA Administrator (1954-1959)

---

## CONTEXT: RFC DISSOLUTION

### Reconstruction Finance Corporation
- Created 1932 under Hoover
- Plagued by corruption scandals
- Fulbright investigation (1950) exposed problems
- Republicans needed replacement to gain bipartisan support for RFC dissolution

### SBA Creation
- Title I: Dissolved RFC
- Title II: Created SBA
- Initial loan fund: $275 million

---

## MISSION

### Small Business Support
- Loans and loan guarantees
- Government contracting assistance
- Counseling and training
- Disaster assistance

### Original Purpose
- "Aid, counsel, assist and protect the interests of small business concerns"
- "Preserve free competitive enterprise"

### Scale Today
- Backs approximately $28 billion in loans annually
- Served over 500,000 small businesses in 2023
