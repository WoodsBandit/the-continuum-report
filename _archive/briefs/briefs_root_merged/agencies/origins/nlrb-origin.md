# ORIGIN STORY: NATIONAL LABOR RELATIONS BOARD

## FOUNDING DATE
July 5, 1935 (Wagner Act signed)
August 27, 1935 (NLRB first meeting)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
National Labor Relations Act of 1935 (Wagner Act)

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### ROBERT F. WAGNER (Senator, New York)

#### Background
- **Born:** June 8, 1877, Nastatten, Germany
- **Died:** May 4, 1953
- **Immigration:** Came to U.S. age 9; grew up in poverty in New York City
- **Education:** City College of New York; New York Law School
- **Career:** NY State legislator; NY Supreme Court Justice; U.S. Senator (1927-1949)

#### Religion
- **Roman Catholic** - devout; immigrant Catholic identity shaped views

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democratic
- **Philosophy:** Progressive; labor rights champion; urban liberal
- Co-author of many New Deal laws (Social Security, public housing)

---

## CONTEXT: LABOR STRIFE

### Pre-Wagner Act Conditions
- Employers free to spy on, interrogate, discipline, discharge, and blacklist union members
- Company unions (employer-controlled) widespread
- 1933-1934: Wave of strikes across nation
- Violent confrontations between workers and private security forces
- Citywide general strikes; factory takeovers

### Legal Background
- 1935: Supreme Court struck down National Industrial Recovery Act
- Organized labor again vulnerable to employer retaliation
- Need for permanent labor law framework urgent

---

## HISTORICAL ROOTS

### Colorado Fuel and Iron Strike (1914)
- Bloody strike against Rockefeller-owned company
- John D. Rockefeller Jr. brought in Canadian politician Mackenzie King
- Settlement created Management-Labor conciliation board
- Template for future labor dispute resolution

---

## CREATION OF NLRB

### The Wagner Bill
- February 1935: Senator Wagner introduced National Labor Relations Act
- Proposed independent agency (NLRB) with three members
- Appointed by President, confirmed by Senate

### "Labor's Magna Carta"
The Act provided:
- Workers' right to organize into trade unions
- Right to collective bargaining
- Right to strike
- Ban on company unions
- Employers required to bargain with chosen union representatives
- Prohibited employer "unfair labor practices"

### Passage
- July 5, 1935: FDR signed into law
- August 27, 1935: NLRB held first meeting

---

## CONSTITUTIONAL CHALLENGE

### Opposition
- Strongly opposed by conservatives and Republicans
- Business community challenged constitutionality

### NLRB v. Jones & Laughlin Steel Corp. (1937)
- April 12, 1937: Supreme Court upheld Wagner Act
- 5-4 decision
- Validated broad federal authority over labor relations
- Part of "Constitutional Revolution of 1937"

---

## FIRST CHAIRMAN

### J. WARREN MADDEN

#### Background
- Law professor at University of Pittsburgh
- First NLRB Chairman (1935-1940)

#### Religion
- Not documented in sources

#### Politics
- New Deal liberal; labor sympathizer

---

## LATER AMENDMENTS

### Taft-Hartley Act (1947)
- Amended Wagner Act
- Established unfair labor practices for unions (not just employers)
- Allowed states to pass right-to-work laws
- Restricted union political activity
