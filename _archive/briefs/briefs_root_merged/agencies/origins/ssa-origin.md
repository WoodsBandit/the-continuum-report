# ORIGIN STORY: SOCIAL SECURITY ADMINISTRATION

## FOUNDING DATE
August 14, 1935 (Social Security Act signed)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Social Security Act of 1935

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### FRANCES PERKINS (Driving Force)

#### Background
- **Born:** April 10, 1882, Boston, Massachusetts
- **Education:** Mount Holyoke College (1902); Columbia University MA in sociology (1910)
- **Formative Experience:** Witnessed Triangle Shirtwaist Factory fire (1911) from across the street - motivated her reform work
- **Early Career:** Volunteer at Jane Addams's Hull House, Chicago (1904-1907)
- **Position:** Secretary of Labor (1933-1945) - first woman to hold cabinet position in U.S. history

#### Religion
- **Episcopalian** (practicing)
- Note: Some adversaries suggested she was secretly Jewish - "a conspiracy theory bigots concocted because antisemitism was prevalent in American society"

#### Politics
- **Philosophy:** Progressive reformer, labor advocate
- **Approach:** Pragmatic - worked to make Social Security politically viable

#### Role in Social Security
- Described as the "driving force" behind Social Security legislation
- Led working team that created Social Security plan
- Steered bill through "Washington's treacherous political waters"
- Chaired President's Committee on Economic Security

---

### EDWIN WITTE ("Father of Social Security")

#### Background
- **Title:** Known as the "Father of Social Security"
- **Position:** Chairman, Department of Economics, University of Wisconsin
- **Origin:** Wisconsin native from rural Jefferson County
- **Prior Role:** Headed Wisconsin Legislative Reference Bureau (drafts legislation) in 1920s

#### Intellectual Heritage
- Product of the "Wisconsin Idea" - concept that government and academic experts should help solve social and economic problems
- Mentors: John R. Commons and Richard T. Ely (designers of Wisconsin Idea)
- Conceptual underpinnings of Social Security came directly from Wisconsin Idea

#### Role in Social Security
- July 24, 1934: Offered executive director position for Committee on Economic Security
- July 26, 1934: Arrived in Washington ready to work
- Led technical work of designing the system

---

### ARTHUR J. ALTMEYER ("Mr. Social Security")

#### Background
- **Born:** May 8, 1891, De Pere, Wisconsin
- **Died:** October 16, 1972
- **Early Career:** Public school teacher and principal
- **Education:** University of Wisconsin-Madison graduate student (1918); studied under John R. Commons

#### Role in Social Security
- Mentioned by Perkins as her "right-hand man"
- Chairman of Social Security Board (1937-1946)
- First Commissioner for Social Security (1946-1953)
- Wilbur Cohen called Altmeyer "Mr. Social Security" in 1973 speech

---

### FRANKLIN D. ROOSEVELT (President)

#### Role
- Signed Social Security Act August 14, 1935
- Championed legislation as part of New Deal
- Created President's Committee on Economic Security to design program

---

## POLITICAL CONTROVERSY

### "Socialism" Charge
- Opponents decried proposal as socialism
- Senator Thomas Gore (D-OK) at Senate Finance Committee hearing asked Perkins: "Isn't this Socialism?"
- Perkins replied it was not
- Gore continued: "Isn't this a teeny-weeny bit of Socialism?"

### Political Passage
- Part of New Deal legislative agenda
- Required navigating opposition from multiple directions
- Perkins's political skill credited with passage

---

## INTELLECTUAL ORIGINS

### The Wisconsin Connection
- Wisconsin provided intellectual framework
- John R. Commons (economist) - one of handful of American economists versed in social insurance
- Richard T. Ely - also influenced Witte and Altmeyer
- Wisconsin already had progressive social insurance programs
