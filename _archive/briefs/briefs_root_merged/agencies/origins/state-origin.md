# ORIGIN STORY: DEPARTMENT OF STATE

## FOUNDING DATE
September 26, 1789 (appointment); March 22, 1790 (entered duty)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Department of Foreign Affairs renamed Department of State by Act of Congress

---

## KEY FOUNDER: THOMAS JEFFERSON

### Background
- **Born:** April 13, 1743, Shadwell, Virginia
- **Family:** Virginia planter elite
- **Education:** College of William & Mary (1762); studied law
- **Legal Career:** Admitted to Virginia bar 1767
- **Political Start:** Elected to Virginia House of Burgesses 1769
- **Prior Role:** U.S. Minister to France 1785-1789

### Religion
- **Religious Views:** Diverged widely from traditional Christianity of his era
- **Philosophy:** Most comfortable with Deism, rational religion, theistic rationalism, and Unitarianism
- **Beliefs:** Did not believe in miracles or divinity of Jesus, though held lifelong esteem for Jesus' moral teachings
- **Jefferson Bible:** Produced a Bible cutting out supernatural elements of Christ's life
- **Public Stance:** Raised Anglican but reluctant to reveal personal religion publicly
- **Catholic theologian assessment (Avery Dulles):** "Christian deist" - believed Christianity was highest expression of natural religion

### Politics
- **Party:** Co-founded Democratic-Republican Party (opposed Hamilton's Federalists)
- **Philosophy:** Limited federal government, agrarianism, strict constitutional interpretation
- **Key Principle:** Freedom of thought essential for true religion AND functioning republic
- **Separation of Church and State:** Coined "wall of separation" interpretation of First Amendment

### Views on Founding Documents / America
- **Declaration of Independence:** Primary author (1776, at age 33)
  - Drafted June 11-28, 1776
  - Drew on Virginia Constitution draft, Virginia Declaration of Rights (George Mason)
  - Phrase "all men are created equal" called "one of the best-known sentences in the English language"
- **Constitution:** Supported Constitution but desired addition of Bill of Rights
- **Religious Liberty:** Urged James Madison to amend Constitution to include written guarantee of religious liberty (1787)
- **Virginia Statute for Religious Freedom:** Author of landmark religious freedom legislation
- **Deism in Declaration:** Phrases such as "Nature's God" typical of Deism (though also used by non-Deists)

### Key Founding Contributions
- Author of Declaration of Independence
- First Secretary of State (1790-1793)
- Author of Virginia Statute for Religious Freedom
- Champion of religious liberty in Constitution

---

## TENURE AS SECRETARY OF STATE
March 22, 1790 - December 31, 1793
