# ORIGIN STORY: CENTERS FOR DISEASE CONTROL AND PREVENTION

## FOUNDING DATE
July 1, 1946

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Administrative action within U.S. Public Health Service

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### DR. JOSEPH W. MOUNTIN (Founder)

#### Background
- Chief, Bureau of State Services, U.S. Public Health Service
- Assistant Surgeon General
- Visionary public health leader
- Conceived "Centers" concept

#### Religion
- Not documented in sources

#### Politics
- Career public health official; apolitical

### Mountin's Vision
- Wanted "center" to help states eradicate infectious diseases
- Deliberately avoided word "institute" to not compete with NIH
- Sought practical, applied public health mission
- Focused on working with state and local health departments

---

## ORIGINS IN MALARIA CONTROL

### World War II Background
- U.S. military suffered severely from malaria in WWI
- 600+ military bases in malaria-endemic Southern U.S.
- 1,000+ essential war facilities in South
- 1942: Malaria Control in War Areas (MCWA) established
- MCWA successful in protecting military personnel

### From War to Peace
- As WWII ended, Mountin saw opportunity
- MCWA expertise could be redirected to civilian health
- July 1, 1946: Communicable Disease Center (CDC) founded
- Descended directly from MCWA

---

## WHY ATLANTA?

### Geographic Logic
- Malaria endemic in Southern United States
- Needed to be in heart of malaria zone
- Far from Washington bureaucracy
- Close to problems being addressed

### Original Location
- Sixth floor of Volunteer Building on Peachtree Street, Atlanta
- Hundreds of miles from other federal agencies

### Move to Clifton Road
- 1947: CDC employees collected $10 to pay Emory University for 15 acres
- Clifton Road remains CDC headquarters today

---

## EARLY OPERATIONS

### Malaria Focus
- 59% of first-year personnel focused on malaria eradication
- DDT (available since 1943) was primary weapon
- Over 6.5 million homes sprayed
- Malaria virtually eliminated from U.S. by 1951

### Small Beginning
- 369 employees at founding
- Only 7 medical officers
- Key jobs: entomologists and engineers (to fight mosquitoes)
- Budget: modest

---

## EVOLUTION OF MISSION

### Expanding Scope
Mountin pushed CDC to take on other communicable diseases:
- Typhoid
- Diphtheria
- Tuberculosis
- Polio
- Eventually: all infectious diseases

### Name Changes
- 1946: Communicable Disease Center
- 1970: Center for Disease Control
- 1980: Centers for Disease Control
- 1992: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (kept CDC acronym)

---

## CDC TODAY

### Global Health Leader
- World's premier public health agency
- Responds to outbreaks worldwide
- Sets vaccination schedules
- Tracks disease patterns
- Employs approximately 15,000+

### Part of HHS
- Component of Department of Health and Human Services
- Headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia
