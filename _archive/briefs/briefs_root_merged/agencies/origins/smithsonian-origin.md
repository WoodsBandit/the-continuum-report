# ORIGIN STORY: SMITHSONIAN INSTITUTION

## FOUNDING DATE
August 10, 1846

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Act to Establish the Smithsonian Institution (August 10, 1846)

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### JAMES SMITHSON (Benefactor)

#### Background
- **Born:** c. 1765, Paris, France
- **Died:** June 27, 1829, Genoa, Italy
- **Nationality:** British
- **Education:** Oxford University
- **Career:** Scientist; mineralogist; chemist
- **Never visited the United States**

#### Religion
- Not documented (Church of England by birth)

#### Politics
- Sympathetic to republican ideals
- Rejected aristocratic pretensions despite noble birth

### The Mystery of His Gift
- Illegitimate son of Hugh Smithson, Duke of Northumberland
- Could not inherit father's title due to birth circumstances
- British society denied him full recognition
- May have sought lasting fame through his bequest

### Famous Statement
- "My name shall live in the memory of man when the titles of the Northumberlands... are extinct and forgotten"

---

## THE BEQUEST

### Smithson's Will
- Left estate to nephew, Henry James Hungerford
- If nephew died without heirs, entire estate to United States
- Purpose: "an establishment for the increase and diffusion of knowledge among men"

### Timeline
- 1829: Smithson died in Genoa
- 1835: Nephew died without heirs
- 1838: U.S. received bequest
- Amount: Over 100,000 gold sovereigns (~$500,000)
- Equivalent today: Approximately $17 million

---

## CONGRESSIONAL DEBATE

### What to Do With the Gift?
- Congress debated for 8 years
- Various proposals:
  - National university
  - Observatory
  - Library
  - Museum
  - Research institution
- John Quincy Adams championed accepting and using gift wisely

### Resolution
- August 10, 1846: President James K. Polk signed act establishing Smithsonian
- Compromise: "Establishment" with multiple functions
- Included museum, library, research, publications

---

## SIGNING PRESIDENT

### JAMES K. POLK

#### Background
- **Born:** November 2, 1795, Pineville, North Carolina
- **Died:** June 15, 1849
- **Career:** Lawyer; Tennessee Governor; President (1845-1849)

#### Religion
- **Methodist** (baptized on deathbed)
- Parents were Presbyterian; wife was Presbyterian

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democratic
- **Philosophy:** Jacksonian Democrat; expansionist

---

## FIRST SECRETARY

### JOSEPH HENRY (1846-1878)

#### Background
- **Born:** December 17, 1797, Albany, New York
- **Died:** May 13, 1878
- **Career:** Physicist; discovered electromagnetic induction (simultaneously with Faraday)
- Professor at Princeton

#### Religion
- **Presbyterian**

#### Politics
- Scientist; focused on research over politics

### Henry's Vision
- Emphasized research and publication
- "Increase" knowledge through original research
- "Diffuse" knowledge through publications
- Resisted being merely a museum
- Established Smithsonian as research institution

---

## "THE NATION'S ATTIC"

### Evolution
- Grew to become world's largest museum and research complex
- Now includes 21 museums and galleries
- National Zoo
- Research facilities worldwide
- Over 154 million artifacts and specimens

### Free Admission
- From beginning: Free and open to public
- Funded by federal appropriations and Smithsonian Trust
- Embodies democratic access to knowledge

---

## SMITHSON'S REMAINS

### Brought to America
- 1904: Alexander Graham Bell led expedition to Genoa
- Retrieved Smithson's remains
- Interred at Smithsonian Castle
- Smithson finally "came" to America 75 years after his death
