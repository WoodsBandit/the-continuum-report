# ORIGIN STORY: UNITED STATES PATENT AND TRADEMARK OFFICE

## FOUNDING DATE
April 10, 1790 (First Patent Act)
July 31, 1790 (First patent granted)
1836 (Became distinct bureau)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Patent Act of 1790

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### GEORGE WASHINGTON (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** February 22, 1732, Westmoreland County, Virginia
- **Died:** December 14, 1799
- **Career:** Surveyor; planter; Commander; first President

#### Religion
- **Episcopalian** (Anglican/Church of England)

#### Politics
- **Party:** None
- **Philosophy:** Federalist in practice

### Signed First Patent Law
- April 10, 1790: Washington signed Patent Act
- Among earliest laws of new republic
- Recognized importance of protecting invention

---

### THOMAS JEFFERSON (First Patent Examiner)

#### Background
- Secretary of State under Washington
- Inventor himself (moldboard plow, wheel cipher, etc.)

#### Religion
- **Deist**

#### Politics
- Democratic-Republican

### Original Patent Board
The first patent board consisted of three Cabinet members:
1. **Thomas Jefferson** (Secretary of State)
2. **Henry Knox** (Secretary of War)
3. **Edmund Randolph** (Attorney General)

### Jefferson's Involvement
- Personally examined early patent applications
- Brought scientific understanding to review
- Helped establish standards for patentability

---

## FIRST PATENT

### Samuel Hopkins (July 31, 1790)
- First U.S. patent ever granted
- Process for making potash (potassium carbonate)
- Ingredient used in fertilizer and soap
- Signed by Washington, Jefferson, and Randolph

---

## EVOLUTION OF PATENT OFFICE

### 1802: First Clerk
- Congress created clerk position in State Department
- Dedicated to handling patent matters
- Beginning of specialized staff

### 1836: Distinct Bureau
- Patent Office became distinct bureau
- First Commissioner of Patents appointed
- Organized examination system established
- Fire that year destroyed many early records

### 1849: Move to Interior
- Patent Office transferred to Department of Interior
- Remained there until 1925

### 1925: Move to Commerce
- Patent Office transferred to Department of Commerce
- Recognized connection to business/industry

### 1975: Name Change
- Renamed "Patent and Trademark Office"
- Reflected expanded trademark responsibilities
- "USPTO" became official abbreviation

---

## CONSTITUTIONAL BASIS

### Article I, Section 8, Clause 8
- Congress has power "To promote the Progress of Science and useful Arts"
- By securing to authors and inventors exclusive rights
- For limited times to their writings and discoveries
- Foundation for patent and copyright law

---

## MISSION

### Patents
- Examine patent applications
- Grant patents for inventions
- Maintain patent records
- Promote innovation

### Trademarks
- Register trademarks
- Protect brand identities
- Combat counterfeiting

### Economic Impact
- Patents fuel innovation economy
- Trademarks protect consumer trust
- Intellectual property = significant economic asset
