# ORIGIN STORY: DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION

## FOUNDING DATE
October 15, 1966 (DOT Act signed)
January 16, 1967 (First Secretary sworn in)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Department of Transportation Act

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### ALAN S. BOYD (First Secretary of Transportation)

#### Background
- **Born:** July 10, 1922, Jacksonville, Florida
- **Died:** October 18, 2020, Ravenna, Seattle
- **Military:** World War II Army Troop Transport Command

#### Education
- University of Virginia Law School - law degree (1948)

#### Early Career
- Returned to Florida to practice law after graduation
- Chosen for state commission exploring transportation development and regulation

#### Religion
- Not documented in sources

#### Politics
- Not documented (served under LBJ, Democrat)

---

## AVIATION CAREER

### Civil Aeronautics Board
- 1959: Appointed by Eisenhower to Civil Aeronautics Board (CAB)
- Continued serving during Kennedy years
- Became CAB Chairman
- Helped ailing airline industry recover
- Standardized fare reductions
- Approved government subsidies for less profitable routes

### International Aviation Diplomacy
- Attended emergency meetings in London between major nations
- Gave daily press conferences asking why European leaders refused to pass aviation cost savings to citizens
- Prime Minister Harold Macmillan complained to JFK about Boyd
- Result: Boyd recalled to Washington and chastised by Kennedy

### LBJ Connection
- February 19, 1961: Private jet owned by LBJ (in wife's name) crashed at LBJ ranch
- This aviation connection may have led to subsequent federal posts

---

## CREATION OF DOT

### Architects
- **Charles Schultze:** Director of Bureau of the Budget
- **Joseph A. Califano Jr.:** Special Assistant to the President
- Both pushed for new department
- Urged Boyd to explore prospects for transportation department initiative

### Task Force
- Boyd created task force to study the issue
- October 22, 1965: Task force submitted recommendations advocating Department of Transportation

### Legislative Path
- January 1966: LBJ announced intention in State of the Union
- March 6, 1966: Johnson sent bill to Congress

---

## APPOINTMENT

### The Selection
- November 6, 1966: At Texas ranch, Johnson announced Boyd nomination
- Boyd was Under Secretary of Commerce at the time
- Johnson recalled moment occurred on Sunday after President returned from church

### LBJ's Assessment
- "We looked at business, we looked at labor, we looked at public officials, we looked at State commissions... And the one and only name that came up was Alan Boyd"

### Swearing In
- January 16, 1967

---

## AS SECRETARY (1967-1969)

### Focus Areas
- Airports
- Air traffic control system
- Automobile safety
- Driver education
- Alcoholism
- Highway beautification program (Lady Bird Johnson's pet project)

### Policy Proposals
Later adopted by Nixon administration:
- Aviation trust fund for airport and airway spending
- Increased federal commitment to mass transit

---

## LATER CAREER

- Illinois Central Railroad president (1969-1972)
- Amtrak president (until June 1982)
- Airbus Industrie of North America president
- 1994: Tony Jannus Award for contributions to commercial aviation
- 2009: Philip J. Klass Award for Lifetime Achievement (Aviation Week)
