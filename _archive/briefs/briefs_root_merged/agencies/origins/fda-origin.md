# ORIGIN STORY: FOOD AND DRUG ADMINISTRATION

## FOUNDING DATE
June 30, 1906 (Pure Food and Drug Act signed)
1930 (Name "Food and Drug Administration" adopted)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Pure Food and Drug Act of 1906

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### HARVEY WASHINGTON WILEY ("Father of the FDA")

#### Background
- **Born:** October 18, 1844, Kent, Indiana
- **Died:** June 30, 1930
- **Education:** Hanover College; Indiana Medical College (MD); Harvard (chemistry)
- **Career:** Professor; Indiana State Chemist; Chief Chemist, USDA Bureau of Chemistry

#### Religion
- **Methodist** - raised in devout Methodist household

#### Politics
- Progressive reformer; consumer protection advocate
- Worked across party lines for regulation

### Wiley's "Poison Squad"
- Early 1900s: Conducted controversial experiments
- Volunteers consumed food preservatives
- Studied effects on human health
- Built scientific case for regulation
- Generated public attention and support

## CREATION OF FDA

### The Problem
- Adulterated food widespread
- Patent medicines with dangerous ingredients (cocaine, opium)
- No labeling requirements
- No safety testing
- Interstate commerce spread hazards nationwide

### Pure Food and Drug Act (1906)
- Prohibited interstate transport of adulterated or misbranded food and drugs
- Required accurate labeling
- Established federal enforcement authority
- Enforcement by Bureau of Chemistry (USDA)

### Later Evolution
- **1930:** Bureau of Chemistry renamed Food and Drug Administration
- **1940:** FDA moved to Federal Security Agency
- **1953:** FDA moved to Department of Health, Education, and Welfare
- **1979:** FDA moved to Department of Health and Human Services

---

## FIRST "COMMISSIONER" EQUIVALENT

### HARVEY WILEY (Chief, Bureau of Chemistry)
- Led enforcement of Pure Food and Drug Act
- Served until 1912
- Resigned over disputes about enforcement rigor
- Continued advocacy outside government

---

## STRENGTHENING FDA

### 1938 Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act
- Passed after sulfanilamide disaster killed 100+ people
- Required safety testing before marketing drugs
- Expanded FDA authority significantly

### 1962 Kefauver-Harris Amendment
- Passed after thalidomide tragedy
- Required proof of efficacy (not just safety)
- Strengthened clinical trial requirements

---

## CONTEXT: PROGRESSIVE ERA

### Broader Reform Movement
- Antitrust enforcement (Sherman Act)
- Railroad regulation (ICC)
- Meat inspection
- Child labor laws
- Trust-busting

### Wiley's Legacy
- Established principle of federal consumer protection
- Science-based regulation
- FDA now regulates 25% of U.S. consumer spending
