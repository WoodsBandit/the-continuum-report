# ORIGIN STORY: DEPARTMENT OF HOUSING AND URBAN DEVELOPMENT

## FOUNDING DATE
September 9, 1965 (HUD established)
January 13, 1966 (First Secretary nominated)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Department of Housing and Urban Development Act

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### ROBERT C. WEAVER (First Secretary of HUD)

#### Background
- **Born:** December 29, 1907
- **Died:** July 17, 1997
- **Heritage:** Great-grandson of an enslaved man
- **Father:** Mortimer Weaver; worked for postal service
- **Mother:** Florence (n√©e Freeman); daughter of Robert Tanner Freeman, first African American to earn dental degree in U.S.

#### Education
- Harvard University: B.S. (1929), M.A. (1931), Ph.D. in economics (1934)
- Held more Harvard degrees than anyone else in Kennedy administration's upper ranks

#### Civil Rights Work
- Active in civil rights movement
- National Chairman of NAACP (one year term)
- 1960: Board chairman of NAACP when JFK sought his civil rights advice
- FDR administration: One of 45 prominent African Americans appointed; part of informal "Black Cabinet"

#### Religion
- Not documented in sources

#### Politics
- **Philosophy:** Civil rights advocate; housing equity champion
- **Career Path:** Rose through government positions focused on housing and urban issues

---

## HISTORIC SIGNIFICANCE

### First African American Cabinet Secretary
- January 13, 1966: Appointed by LBJ as first Black cabinet member in U.S. history
- "Reached the highest levels of the executive branch"
- Today, HUD headquarters in DC bears his name

---

## PATH TO CABINET

### JFK's Promise
- Kennedy intended to appoint Weaver when he proposed creating HUD
- JFK never got to fulfill promise (Congress didn't create HUD until 1965)

### LBJ's Hesitation
- Initially reluctant to appoint Weaver as Kennedy had promised
- Worried Southern congressmen would refuse to work with Weaver
- Many LBJ advisers had "little experience working with Black professionals"

### The Decision
- Bill Moyers rated Weaver highly on potential effectiveness
- Moyers noted Weaver's "strong accomplishments and ability to create teams"
- Johnson concluded Weaver was "best-qualified administrator"
- Passage of Voting Rights Act (1965) reduced obstacles from "Solid South"

### The Appointment
- January 13, 1966: Johnson finally appointed Weaver
- "Civil rights leaders and the Black press celebrated the monumental step"

---

## AS HUD SECRETARY (1966-1969)

### Key Achievement
- Championed the Fair Housing Act of 1968
- Prohibited housing discrimination based on race, color, religion, or national origin

### LBJ's Interest
- January 1965: In phone call with Dr. Martin Luther King Jr., Johnson expressed interest in making housing cabinet-level
- Explicitly wanted Weaver to serve as first secretary
