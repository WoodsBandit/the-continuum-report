# ORIGIN STORY: DEPARTMENT OF EDUCATION

## FOUNDING DATE
October 17, 1979 (bill signed)
May 4, 1980 (began operations)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Department of Education Organization Act

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### SHIRLEY HUFSTEDLER (First Secretary of Education)

#### Background
- **Born:** August 24, 1925, Denver, Colorado
- **Died:** March 30, 2016
- **Family:** Mother's side emigrated from Germany; Missouri pioneers
- **Father:** Worked in construction; family moved frequently during Great Depression
- **Childhood:** Lived in New Mexico, Montana, California, Wyoming; frequently changed schools

#### Education
- University of New Mexico
- Stanford University Law School - law degree

#### Judicial Career
- U.S. Circuit Judge, Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals (1968-1979)
- At appointment as Secretary, she was highest-ranking woman in U.S. federal judiciary
- Considered potential Supreme Court nominee under Carter

#### Religion
- Not documented in sources

#### Politics/Jurisprudence
- **Philosophy:** Liberal orientation
- **Focus Areas:** Civil rights, civil liberties, women's rights, First/Fourth/Fifth Amendment protections
- **Advocacy:** Broad access to justice

#### As Secretary
- December 6, 1979 - January 20, 1981
- No previous education policy experience
- Spent much time organizing new department during federal hiring freeze
- Worked to ensure strong Office for Civil Rights

---

### JIMMY CARTER (President)

#### Campaign Promise
- 1976: Promised National Education Association (NEA) he would push for separate education department
- NEA goal: Sought separate department for a century
- In return: NEA made first presidential endorsement in its 117-year history

#### Signing
- October 17, 1979: Signed bill in East Room of White House
- Within two weeks nominated Hufstedler

---

## POLITICAL CONTEXT

### Opposition
- **Conservative Lawmakers:** Concerned about bureaucratic intrusion into education
- **Some Liberals:** Worried creation would make getting additional federal aid more difficult
- Congress approved despite opposition from both sides

### Reagan's Response
- Carter defeated by Ronald Reagan (1980)
- Reagan early in term sought to downgrade new department to sub-Cabinet-level foundation
- Department survived and continues today

---

## WHY NO EDUCATION EXPERIENCE?

Carter chose federal judge with no education policy background because:
- Wanted strong administrator and legal mind
- Trusted her liberal civil rights orientation
- Valued her judicial reputation
- Believed organizational skills more important than education expertise for startup
