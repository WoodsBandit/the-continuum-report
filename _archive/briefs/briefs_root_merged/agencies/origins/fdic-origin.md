# ORIGIN STORY: FEDERAL DEPOSIT INSURANCE CORPORATION

## FOUNDING DATE
June 16, 1933 (Banking Act signed)
January 1, 1934 (Deposit insurance began)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Banking Act of 1933 (Glass-Steagall Act)

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### FRANKLIN D. ROOSEVELT (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** January 30, 1882, Hyde Park, New York
- **Died:** April 12, 1945
- **Career:** Governor of NY; President (1933-1945)

#### Religion
- **Episcopalian**

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democratic
- **Philosophy:** New Deal; federal intervention in economy

### FDR's Initial Opposition
- Roosevelt was personally dubious about deposit insurance
- Said: "We do not wish to make the United States Government liable for the mistakes and errors of individual banks, and put a premium on unsound banking in the future"
- Bankers also opposed; called it "socialist"
- **Public support was overwhelming** - FDR yielded to popular demand

## THE BANKING CRISIS

### Collapse of Banking System
- 1921-1929: Approximately 5,700 bank failures (concentrated in rural areas)
- 1929-1933: Nearly 10,000 additional failures (one-third of all U.S. banks)
- After 1929 crash: Bank runs became epidemic
- Series of panics in 1930-1931 turned recession into Great Depression

### March 1933 Crisis
- March 6, 1933: FDR declared national bank holiday
- All banks ordered temporarily closed
- March 12, 1933: FDR's first "Fireside Chat" reassured depositors
- Banks reopened March 13 with restored confidence

---

## CREATION OF FDIC

### The Banking Act of 1933
- June 16, 1933: FDR signed into law
- Part of New Deal relief programs
- Created FDIC as independent federal agency
- Also separated commercial and investment banking (Glass-Steagall provisions)

### Initial Insurance
- First national deposit insurance system
- Initially protected up to $2,500 per depositor
- Funded by bank assessments

---

## FIRST CHAIRMAN

### WALTER J. CUMMINGS (Temporary)
- Headed temporary deposit insurance fund
- January 1, 1934: Temporary insurance began

### LEO T. CROWLEY (First Permanent Chairman)
- Appointed 1934
- Wisconsin banker and New Deal official

---

## EXTRAORDINARY SUCCESS

### Perfect Record
- "Since its start in 1933 no depositor has ever lost a penny of FDIC-insured funds"
- Backed by full faith and credit of U.S. government
- Eliminated bank runs as existential threat to banking system

### Coverage Evolution
- 1934: $2,500 per depositor
- Today: $250,000 per depositor per institution
