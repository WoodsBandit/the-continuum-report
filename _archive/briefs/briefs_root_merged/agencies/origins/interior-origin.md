# ORIGIN STORY: DEPARTMENT OF THE INTERIOR

## FOUNDING DATE
March 3, 1849 (established)
March 8, 1849 (first Secretary sworn in)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Act of Congress creating the "Home Department"

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### JAMES K. POLK (President who signed creation)

One of Polk's last acts as president was signing the bill creating the Department of the Interior on March 3, 1849 - the first new cabinet position since the early days of the Republic.

---

### THOMAS EWING SR. (First Secretary of the Interior)

#### Background
- **Born:** December 28, 1789, West Liberty, Virginia (now West Virginia)
- **Died:** October 26, 1871
- **Education:** Ohio University, 1816
- **Legal Career:** Admitted to state bar; opened law practice in Lancaster, Ohio
- **Early Office:** Prosecuting attorney for Fairfield County (1818-1829)

#### Religion
- **Birth:** Born Presbyterian
- **Adult Life:** Attended Catholic services with family for many years
- **Death:** Formally baptized into Catholic faith during last illness
- **Family:** Married Maria Wills Boyle (Roman Catholic); raised children in her faith

#### Politics
- **Party:** Whig (joined 1833; remained Whig even when party collapsed)
- **Philosophy:** Conservative Whig
- **Prior Federal Service:**
  - U.S. Senator from Ohio (1830-1836)
  - Secretary of the Treasury under William Henry Harrison (1841)
  - Resigned under Tyler administration

#### Selection
- Lost 1848 Senate bid
- Newly inaugurated President Zachary Taylor nominated Ewing for Secretary of new "Home Department"

#### Tenure
- March 8, 1849 to July 22, 1850
- Served under Taylor and briefly under Millard Fillmore

---

### REMARKABLE FAMILY LEGACY

Thomas Ewing Sr. fostered an extraordinary military family:

- **William Tecumseh Sherman:** Foster son; later famous Union general; married Ewing's daughter Ellen
- **Thomas Ewing Jr.:** Son; Union army general; two-term U.S. Congressman from Ohio
- **Hugh Boyle Ewing:** Son; Union general during Civil War
- **Charles Ewing:** Son; Union general during Civil War

---

## ORIGINAL MISSION

The Interior Department consolidated domestic affairs scattered across government:
- Public lands
- Indian affairs (from War Department)
- Patent Office (from State Department)
- Census
- Pensions

Called the "Home Department" to distinguish from State Department's foreign affairs.
