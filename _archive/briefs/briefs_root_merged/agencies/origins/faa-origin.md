## THE CATALYST: 1956 GRAND CANYON MID-AIR COLLISION

### The Disaster
- June 30, 1956: United Airlines DC-7 and TWA Lockheed Constellation collided
- Occurred over Grand Canyon, Arizona
- 128 people killed (all aboard both aircraft)
- Worst aviation disaster in U.S. history at the time

### The Problem Exposed
- Inadequate air traffic control system
- Overlapping and fragmented authority
- Civil Aeronautics Administration (CAA) underfunded
- Military and civilian airspace poorly coordinated
- Growing jet age made crisis urgent

---

## CREATION OF FAA

### Federal Aviation Act of 1958
- August 23, 1958: Eisenhower signed into law
- Created **Federal Aviation Agency** (independent agency)
- Unified control over civil aviation and airspace
- Combined functions from CAA and other agencies

### 1967 Reorganization
- Department of Transportation created
- FAA placed under DOT
- Renamed **Federal Aviation Administration**
- Lost independent agency status

---

## FIRST ADMINISTRATOR

### ELWOOD RICHARD "PETE" QUESADA

#### Background
- **Born:** April 13, 1904, Washington, D.C.
- **Died:** February 9, 1993
- **Military:** Lieutenant General, U.S. Air Force (retired)
- Close friend of President Eisenhower

#### Religion
- **Roman Catholic**

#### Politics
- Republican; Eisenhower loyalist
- Nonpartisan in aviation matters

### Quesada's Leadership
- First FAA Administrator (1958-1961)
- Strong, decisive leader
- Pushed for significant safety improvements
- Mandated flight recorders ("black boxes")
- Stricter pilot training requirements
- Set tone for safety-first culture

---

## WHAT FAA UNIFIED

### Predecessor Agency Functions
- Civil Aeronautics Administration (CAA) - within Commerce Department
- Airways Modernization Board
- Air navigation facilities operation
- Air traffic control
- Pilot certification
- Aircraft certification

### New Authority
- Complete authority over U.S. airspace
- Safety regulation of civil aviation
- Air traffic management
- Airport development

---

## CONTEXT: JET AGE

### Aviation Revolution
- 1958: Boeing 707 entered commercial service
- Jet aircraft faster, flew higher
- More passengers, higher stakes
- Old regulatory system couldn't keep pace
- Public demanded safer skies

---

## MISSION TODAY

### Responsibilities
- Air traffic control (busiest airspace in world)
- Aviation safety regulation
- Pilot and aircraft certification
- Airport development grants
- Aerospace research

### Scale
- Manages 45,000+ flights daily
- Employs approximately 45,000
