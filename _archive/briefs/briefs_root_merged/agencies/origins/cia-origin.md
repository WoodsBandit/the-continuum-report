# ORIGIN STORY: CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE AGENCY

## FOUNDING DATE
September 18, 1947 (National Security Act)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
National Security Act of 1947

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### WILLIAM J. "WILD BILL" DONOVAN (Father of American Intelligence)

#### Background
- **Position:** Recognized as "Father of American Intelligence"
- **Education:** Columbia Law School, 1908 graduate (classmate of FDR)
- **Legal Career:** U.S. Attorney, assistant to chief Nuremberg prosecutor, assistant to U.S. Attorney General, renowned Supreme Court advocate
- **Business:** Founder of powerhouse Wall Street law firm
- **Military:** Highly decorated WWI veteran

#### Role in Intelligence
- July 1941: Appointed by FDR as Coordinator of Information (COI)
- June 1942: Office rechristened Office of Strategic Services (OSS) with Donovan at head
- OSS conducted wartime intelligence and unconventional warfare operations
- 1945: Truman disbanded OSS against Donovan's urgings
- 1947: When CIA created, Truman passed over Donovan for directorship

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Philosophy:** Believed in centralized intelligence capability for national security

---

### ALLEN WELSH DULLES (First Civilian Director)

#### Background
- **Born:** April 7, 1893, Watertown, New York
- **Family:** Son of Presbyterian minister; grandson of a Secretary of State
- **Upbringing:** Household that valued public service
- **Education:** Princeton University (1916); George Washington University Law (1926)
- **Legal Career:** Partner at Sullivan & Cromwell (brother John Foster Dulles was also partner)
- **Diplomatic:** Entered diplomatic service after Princeton

#### Religion
- **Presbyterian** (father was Presbyterian minister)

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Philosophy:** Active in Republican politics in 1930s
- **Cold War View:** May 1941 - urged U.S. to enter WWII out of "enlightened selfishness"
- **Approach:** Saw clandestine activities as essential part of struggle against communism, "even if it did not always comply with the law" (per SourceWatch characterization of documented CIA operations under his leadership)

#### Role in CIA
- October 1941: Recruited by Donovan to OSS
- June 27, 1947: Briefed Congressional Committee considering CIA formation
- Adviser to Congress as it drew up National Security Act of 1947
- Member of "Georgetown Set" of Cold War liberals involved in CIA founding
- First civilian Director of Central Intelligence; longest-serving director

#### Notable Operations (as DCI)
- 1953 Iranian coup d'etat
- 1954 Guatemalan coup d'etat
- Project MKUltra mind control program
- Bay of Pigs Invasion (1961)

---

### HARRY S. TRUMAN (President)

#### Role
- Disbanded OSS in September 1945
- Signed National Security Act of 1947 creating CIA
- Passed over Donovan for first CIA directorship
- Appointed Rear Admiral Roscoe H. Hillenkoetter as first Director of Central Intelligence

---

## POLITICAL CONTEXT

### From OSS to CIA
- OSS proved value of centralized intelligence during WWII
- Truman initially skeptical of peacetime intelligence agency
- Cold War tensions with Soviet Union changed calculus
- National Security Act of 1947 created both CIA and National Security Council

### Institutional Design
- CIA designed as civilian agency (unlike military intelligence)
- Director reports to President through National Security Council
- Explicitly prohibited from domestic law enforcement
