# ORIGIN STORY: UNITED STATES MARSHALS SERVICE

## FOUNDING DATE
September 24, 1789

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Judiciary Act of 1789

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### GEORGE WASHINGTON (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** February 22, 1732, Westmoreland County, Virginia
- **Died:** December 14, 1799
- **Military:** Commander-in-Chief, Continental Army
- **Career:** Surveyor; planter; Commander; first President

#### Religion
- **Episcopalian** (Anglican/Church of England)
- Vestryman in his parish; attended church but rarely took communion
- Avoided sectarian religious language; often used "Providence"

#### Politics
- **Party:** None (warned against factions)
- **Philosophy:** Federalist in practice; strong central government; republican virtue

---

## AMERICA'S FIRST FEDERAL LAW ENFORCEMENT

### The Judiciary Act
- September 24, 1789: Washington signed Judiciary Act into law
- Created federal court system
- Established "Office of the United States Marshal"
- **First federal law enforcement agency in American history**

### Why Marshals Were Needed
- Constitution created no regional administrative structure
- No agency to represent federal interests at local level
- Courts needed enforcement arm
- Federal government needed "strong right arm"

---

## THE FIRST MARSHALS

### Washington's Appointments
- September 26, 1789: Washington signed commissions of thirteen Marshals
- One marshal for each federal judicial district
- October 1-2, 1789: President dispatched most commissions

### Selection Criteria
- Washington sought men with "strong local ties"
- Deep commitment to new nation
- Eight of first 16 were life-long residents of their districts
- Seven had lived there more than five years

### Backgrounds
- At least two had law enforcement experience (sheriff; Admiralty marshal)
- One trained physician
- Others: farmers, merchants, businessmen, lawyers, politicians
- Most corresponded with Washington; many "called him friend"
- Most owed appointments to previous associations with Washington
- Many served under his command during Revolutionary War

---

## ORIGINAL DUTIES

### Court Officers
- Served writs, subpoenas, and warrants
- Managed federal jails
- Secured courtrooms
- Enforced orders of federal courts

### Visible Federal Authority
- Most visible representation of federal authority in young republic
- Only federal officials in many communities
- Enforced federal law where no other mechanism existed

---

## HISTORICAL SIGNIFICANCE

### Continuous Service
- Oldest federal law enforcement agency
- Continuous service since 1789
- Over 235 years of operation

### Evolution of Role
- Western expansion: enforced law in territories
- Civil War: served both Union and (briefly) Confederacy
- Reconstruction: protected civil rights
- Modern era: fugitive apprehension, witness protection, court security

---

## NOTABLE EARLY MARSHALS

### First Generation
- Chosen for loyalty to new nation
- Proven in time of war (Revolution)
- Strong community ties ensured respect for states
- Washington's successors continued this appointment pattern
