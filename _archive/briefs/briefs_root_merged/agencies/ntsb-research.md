# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: NATIONAL TRANSPORTATION SAFETY BOARD

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The NTSB is an independent agency investigating transportation accidents across all modes, established April 1, 1967. With ~$145M budget and ~400 staff, it determines probable causes and issues safety recommendations. Has NO enforcement authority; relies on advocacy to effect change.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** National Transportation Safety Board
**Founding:** April 1, 1967 (in DOT); independent April 1, 1975
**Current Chair:** Jennifer Homendy (15th Chair, since August 2021)
**Headquarters:** 490 L'Enfant Plaza SW, Washington, DC 20594
**Regional Offices:** Anchorage, Aurora (CO), Federal Way (WA)

---

## 2. BUDGET

**FY2024:** $145 million ($15.7M increase)
**FY2025:** Budget request submitted March 2024
**Staff:** ~400 employees

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**5 Board Members:** Presidential appointees, 5-year terms, max 3 same party
- At least 3 must have technical transportation safety qualifications
- Chair and Vice Chair: 3-year terms

**Modal Offices:**
- Office of Aviation Safety
- Office of Highway Safety
- Office of Marine Safety
- Office of Railroad, Pipeline & Hazardous Materials
- Office of Research and Engineering

**Support Offices:**
- Safety Recommendations and Communications
- Administrative Law Judges
- General Counsel

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Investigation Authority
- Civil aviation accidents
- Select highway crashes
- Ship/marine accidents
- Pipeline incidents
- Railroad accidents
- Hazardous materials releases

### Safety Recommendations
- 15,500+ issued to 2,470+ recipients
- ~82% historical implementation rate
- DOT agencies must respond within 90 days

### Most Wanted List (1990-2023)
- 422 safety improvements identified
- Discontinued end of 2023 after 35 years

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Boeing 737 MAX Investigations
- Lion Air/Ethiopian crashes (2018-2019): 346 deaths; MCAS system failures
- Alaska Airlines door plug blowout (January 2024): 19 new recommendations
- Boeing sanctioned for disclosing non-public information
- "Few entities know the rules better than Boeing"

### Tesla Autopilot Investigations
- Multiple fatal crashes (2016-2020)
- Findings: Ineffective driver monitoring, system used outside design domain
- Tesla failed to respond to recommendations for 4+ years
- NHTSA blamed for regulatory laxity
- 65+ reported fatalities through October 2025

### East Palestine Derailment (February 2023)
- Norfolk Southern train derailed 38 cars; 11 with hazardous materials
- "Vent and burn" decision found "not necessary"
- Norfolk Southern obstruction: Chair threatened subpoenas twice
- "Unprecedented and reprehensible" abuse of party process
- 34 new recommendations issued

### No Enforcement Authority
- Cannot compel adoption of recommendations
- Cannot impose fines or penalties
- Cannot investigate criminal activity
- Effectiveness depends entirely on advocacy quality

---

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- FAA: Aviation safety; NTSB appellate role for certificate actions
- FHWA: Highway safety enforcement
- FRA: Railroad safety enforcement
- Coast Guard: Marine safety; MOU for casualty investigations
- NHTSA: Vehicle safety; NTSB recommendations often ignored

**Unique Role:** Court of appeal for FAA and Coast Guard certificate suspensions/revocations
