# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: OFFICE OF PERSONNEL MANAGEMENT

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

OPM is the independent agency managing federal civil service, established January 1, 1979. With ~$1.35B budget and 2,246 FTE, it administers USAJobs, retirement (FERS/CSRS), and health benefits (FEHB) for 9M+ beneficiaries. Central to Schedule F controversy.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Office of Personnel Management
**Founding:** January 1, 1979 (Civil Service Reform Act of 1978)
**Predecessor:** U.S. Civil Service Commission (1883-1979)
**Current Director:** Scott Kupor (July 14, 2025)
**Headquarters:** Washington, DC

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### USAJobs
- Official federal employment portal
- Centralized job search and application
- Government's primary recruitment mechanism

### Retirement Systems
- CSRS: Pre-1984 employees (legacy)
- FERS: Post-1984 employees (includes TSP)

### Federal Employees Health Benefits (FEHB)
- Established 1959
- Largest employer-sponsored health insurance globally
- 9+ million beneficiaries
- Government pays 72-75% of premiums

### Security Clearances (Transferred 2019)
- NBIB transferred to Defense Counterintelligence and Security Agency
- ~3,300 OPM employees transferred
- DCSA now conducts ~95% of background investigations

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### 2015 Data Breach
- First disclosure (June 2015): 4.2M records
- Second disclosure (July 2015): 21.5M records
- Combined: 25.7M records; 22.1M individuals
- Data: SSNs, fingerprints, SF-86 forms, mental health records
- Attribution: China (Jiangsu State Security Department)
- Aftermath: Director Archuleta resigned

### Schedule F/Schedule Policy-Career (2025)
- EO 14171 (January 20, 2025): "Restoring Accountability"
- Scope: 50,000-200,000 positions potentially affected
- Record opposition: 40,500 comments; 94% opposed
- Constitutional claims: Unilateral presidential authority
- NTEU lawsuit: January 21, 2025
- Removes civil service protections, notice/appeal rights
- Threatens whistleblower protections
