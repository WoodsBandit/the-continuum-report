# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: BUREAU OF INDIAN AFFAIRS

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

BIA is the oldest federal agency in continuous existence (1824), managing trust responsibilities to 574 federally recognized tribes. With ~$2.5B budget and trust assets of $8B+, it provides services to 2.5 million American Indians and Alaska Natives. Faces boarding school legacy, trust fund mismanagement history, and chronic underfunding.

### Trust Land & Natural Resources

- Forestry, irrigation, safety of dams
- Environmental services

### Tribal Services
- Tribal Government Services Division
- Social services programs
- Economic development

### Financial Trust Management
- ~300,000 Individual Indian Monies (IIM) accounts

### Law Enforcement (OJS)
- 208 law enforcement programs

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Boarding School System
- 526+ government-funded schools (19th-20th centuries)
- At least 973 verified child deaths
- Sexual, physical, emotional abuse documented
- Forbidden from speaking Native languages
- Federal Indian Boarding School Initiative (2021)
- Presidential apology (October 2024)
- Vatican apology (June 2024)

### Trust Fund Mismanagement
- Documented since 1980s; worsened through 2000s
- ~300,000 IIM accounts affected
- $2+ billion in trust assets mismanaged
- 1989 Senate Report: "fraud, corruption and mismanagement"
- Cobell v. Salazar: $3.4 billion settlement (2009)
- Acknowledged "century-long federal bungling"

### Education Underfunding
- Chronic underfunding of BIE schools
- Bureaucratic delays in fund processing
- Treaty-mandated education treated as discretionary
- TCUs dramatically underfunded
