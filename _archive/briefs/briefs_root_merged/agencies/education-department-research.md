# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: U.S. DEPARTMENT OF EDUCATION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The U.S. Department of Education (ED), established in 1979, is the federal government's primary education agency. With approximately 4,100 employees and a FY2024 budget of $268 billion, it administers $1.6 trillion in federal student loans, K-12 funding, and special education services. The department faces proposed elimination under the Trump administration.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** United States Department of Education (ED)

**Founding Date:** October 17, 1979; operations began May 1980

**Current Secretary:** Linda McMahon (sworn in March 3, 2025, 13th Secretary)

**Headquarters:** Lyndon Baines Johnson Building, 400 Maryland Avenue, SW, Washington, DC 20202

**Workforce:** ~4,100 employees (smallest Cabinet-level agency)

---

## 2. BUDGET & APPROPRIATIONS

**FY2024:** $268 billion
**FY2025:** Continuing Resolution at FY2024 levels

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

### Statutorily-Established Offices
- Federal Student Aid (FSA) - $1.6 trillion loan portfolio
- Office of Elementary and Secondary Education (OESE)
- Office of Special Education and Rehabilitative Services (OSERS)
- Office for Civil Rights (OCR)
- Institute of Education Sciences (IES)

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

- Federal Student Loans: $1.6 trillion, 43 million borrowers
- Pell Grants: $31 billion to 6.5 million students
- Title I K-12 Funding: 26 million students
- IDEA Special Education: 8+ million students

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Student Loan Crisis
- Total debt: $1.797 trillion
- 42.5 million affected borrowers
- For-profit colleges: 44% of defaults, 22% of borrowers

### Title IX Enforcement Turmoil
- 2024 Final Rule vacated by federal court
- Conflicting enforcement across states

### Proposed Elimination (2025)
- Executive order to abolish ED
- Requires Congressional action
- ~50% workforce has left or been terminated

---

## 6. INTERAGENCY RELATIONSHIPS

- HHS: Head Start coordination, special education transfer proposed
- Labor: Workforce development, proposed program transfers
- Justice: Civil rights enforcement
- Interior: Indian Education programs
