# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: UNITED STATES MARSHALS SERVICE

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

USMS is the oldest federal law enforcement agency (1789), operating as DOJ's enforcement arm for federal courts. With ~$4B budget and 5,500 employees, it operates 94 district offices, runs fugitive task forces, administers WITSEC, and manages federal prisoner transport. Faces use-of-force and asset forfeiture controversies.

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Fugitive Operations
- Principal mission; Regional Fugitive Task Forces
- Great Lakes RFTF: 117,700+ apprehended since 2003
- International operations including Jamaica FAT

### WITSEC (Witness Security)
- Established 1971
- 19,250+ witnesses/families protected
- No compliant participant ever harmed/killed
- 90% conviction rate with WITSEC testimony

### Asset Forfeiture
- Primary DOJ disbursing agency
- 30,000+ annual payments exceeding $500M
- Real estate, cash, vehicles, jewelry, aircraft

### JPATS (Prisoner Transport)
- Largest prison transport network globally
- 265,000 prisoner movements annually
- Four full-sized aircraft

### Judicial Security
- 5,000+ court security officers
- 400+ federal court facilities
- Protects judges, jurors, proceedings

### Tactical Operations
- Special Operations Group (SOG) since 1971
- Nation's oldest federal tactical unit
- FY2024: 16,518 hours in high-threat situations

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Use of Force
- 147 shooting incidents (2019-2021)
- Average 31 people shot per year; 22 killed
- No de-escalation requirement
- No federal body camera mandate
- DOJ refuses to release shooting information

### Asset Forfeiture Concerns
- Critics: Violates 4th/5th Amendment rights
- 50% of victims not convicted (Baltimore Sun, 2012)
- "Perverse financial incentives"
- Culley v. Marshall (2024): SCOTUS upheld practices

### Accountability Issues
- GAO: Federal agencies should improve shooting review
- Former DOJ IG: "Remarkable that feds aren't policing their own"
