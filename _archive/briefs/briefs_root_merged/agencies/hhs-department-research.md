# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: U.S. DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES (HHS)

## I. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** United States Department of Health and Human Services

**Founding Date:** April 11, 1953 (as HEW; renamed October 17, 1979)

**Headquarters:** Hubert H. Humphrey Building, Washington, D.C.

**Current Secretary:** Robert F. Kennedy Jr. (confirmed February 13, 2025; 26th Secretary)

**Status:** Largest civilian federal department (~28% of federal outlays)

---

## II. BUDGET ANALYSIS

**FY2025 Estimated Outlays:** $1.802 trillion
**FY2024 Estimated Outlays:** $1.670 trillion
**Year-over-Year Growth:** +$132 billion (+8%)

**Budget Composition:**
- Medicare & Medicaid: 85% of outlays (mandatory)
- Discretionary: ~9% of outlays

---

## III. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

### Major Operating Divisions (11 total)

1. **CDC** - Disease prevention, immunization, public health research
2. **FDA** - Drug approval, food safety, medical devices
3. **NIH** - Biomedical research (largest global medical research funder)
4. **CMS** - Medicare, Medicaid, CHIP
5. **HRSA** - Health workforce, vulnerable populations
6. **IHS** - Indian Health Service
7. **SAMHSA** - Addiction and mental health
8. **ACF** - Administration for Children and Families
9. **ACL** - Administration for Community Living (planned dissolution)
10. **AHRQ** - Healthcare research
11. **ATSDR** - Toxic substances

### 2025 Reorganization (RFK Jr.)
- Reduction from 28 to 15 top-level divisions
- "Administration for a Healthy America" (merging 5 agencies)
- CDC reoriented toward infectious disease
- 20% workforce reduction target (~20,000 positions)

---

## IV. MISSION STATEMENT

"To enhance the health and well-being of all Americans by providing effective health and human services and by fostering sound, sustained advances in the sciences underlying medicine, public health, and social services."

---

## V. KEY PROGRAMS

### Healthcare Coverage (CMS)
- **Medicare:** Insurance for 65+ and disabled
- **Medicaid:** Joint federal-state for low-income (83 million FY2024)
- **CHIP:** Children's health insurance

### Public Health (CDC)
- Infectious disease surveillance
- Immunization programs
- Chronic disease prevention
- Emergency response

### Drug Approval (FDA)
- Drug licensing and approval
- Medical device evaluation
- Food safety oversight

### Medical Research (NIH)
- Biomedical research funding
- Vaccine development
- Disease-specific research institutes

---

## VI. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### COVID-19 Response
- Opioid crisis exacerbation during pandemic
- Supply chain disruptions
- Vaccine recommendation debates

### Vaccine Program Controversies (2025)
- RFK Jr. fired all ACIP members, replaced with vaccine skeptics
- $500 million cut from mRNA vaccine development
- CDC website modified to include vaccine-autism claims
- COVID vaccine delisted for healthy children, pregnant women

### Drug Pricing
- 350+ medications with 2026 price increases planned
- Pfizer: ~80 price increases (some 15%)
- Pharmaceutical user fee elimination under consideration ($3.3B annually)

### FDA Approval Changes
- Proposed delays in vaccine approval requiring additional data
- Career staff recommendations overridden
- Novavax approval deviation from historical practices
