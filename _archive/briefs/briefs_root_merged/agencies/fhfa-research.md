# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: FEDERAL HOUSING FINANCE AGENCY

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The FHFA is an independent agency regulating housing GSEs (Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac, 11 FHLBanks), established July 30, 2008. Self-funded through entity assessments, it oversees $8+ trillion in mortgage market funding. GSEs remain in 16+ year conservatorship; affordable housing goals reduced in 2025.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Federal Housing Finance Agency
**Founding:** July 30, 2008 (Housing and Economic Recovery Act)
**Current Director:** Sandra L. Thompson (June 22, 2022)
**Status:** Independent federal agency

---

## 2. BUDGET/FUNDING

**Funding Source:** No Congressional appropriations; self-funded through assessments on regulated entities (12 CFR Part 1206)
**Regulated Entities:** Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac, 11 Federal Home Loan Banks, Office of Finance
**Assessment Coverage:** Examination, compliance monitoring, housing goal verification, new product reviews

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**Single Director:** Presidential appointee, 5-year term; also chairs Federal Housing Oversight Board

**Constitutional Issue:** Collins v. Yellen (2023) - 7-2 ruling found structure violates separation of powers (for-cause removal protection unconstitutional)

**Key Divisions:**
- Division of Enterprise Regulation
- Division of Housing Mission and Goals
- Division of Federal Home Loan Bank Regulation
- Division of Conservatorship Oversight and Readiness
- Division of Accounting and Financial Standards
- Office of Inspector General

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### GSE Conservatorship (Since September 6, 2008)
- Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac placed in conservatorship
- FHFA exercises all management, board, shareholder powers
- Treasury provides financial support via SPSPAs
- Combined GSEs: $8.1-8.4 trillion in mortgage market funding

### Federal Home Loan Banks Oversight
- Supervises 11 FHLBanks and Office of Finance
- FHLBanks provide liquidity to ~80% of U.S. lending institutions
- Members: Thrifts, commercial banks, credit unions, insurance companies, CDFIs

### Affordable Housing Goals
- Congressional mandate (1992) for low/moderate-income lending
- Subject to periodic rulemakings

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Perpetual Conservatorship
- 16+ years with no statutory end date
- FHFA: Continues until Congress passes housing finance legislation
- Critics: "Perpetual conservatorship was never contemplated under HERA"
- Legal scholars: Exit necessary to reduce government influence

### Net Worth Sweep (Third Amendment, 2012)
- Treasury receives all GSE net worth exceeding $3B capital reserve
- GSEs paid $301B+ in dividends (exceeds 2008 rescue costs)
- Prevents capital accumulation to exit conservatorship
- Fifth Circuit: Sweep exceeds FHFA conservatorship authority
- 2024: Jury awarded shareholders $612.4M for breach of implied covenant

### 2025-2026 Housing Goals Reduction
- Single-Family Low-Income: 25% → 21%
- Very Low-Income: 6% → 3.5%
- Refinance Goal: 26% → 21%
- Minority Census Tract Subgoal: Eliminated (race-neutral approach)
- National Urban League: "Will erase ability to evaluate how effectively GSEs serve communities of color"
- MBA: Welcomed as "constructive" and sustainable
