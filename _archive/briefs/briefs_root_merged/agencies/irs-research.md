# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: INTERNAL REVENUE SERVICE

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The IRS is the federal tax collection agency within Treasury, established July 1, 1862. With $12.3B annual appropriation (plus IRA funding), it processes 150M+ individual returns annually. Faces unprecedented 2025 leadership turnover, 52% IRA funding reduction ($78.9B → $37.6B), and legacy system modernization crisis.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Internal Revenue Service (IRS)
**Founding:** July 1, 1862 (as Office of Commissioner of Internal Revenue)
**Commissioner:** Billy Long (confirmed June 2025); Treasury Secretary Bessent as acting (August 2025)
**Parent Agency:** Department of the Treasury
**Headquarters:** 1111 Constitution Avenue NW, Washington, D.C.

---

## 2. BUDGET

**Annual Congressional Appropriation:** $12.32 billion (FY2023-2025)
**FY2024 Total:** ~$19.3 billion (including $7.2B IRA drawdown)
**FY2025 Total:** ~$22.8 billion (including $9.3B IRA drawdown)

**Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) Funding Crisis:**
- Original Authorization (2022): $78.9 billion over 10 years
- First Rescission (2023): -$1.4 billion
- Second Rescission (2024): -$20.2 billion
- Third Rescission (2025): -$20.2 billion
- FY2026 Proposed: Additional -$11.7 billion
- **Current Remaining:** ~$37.6 billion (52% reduction)
- **Enforcement Funding:** $3.8B remaining (83% cut from $45.6B)

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**Executive Leadership:**
- Commissioner (reports to Treasury Secretary)
- Deputy Commissioner
- Chief Counsel

**Four Primary Operating Divisions:**

1. **Wage & Investment (W&I)/Taxpayer Services**
   - Individual taxpayers, return processing, refunds
   - 150+ million individual returns annually

2. **Small Business/Self-Employed (SB/SE)**
   - Small business and self-employed taxpayers

3. **Large Business and International (LB&I)**
   - Businesses with $10M+ assets
   - Global High Wealth compliance

4. **Tax Exempt and Government Entities (TE/GE)**
   - Employee benefit plans, exempt organizations
   - 501(c)(3), 501(c)(4) oversight

**Specialized Division:**
- Criminal Investigation (CI): 90%+ federal conviction rate

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Tax Collection & Compliance
- Individual, corporate, excise, estate tax administration
- International tax compliance and transfer pricing

### Enforcement Programs
- Legal Source Tax Crimes
- Illegal Source Financial Crimes
- Public Corruption investigations
- International Tax Avoidance
- Healthcare Fraud (multi-agency)
- Cybercrimes Program (cryptocurrency, dark web)

### High-Income Enforcement Initiative
- Directive: 8% audit rate for $10M+ income individuals
- January 2024: $520 million recovered from millionaires with tax debt

### Taxpayer Services
- Electronic and paper return processing
- VITA, TCE, LITC programs

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### IRA Funding Cuts (2023-2025)
- Original $80 billion reduced to $37.6 billion (52%)
- Enforcement funding: $45.6B → $3.8B (83% reduction)
- Republican-led Congress eliminated virtually all high-income enforcement funding
- Taxpayer Services funding projected to deplete by FY2025

### Audit Rate Inequality
- January 2023: Black taxpayers audited at 4.7x rate of non-Black taxpayers
- IRS acknowledged algorithmic bias in May 2024
- Required 8% audit rate for $10M+ earners remains problematic

### Political Targeting (2009-2013 Tea Party Controversy)
- IRS subjected conservative 501(c)(4) organizations to excessive scrutiny
- Keywords: "Tea Party," "Patriot," "9/12" triggered extra review
- 292 conservative groups vs. 6 progressive groups selected
- No criminal charges; settlements paid to affected organizations

### System Modernization Crisis
- Legacy systems: 63% of IRS IT infrastructure
- March 2025: All 23 modernization programs paused
- June 2025: New framework with only 9 initiatives (reduced scope)
- No legacy systems decommissioned despite efforts
- ~50 million paper returns + 135 million correspondence pieces annually
- IT brain drain from hiring freezes

### Leadership Turnover (2025)
- Seven commissioners served throughout 2025
- Unprecedented instability
