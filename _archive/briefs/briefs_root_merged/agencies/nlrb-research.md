# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: NATIONAL LABOR RELATIONS BOARD

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The NLRB is an independent agency enforcing labor rights under the National Labor Relations Act of 1935. With ~$300M budget and 26 regional offices, it oversees union elections and unfair labor practice complaints. Faces unprecedented 2025 restructuring under Trump administration.

---

**Current Chair:** James D. Murphy (Republican, December 2025)

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**5-Member Board:** Presidential appointees, 5-year staggered terms, max 3 same party

**Bifurcated Leadership:**
- Board: Quasi-judicial decisions on formal records
- General Counsel: Independent 4-year term, prosecutes ULP cases

**Regional Operations:**
- 26 regional offices nationwide
- Administrative Law Judges in DC, NY, San Francisco

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Union Elections & Representation
- Supervises secret ballot elections
- Types: RC (Certification), RD (Decertification), RM (Representation)
- Requires 30% employee interest to file

### Unfair Labor Practice Complaints
- 20,000-30,000 charges per year
- Common violations: Threats, interrogations, unlawful discipline
- Employers charged in 41.5% of union campaigns

### Remedial Authority
- Make-whole remedies (reinstatement, back pay)
- Section 10(j) injunctions through District Court
- No penalty authority

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### 2025 Trump Administration Shakeup
- January 27: Fired Board member Wilcox and GC Abruzzo
- February 14: 29 prior GC memos rescinded/under review
- March 6: Federal judge ordered Wilcox reinstatement
- March 28: Appeals court granted stay, removed Wilcox
- December 18: New Republican majority confirmed

### Amazon/Starbucks Unionization
- 250+ open NLRB cases against Amazon
- 741 open/settled cases against Starbucks
- ~400 Starbucks locations voted to unionize
- Zero collective bargaining agreements achieved
- Corporate "strategy of infinite delay"

### Joint Employer Rule
- Biden (October 2023): Expanded to "possessing or reserving authority"
- March 2024: Texas court struck down as "unlawfully broad"
- July 2024: NLRB withdrew appeal
- 2020 employer-friendly rule now applicable

### Constitutional Challenges
- SpaceX, Amazon, Trader Joe's, Starbucks challenging NLRB authority
- Arguments that agency structure is unconstitutional
