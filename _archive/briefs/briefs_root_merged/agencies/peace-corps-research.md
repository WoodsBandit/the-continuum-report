# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: PEACE CORPS

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Peace Corps is an independent federal agency promoting world peace through volunteer service, established March 1, 1961. With ~$400-430M budget, it operates in 60+ countries with 3,300+ volunteers. Faces volunteer safety/sexual assault response failures, COVID evacuation recovery (50% of pre-pandemic levels), and ongoing medical care concerns.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Peace Corps (independent U.S. federal agency)
**Founding:** March 1, 1961 (Executive Order 10924 by JFK); September 22, 1961 (Peace Corps Act)
**First Director:** Sargent Shriver
**Headquarters:** Washington, D.C.
**Mission:** Promote world peace and friendship through trained volunteers
**Historical Service:** 240,000+ Americans in 144 countries since 1961

---

## 2. BUDGET

**Recent Range:** $400-430 million annually
**COVID Supplemental:** $88 million (P.L. 116-136) for evacuation
**Status:** Independent agency budget; check Congress.gov for FY2024-2025 specifics

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**Leadership:**
- Director (presidential appointee, Senate confirmed)
- Deputy Director

**Three Regions (Office of Global Operations):**
1. Africa Region
2. Europe, Mediterranean, and Asia Region
3. Inter-America and Pacific Region

**Country-Level:**
- Country Directors manage individual posts
- Regional Directors oversee country directors
- Supporting staff: Management, Programming, Training

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Peace Corps Volunteer (Standard)
- 27 months total (3 training + 24 service)
- Housing, living allowance, medical/dental provided
- $10,000+ readjustment allowance
- Graduate school benefits, federal hiring preferences

### Six Work Sectors
1. Agriculture - Food security, climate adaptation
2. Health - Nutrition, maternal/child health, HIV/AIDS
3. Education - English, literacy, math, science
4. Community Economic Development
5. Environment - Conservation
6. Youth in Development

### Peace Corps Response
- Short-term, high-impact assignments
- Skilled professionals for rapid deployment

### Virtual Service Pilot
- Remote service opportunities

### Coverage
- 60+ countries
- 3,337 volunteers (September 2024)

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Sexual Assault Response Failures
- 1 in 3 volunteers (2019) experienced sexual assault (double 2015 rate)
- Misrepresentation of assault reports
- Failing to explain forensic exam options
- Staff ignoring known threats
- Secondary trauma inflicted on victims
- Legislative Response: Peace Corps Volunteer Protection Act (2011)
- Current Law: 22 USC 2507b mandates Sexual Assault Response Liaison
- SAPR Program launched 2013

### COVID-19 Evacuation (2020)
- Complete evacuation of all volunteers
- $88 million supplemental appropriation
- Staggered return began 2022
- September 2024: 3,337 volunteers (~50% pre-pandemic)
- Slot fill rate: 45% (down from 94% in 2019)

### Ongoing Challenges
- Medical care and physical safety in operating environments
- Geographic and staffing recovery from pandemic

---

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- Independent Agency: Maintains separation from foreign policy objectives
- USAID: Different focus (large-scale development vs. volunteer service)
- State Department: Embassy coordination for logistics/safety; operational independence maintained
- Host Countries: Volunteers integrate into local communities
