# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: U.S. CENSUS BUREAU

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Census Bureau is the nation's statistical agency, established March 6, 1902. With ~$1.4B budget and 4,000+ employees, it conducts the decennial census, ACS, and 130+ economic surveys. Faces 2020 citizenship question aftermath, differential privacy controversy, political interference concerns, and director vacancy following 2025 resignation.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** United States Census Bureau (officially Bureau of the Census)
**Founding:** March 6, 1902 (permanent Census Office within Interior)
- Relocated to Commerce (1903)
- Part of Department of Commerce since 1913
**Current Director:** George M. Cook (Acting, late 2025)
- Robert Santos resigned February 2025
- Ron Jarmin served as interim Acting Director early 2025
- No permanent nominee announced
**Headquarters:** 4600 Silver Hill Road, Suitland, Maryland 20746
- Current building opened 2006-2007
- Houses 4,000+ employees

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Decennial Census
- Conducted every 10 years
- Counts every person in 50 states, DC, 5 territories
- Determines congressional representation through apportionment
- Questions: Age, sex, race, Hispanic origin, owner/renter status

### American Community Survey (ACS)
- Annual demographics survey (~3.5 million addresses annually)
- Replaced 1990 census long-form; launched 2005
- **40+ topics:** Education, employment, income, housing, transportation, internet access, disability, ancestry, citizenship, language, migration
- Largest household survey administered by Census Bureau
- Response required by law (Title 13)

### Economic Statistics Programs
- 130+ surveys and programs annually
- **Economic Census:** 5-year measure (years ending in 2 and 7); benchmark for GDP
- **Current Population Survey:** Labor statistics
- **Advance Monthly Retail Trade Survey:** Early retail indicators
- **Annual Business Survey:** R&D estimates
- **County Business Patterns:** Subnational data by industry
- **Business Formation Statistics:** Quarterly applications
- **Commodity Flow Survey:** Joint project tracking goods movement

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### 2020 Census Citizenship Question Controversy
- Secretary Wilbur Ross announced citizenship question (March 26, 2018)
- Claimed DOJ requested for Voting Rights Act enforcement
- Documents revealed political motivations
- **Lawsuits:** 14 State AGs + civil rights organizations
- **Harvard research:** Question "significantly increases questions skipped, especially among Hispanic populations"
- Experts predicted ~9 million people would not complete forms
- **Supreme Court (June 2019):** 5-4 ruling found rationale "contrived" and "pretextual"; blocked question
- 2020 census proceeded without citizenship question
- **2024:** Rep. Chuck Edwards' Voter Population Accuracy Act seeks reinstatement for 2030

### 2020 Census Undercount and Differential Privacy
**Miscounts:**
- Post-Enumeration Survey found statistically significant miscounts
- 8 states overcounted, 6 states undercounted
- Extreme undercount: Arkansas (5.04%); largest overcount: Hawaii (6.79%)

**Differential Privacy Controversy:**
- Census implemented "differential privacy" (DP) replacing prior "swapping" method
- TopDown algorithm injects "noise" into population counts
- **Critics:**
  - Systematically undercounts mixed-race and mixed-partisan precincts
  - Unpredictable racial and partisan biases
  - Potentially violates "One Person, One Vote"
  - Disproportionately affects rural and minority populations
  - Steven Ruggles (UMN): "blunt instrument" causing unnecessary data degradation
- Census Bureau has until 2028 to decide privacy system for 2030 census

### Political Interference (2020)
- Trump appointees exerted "unprecedented" pressure on career staff
- **Key Interference Attempts:**
  1. Citizenship question push
  2. Pressure to end census early
  3. Exclude unauthorized immigrants from apportionment (violates 14th Amendment)
  4. Pressure on privacy protections
- September 2020: Ron Jarmin noted unusually high political engagement
- House Oversight Committee hearing on miscounts

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

**Department of Commerce (Parent Agency):**
- Principal subdivision of DOC
- Director reports to Secretary of Commerce
- DOC Inspector General provides oversight

**Office of Management and Budget (OMB) - Statistical Policy:**
- OMB maintains Statistical Policy Directives (SPDs)
- **SPD No. 14:** Official poverty measure
- **SPD No. 15:** Race and ethnicity standards (revised March 28, 2024)
- Census implements OMB-issued standards
- U.S. Chief Statistician coordinates federal statistical system
- Interagency Council on Statistical Policy (ICSP)

**Data Sharing with Federal Agencies:**
**Legal Authority:**
- Title 13, Section 6 authorizes use of existing data from agencies
- Paperwork Reduction Act of 1995

**Routine Data Sharing:**
- Social Security Administration
- Internal Revenue Service
- CMS
- EEOC
- State agencies (SNAP, TANF, WIC)

**Federal Statistical Research Data Centers (FSRDCs):**
- Partnership between federal statistical system and research institutions
- Seven federal partner agencies provide data
- Standard Application Process (SAP) Portal for 16 principal statistical agencies

**Privacy Protections:**
- Title 13 prohibits sharing personal information with DHS or ICE
- Staff sharing census data with law enforcement: 5-year prison, $250,000 fines
- All employees sign sworn affidavit of nondisclosure
