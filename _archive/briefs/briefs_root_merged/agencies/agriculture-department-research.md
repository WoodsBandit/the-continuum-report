# USDA ANALYTICAL BRIEF

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The United States Department of Agriculture (USDA), established in 1862 by President Abraham Lincoln as "The People's Department," is a comprehensive federal agency responsible for agricultural policy, food safety, natural resource management, and domestic nutrition assistance. With FY2025 estimated total outlays of $231 billion and discretionary appropriations continuing FY2024 levels (~$26 billion), the USDA operates through 22 agencies organized into eight mission areas.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** United States Department of Agriculture (USDA)

**Founding Date:** May 15, 1862

**Cabinet Status:** Elevated to Cabinet level by President Grover Cleveland in 1889

**Current Secretary (33rd):** Brooke Leslie Rollins (sworn in February 13, 2025)

**Headquarters:** Washington, D.C.

---

## 2. BUDGET INFORMATION

**FY2024 Enacted Discretionary Appropriations:** $26.2 billion
**FY2025 Total Estimated Outlays:** $231 billion
- Mandatory Programs: $189.6 billion (82.1% of total)
- Discretionary: Continuing Resolution at FY2024 levels

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

### Eight Mission Areas
1. Farm Production and Conservation (FPAC)
2. Trade and Foreign Agricultural Affairs
3. Food Safety
4. Marketing and Regulatory Programs
5. Research, Education, and Economics
6. Natural Resources and Environment
7. Food, Nutrition, and Consumer Services
8. Rural Development

### Major Agencies
- **Forest Service:** ~30,000 employees; manages ~193 million acres
- **Natural Resources Conservation Service (NRCS)**
- **Farm Service Agency (FSA)**
- **Risk Management Agency (RMA)**
- **Food Safety and Inspection Service (FSIS)**
- **Agricultural Research Service (ARS)**
- **Agricultural Marketing Service (AMS)**

---

## 4. MISSION STATEMENT

"To serve all Americans by providing effective, innovative, science-based public policy leadership in agriculture, food and nutrition, natural resource protection and management, rural development, and related issues."

---

## 5. KEY PROGRAMS

### Farm Support Programs
- Price Loss Coverage (PLC), Agriculture Risk Coverage (ARC)
- Crop Insurance (~89% of major field crops covered)
- FY2024: $9.3 billion in subsidy payments; $10.4 billion premium subsidies

### SNAP (Nutrition Assistance)
- FY2024: 41.7 million monthly participants
- Annual cost: $99.8 billion (70% of USDA nutrition spending)

### Food Safety (FSIS)
- Mandatory continuous inspection of meat, poultry, eggs

### Forest Management
- Forest Stewardship Program
- Joint Chiefs' Landscape Restoration Partnership

---

## 6. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Farm Subsidy Distribution Inequality
- Top 10% of recipients receive 73% of payments (~$91,000/farm average)
- Bottom 80% receive <$3,000 annually
- Black, Hispanic, female farmers receive lower payments
- 90% of subsidies go to only 5 crops: wheat, corn, cotton, rice, soybeans

### Commodity Checkoff Program Issues
- 22 mandatory assessment programs; farmers cannot opt out
- First Amendment legal challenges
- Farm Action Report: USDA failed oversight requirements; history of fraud
- 2000 Pork Checkoff: 53% of farmers voted to end program
- 2013 Egg Board: Attempted to block "Just Mayo" products

### Food Safety Recalls (2024-2025)
- E. coli, metal contamination, allergen issues
- 2012-2023: 1,001 recalls; 205.2 million lbs recalled product

---

## 7. INTER-AGENCY CONNECTIONS

### FDA Coordination
- USDA (FSIS): meat, poultry, eggs
- FDA: all other foods
- Threshold: ≥3% raw meat or ≥2% cooked poultry = USDA control

### EPA Coordination
- Pre-1972: USDA held pesticide authority
- Post-1972: EPA primary regulator under FIFRA
- USDA Office of Pest Management Policy coordinates with EPA

### Interior Department
- Joint land management with Forest Service and BLM
- Great American Outdoors Act: $2.8 billion FY2024
