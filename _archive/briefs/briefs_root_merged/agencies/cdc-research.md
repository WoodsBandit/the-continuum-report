# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: CENTERS FOR DISEASE CONTROL AND PREVENTION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The CDC is the nation's public health protection agency, established July 1, 1946. With ~$9.7B budget and 21,000+ employees, it operates disease surveillance, outbreak response, and immunization programs. Faces 2025 restructuring (20% workforce reduction), vaccine recommendation reversals, and measles resurgence crisis.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)
**Founding:** July 1, 1946 (as Communicable Disease Center)
**Current Director:** Jim O'Neill (Acting, August 2025)
**Parent Agency:** Department of Health and Human Services (HHS)
**Headquarters:** Atlanta, Georgia (1600 Clifton Road)
**Staff:** 21,000+ employees and contractors

---

## 2. BUDGET

**FY2025 Request:** $9.683 billion discretionary; $25.753 billion with mandatory
**FY2024:** Enacted via Further Consolidated Appropriations Act (March 2024)
**FY2026 Proposed:** 53% reduction from FY2024
**Historical:** Core funding $6.5-8B (FY2011-2021)

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**Leadership:**
- Director (Jim O'Neill, Acting)
- Reports to HHS Secretary (Robert F. Kennedy Jr.)

**Major Centers:**

1. **NCIRD** - National Center for Immunization and Respiratory Diseases
   - Vaccine programs, respiratory disease surveillance

2. **NCEZID** - National Center for Emerging and Zoonotic Infectious Diseases
   - Seven divisions; infectious threat detection

3. **NCHS** - National Center for Health Statistics
   - Official U.S. health statistics (Hyattsville, MD)

4. **CSELS** - Center for Surveillance, Epidemiology, and Laboratory Services
   - Science, surveillance, disease prevention policies

5. **OID** - Office of Infectious Diseases
   - Umbrella for NCIRD and NCEZID

**2025 Reorganization:**
- ~2,400 employees reduced (20% decrease)
- ASPR absorbed under CDC
- NIOSH transferred to new Administration for a Healthy America
- 28 HHS divisions consolidated to 15

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Disease Surveillance
- National Notifiable Diseases Surveillance System (NNDSS)
- Manual for Surveillance of Vaccine-Preventable Diseases
- Epidemiology and Laboratory Capacity Cooperative Agreement

### Outbreak Response
- Emergency Operations Center (activated ~70 times)
- Field Epidemiology Training Program: 22,000+ trained since 1980
- 7-1-7 approach: detect <7 days, report <1 day, respond <7 days

### Immunization Programs
- Global Immunization Division
- Stop Transmission of Polio (STOP) program
- Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices (ACIP) oversight

### Health Statistics (NCHS)
- National Vital Statistics System (NVSS)
- National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES)
- National Health Interview Survey (NHIS)
