# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: SOCIAL SECURITY ADMINISTRATION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

SSA is the largest federal social insurance program, serving ~69 million beneficiaries monthly. FY2024 expenditure: $1.5 trillion (22.4% of federal budget). Faces trust fund solvency crisis (depletion 2032-2033), disability backlogs, and service delivery challenges.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Original Establishment:** August 14, 1935

**Independent Agency Status:** March 31, 1995

**Current Commissioner:** Frank J. Bisignano (May 2025)

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

- Social Security Retirement
- Disability Insurance (SSDI)
- Supplemental Security Income (SSI)
- Medicare Enrollment Administration

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Trust Fund Solvency
- OASI depletion: 2033
- Combined depletion: 2032
- Without action: ~24% benefit cuts

### Disability Backlogs
- ~940,000 claimants (July 2025)
- Average processing: 220+ days
- Approval rate: 36% (declining)

### Fraud/Overpayments
- FY2015-2022: $72 billion improper payments
- $23 billion uncollected balance

### Customer Service Crisis
- Phone waits: 30+ minutes (up to 102 min)
- Workforce down ~10%
- 6 of 10 regional offices closing
