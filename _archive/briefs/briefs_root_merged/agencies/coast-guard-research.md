# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: UNITED STATES COAST GUARD

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Coast Guard is the oldest continuous seagoing service (1790), operating under DHS in peacetime and Navy in wartime. With $12.3B budget and 56,000 personnel, it executes 11 statutory missions including maritime safety, drug interdiction, and polar operations. Faces aging fleet crisis, staffing shortages, and Academy sexual assault scandal.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** United States Coast Guard (USCG)
**Founding:** August 4, 1790 (Revenue Cutter Service); January 28, 1915 (modern Coast Guard)
**Current Commandant:** Admiral Kevin E. Lunday (28th Commandant, December 2025)
**Parent Agency:** Department of Homeland Security (peacetime); Department of Navy (wartime)
**Staff:** ~56,000 personnel
**Headquarters:** Washington, D.C.

---

## 2. BUDGET

**FY2025:** $12.32 billion (+4.8% over FY2024)
**FY2024:** ~$11.75 billion
**Deferred Maintenance (FY2024):** $179 million (9x higher than 2019)
**Dry-dock Cancellations:** 52% of scheduled periods canceled

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**Command Hierarchy:**
1. Commandant (Admiral, 4-year term)
2. Two Areas: Atlantic Area, Pacific Area
3. Nine Districts (Rear Admiral command)
4. 37 Sectors (shore-based operational units)
5. Stations, Air Stations, ATON Teams

**District Command:**
- District Commander holds Search and Rescue Coordinator authority
- Each district has dedicated Rescue Coordination Center

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Eleven Statutory Missions

**Non-Homeland Security:**
- Marine Safety (vessel inspection, accident investigation)
- Search and Rescue (primary federal maritime SAR)
- Aids to Navigation
- Living Marine Resources (fisheries enforcement)
- Marine Environmental Protection
- Ice Operations

**Homeland Security:**
- Ports, Waterways, and Coastal Security (PWCS)
- Drug Interdiction
- Migrant Interdiction (Operation Vigilant Sentry)
- Defense Readiness (~5% of deployments)
- Other Law Enforcement

### Maritime Safety & Security Teams (MSSTs)
- Specialized units for port security, drug enforcement
- Deployed on cutters and naval vessels

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Academy Sexual Assault Scandal (Operation Fouled Anchor)
- Six-year internal review: 102 reports (1990-2006)
- Former Commandant Karl Schultz withheld investigation from Congress
- Academy official allegedly directed to lie to Congress and victims
- Most perpetrators handled administratively; minimal punishments
- 29 legal complaints; $290 million in pending claims
- Senator Blumenthal: "Most shameful, disgraceful cover-up" ever seen

### Aging Cutter Fleet
- 241 cutters: 40 major, 201 non-major
- Serious maintenance issues increased 21% (FY2018-2023)
- Parts cannibalized from operational cutters
- Offshore Patrol Cutter program: Cost increased $12.5B → $17.6B
- Lead ship delivery slipped 5+ years (2021 → December 2026)

### Arctic Capability Gaps
- Only two operational polar icebreakers
- USCGC Polar Star: ~50 years old; sole heavy icebreaker
- USCGC Healy: sidelined by electrical fire
- Russia operates 40+ icebreakers (including nuclear)
- Goal: 8-9 new polar icebreakers; first expected 2028

### Staffing Crisis
- Cutter crew vacancy rates: 5% (FY2017) → 13% (FY2024)
- Overworked, understaffed crews on maintenance-burdened vessels

### Immigration Enforcement
- Migrant interdiction at "breaking point"
- Major surge from Haiti and Cuba
- Due process concerns for asylum claims at sea

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- DHS: Parent agency (peacetime) since March 2003
- DOD/Navy: Operates under Navy in wartime per 14 U.S.C. § 3
- DOT (Former Parent): Operated under DOT 1967-2003
- State/Local: Coordination for SAR and law enforcement
