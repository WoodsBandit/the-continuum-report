# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: UNITED STATES POSTAL SERVICE

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

USPS is an independent agency of the executive branch providing universal mail service. Facing structural financial challenges from declining mail volumes, USPS operates under mounting deficits despite the 2022 Postal Service Reform Act. FY2024 net loss: $9.5 billion.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** United States Postal Service (USPS)

**Founding Date:** July 1, 1971 (as independent agency)

**Current Postmaster General:** David Steiner (76th PMG, May 2025)

**Headquarters:** 475 L'Enfant Plaza SW, Washington, DC

---

## 2. BUDGET & FINANCES

**FY2024 Revenue:** $79.5 billion
**FY2024 Expenses:** $89.5 billion
**FY2024 Net Loss:** $9.5 billion
**No Direct Tax Funding:** Self-funded through postage

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

- 7 Areas, 67 Districts
- 31,000+ postal facilities
- 56-65 Regional Processing Centers (planned)

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

- First-Class Mail, Marketing Mail
- Priority Mail, Ground Advantage
- 6-day mail, 7-day package delivery
- Money orders, passport services

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Financial Crisis
- Mail volume: 112.5 billion pieces (3.2% decline)
- 2006 PAEA pre-funding mandate: 92% of losses
- Cumulative losses: ~$109 billion (FY2007-2024)

### DeJoy Reforms
- Network consolidation controversy
- Mail delivery delays
- 2024 election mail concerns
- DeJoy resigned March 2025

### iCOP Surveillance
- Social media monitoring program
- OIG: "exceeded law enforcement authority"

---

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- Treasury: $15 billion borrowing limit
- Congress: ~$50 million annual appropriation
- Competitors: FedEx, UPS, Amazon
