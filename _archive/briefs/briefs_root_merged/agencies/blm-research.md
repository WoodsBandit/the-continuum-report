# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: BUREAU OF LAND MANAGEMENT

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

BLM is the federal agency managing 247.3 million acres of public lands, established July 16, 1946. With ~$1.4B budget and 10,000 employees, it oversees grazing, mining, energy development, and conservation. Faces Bundy standoff legacy, monument reduction controversies, and wild horse management crisis.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Bureau of Land Management (BLM)

**Current Director:** Position vacant; Bill Groffy (Acting)

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Public Lands Management
- 247.3 million surface acres (one-eighth of U.S.)
- 700 million acres subsurface mineral estate
- FLPMA mandates multiple-use management

### Energy Development
- Oil and Gas: 23.2 million acres under lease
- Coal: 273 federal leases; 570 million acres oversight
- Renewable Energy: Solar and wind development

### Livestock Grazing
- Permits and leases for cattle/sheep ranching
- Land use plans identify open/closed areas

### Conservation
- 35 million-acre National Conservation Lands
- Wilderness areas, monuments, historic trails

### Wild Horse and Burro Program
- 82,384 animals on BLM lands vs. 26,785 AML
- 152 of 177 herds overpopulated
- 58,314 animals in off-range holding facilities

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Bundy Standoff (2014)
- 21-year dispute over Nevada grazing rights
- Cliven Bundy owed $1M+ in unpaid fees
- April 2014: Armed militia confronted BLM; federal officers retreated
- Bundy cattle still grazing without permits on closed lands
- Criminal prosecution dismissed 2020

### Bears Ears National Monument (2017-2021)
- Obama designated 1.35 million acres (December 2016)
- Trump reduced 85% (largest reversal in U.S. history)
- 95% of public comments supported keeping monument
- Biden restored to 1.36 million acres (October 2021)

### Grand Staircase-Escalante (2017-2021)
- Clinton designated 1.9 million acres (1996)
- Trump reduced ~50% (to 1 million acres)
- Contains significant Late Cretaceous fossil sites
- Biden restored to 1.87 million acres (October 2021)

### Wild Horse Management
- Population 3x Appropriate Management Level
- 2013 NAS report: AML lacks transparency, scientific support
- Controversial surgical spaying/castration practices
- Sales policy controversy (potential slaughter)
- Fertility control vaccines recommended but underfunded
