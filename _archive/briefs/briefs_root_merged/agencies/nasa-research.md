# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: NATIONAL AERONAUTICS AND SPACE ADMINISTRATION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

NASA is an independent federal agency responsible for civil space programs, aeronautics research, and space exploration. With a FY2025 budget of approximately $25 billion and 10 field centers, NASA operates the Artemis moon program, ISS, and scientific missions.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA)

**Founding Date:** July 29, 1958

**Current Administrator:** Jared "Rook" Isaacman (15th Administrator, December 2025)

**Headquarters:** Mary W. Jackson NASA Headquarters, Washington, D.C.

---

## 2. BUDGET & APPROPRIATIONS

**FY2024-FY2025:** ~$25.3 billion
**Artemis:** $7.6 billion annually
**SLS:** $4.1 billion (FY2025)

---

## 3. KEY PROGRAMS

- Artemis Moon Program
- International Space Station (ISS)
- Mars Exploration
- Commercial Crew Program
- James Webb Space Telescope
- Lunar Gateway

---

## 4. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Challenger (1986) and Columbia (2003) Disasters
- O-ring failure, foam strike
- Organizational safety culture failures
- CAIB found same deficiencies caused both

### SLS Cost Overruns
- Total costs: $23.8 billion
- Cost per launch: $4.2 billion
- 6+ years behind schedule
- GAO: Cost reporting misrepresents performance

---

## 5. INTERAGENCY RELATIONSHIPS

- DOD: Space situational awareness
- NRO: Industrial base coordination
- Commerce/NOAA: Weather satellites (45+ year partnership)
- International: ESA, JAXA, CSA partners
