# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: FEDERAL BUREAU OF PRISONS

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

BOP is the federal prison system, established May 14, 1930. With $8.3B annual budget (largest DOJ allocation) and 122 institutions housing 155,000+ inmates, it operates the nation's federal correctional facilities. Faces severe staffing crisis (8,000+ vacancies), sexual abuse failures (FCI Dublin), Jeffrey Epstein death aftermath, and First Step Act implementation challenges.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Federal Bureau of Prisons (BOP)
**Founding:** May 14, 1930 (Act of Congress, signed by President Herbert Hoover)
- First Director: Sanford Bates
- Created to centralize administration of 11 federal prisons
**Current Director:** William K. Marshall III (sworn in April 21, 2025)
- Succeeded Colette S. Peters (resigned January 20, 2025)
**Headquarters:** Washington, DC (Central Office)

---

## 2. BUDGET

**Annual Budget:** $8.3 billion (largest DOJ allocation)

**FY2024 Infrastructure:** $179.8 million (38% reduction from $290M in FY2023)

**FY2025:** Funded at FY2024 level via Continuing Resolution (P.L. 119-4)

**Infrastructure Crisis:**
- $3 billion maintenance and repair backlog
- 57% of 120 facilities over 30 years old
- Nearly one-third exceed 50 years

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**Agency Scope:**
- 122 federal institutions (penitentiaries, correctional institutions, prison camps)
- 6 regional offices
- 1 Central Office (Washington, DC)
- 2 staff training centers
- 22 Residential Reentry Management offices (halfway houses)
- 35,000+ staff members
- Facilities across 37 states and Puerto Rico

**Six Geographic Regions:**
1. **Western Region (WXR):** AK, AZ, CA, HI, ID, MT, NV, OR, WA, WY, UT
2. **Southeast Region (SER):** AL, FL, GA, MS, PR, SC
3. **South Central Region (SCR):** AR, LA, NM, OK, TX
4. **Mid-Atlantic Region (MXR):** DE, KY, NC, MD, TN, WV, VA
5. **North Central Region (NCR):** CO, IA, KS, IL, IN, MI, MN, MO
6. **Northeast Region:** Based in Philadelphia

**Regional Offices:** Philadelphia, Atlanta, Annapolis Junction, Kansas City, Dallas, Dublin (CA)

**Hierarchy:** Director → Regional Directors → Wardens → Institution Staff

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Federal Inmate Population
- 155,096 federal prisoners (2025)

### First Step Act Implementation (December 2018)
- **Earned Time Credit:** 10-15 days per 30 days in approved programs
- Credits applied toward prerelease custody or early supervised release
- **Cost Differential:** $120/day institution vs. $60/day community custody

### Halfway House Network
- ~160 contracted locations nationwide
- 10,000+ total beds
- Current occupancy: 8,200+ inmates
- **Policy Restriction (April 21):** Capped at 60 days for most; 125 days for RDAP participants

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Staffing Crisis
- 8,000+ correctional officer positions short
- 4,000+ current vacancies
- 2021 AP investigation: Nearly one-third of CO positions vacant
- **"Augmentation":** Cooks, teachers, nurses forced into supervision roles
- Cascading effects: Increased overtime, security compromises, reduced programming
- 2025: Trump administration halting some hiring

### Sexual Abuse and Assault
**Staff-on-Inmate:**
- 2022 Senate investigation: Sexual abuse by staff in 2/3 of facilities housing women
- **FCI Dublin:** "the rape club"; 7 officers convicted (including warden, chaplain)
  - Nearly two dozen inmates abused (2018-2021)
  - Facility permanently closed December 2024

**Inmate-on-Staff:**
- 40% of surveyed staff reported sexual harassment by inmates
- 69% of harassed staff were female
- Thomson Federal Prison (2019-2023): 1,600 female staff attacked

**Backlog:**
- Nearly 8,000 open misconduct investigations
- Hundreds alleging sexual abuse
- $30+ million settlements (last 15 years)

### Jeffrey Epstein Death (August 10, 2019)
**Location:** Metropolitan Correctional Center (MCC) New York
**Official Determination:** Suicide by hanging

**BOP Failures (DOJ OIG):**
- Guards sleeping and shopping online (not doing 30-minute checks)
- Excessive bed linens despite suicide risk
- Unmonitored jail phone use night before death
- Inadequate security cameras
- Cellmate reassignment failure despite prior suicide attempt
- Two guards (Noel, Thomas) admitted falsifying records

**Consequence:** Attorney General Barr removed BOP Director

### Prison Conditions
- Historical peak: 165% capacity (1992); FY2018: 110% average
- Infrastructure deficiencies: Leaking roofs, poor water systems, unsanitary bathrooms
- FCI Tallahassee: Chronic ceiling/window water leaks
- Inadequate medical care documented across system
- Violence increased due to staffing shortages

### Federal Prison Oversight Act (2024)
- DOJ OIG comprehensive inspections of every federal prison
- Risk scoring based on 12+ factors
- Enhanced transparency and accountability

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

**Department of Justice (Parent Agency):**
- Federal law enforcement agency within DOJ
- Attorney General sets policy direction
- Inspector General authority: 100+ reports in past 20 years on systemic issues

**U.S. Marshals Service:**
- Shared responsibility for pre-trial detention
- BOP: Sentenced federal offenders
- USMS: Defendants awaiting trial/sentencing
- Joint use of detention facilities

**Federal Probation and Pretrial Services (Judicial Branch):**
- Separate from executive branch; operates under federal courts in 94 districts
- Community corrections bed-space provision
- Prison designation determinations
- Case management coordination
- Task force participation: crime, terrorism, drug, gang, computer crime

**U.S. Parole Commission:**
- Coordination on supervised release and parole matters
