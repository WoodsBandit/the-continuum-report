# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: U.S. DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** United States Department of Commerce

**Founding Date:** February 14, 1903 (as Department of Commerce and Labor); renamed March 4, 1913

**Current Secretary:** Howard Lutnick (2025)

**Headquarters:** Herbert C. Hoover Building, Washington, D.C.

---

## 2. BUDGET INFORMATION

**FY2025 Enacted (Continuing Resolution):** $10.388 billion
- Administration Request: $11.465 billion
- Change from FY2024: -$440 million (-4.1%)

Three largest bureaus (NOAA, NIST, Census) account for 77% of spending.

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

### Major Bureaus (13 total)
1. **NOAA** - Weather forecasting, climate, fisheries, marine protection
2. **NIST** - Measurement standards, AI safety, CHIPS Program Office
3. **Census Bureau** - Decennial census, economic statistics
4. **Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS)** - Export controls, dual-use items
5. **International Trade Administration (ITA)** - Trade promotion, export assistance
6. **USPTO** - Patents and trademarks
7. **Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA)** - GDP, economic statistics (~$101M budget)
8. **Economic Development Administration (EDA)**
9. **FirstNet Authority**
10. **NTIS**

---

## 4. MISSION STATEMENT

"The Department of Commerce's mission is to create the conditions for economic growth and opportunity for all communities."

---

## 5. KEY PROGRAMS

### NOAA/National Weather Service
- 1.5 million annual forecasts
- 50,000 annual warnings
- 9 specialized forecasting centers (Hurricane, Storm Prediction, Climate, etc.)

### CHIPS Act Implementation
- Total Program: $50 billion direct funding + $75 billion loans/guarantees
- Awards issued (Jan 2025): ~$33.7 billion
- Key recipients: Intel ($7.865B), TSMC ($6.6B), GlobalFoundries ($1.5B)

### Export Controls (BIS)
- Regulates advanced semiconductors, AI technology
- December 2024: Expanded China restrictions
- 80 entities added to Entity List (2024-2025)

---

## 6. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### 2020 Census Citizenship Question
- Secretary Ross announced question addition (March 2018)
- Supreme Court ruled "pretextual reason" violated federal law
- Question dropped; research predicted 2.2% response rate drop

### Export Control Controversies
- US-China Business Council: Controls empowered Chinese competitors
- December 2024 rules expanded to 50%-owned subsidiaries
- Cadence Design Systems: $95 million penalty for illegal exports
- Chinese retaliation: Rare earth export restrictions (October 2024)

### CHIPS Act Challenges
- Only 24 of 161 milestones reported complete (July 2025)
- Target: Increase U.S. leading-edge chip manufacturing from 0% to 20% by 2030

---

## 7. INTER-AGENCY CONNECTIONS

### State Department (Export Controls)
- State: ITAR (munitions)
- Commerce: EAR (dual-use items)
- Export Enforcement Coordination Center (E2C2)

### Intelligence Community
- Office of Intelligence serves as Federal Intelligence Coordination Office
- Strategic Intelligence Division supports Entity List reviews
- CIA provides unclassified intelligence to NTIS

### Treasury
- BEA provides balance of payments data
- CFIUS coordination on foreign investment reviews
