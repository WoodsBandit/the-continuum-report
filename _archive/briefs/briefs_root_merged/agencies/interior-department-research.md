# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: US DEPARTMENT OF THE INTERIOR

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The U.S. Department of the Interior (DOI) is the federal government's primary agency for managing the nation's natural resources and cultural heritage, overseeing approximately 650 million acres of public landsâ€”roughly 30% of the nation's total surface area. Established in 1849, the department manages an expansive portfolio spanning national parks, wildlife refuges, public lands, water resources, geological research, and Indian affairs. With a FY2025 budget of approximately $18.0 billion in direct authority, the department operates through nine major bureaus employing over 60,000 personnel nationwide. Under current leadership (Secretary Doug Burgum, sworn in February 2025), the DOI is pursuing an agenda emphasizing energy development on federal lands, streamlined environmental review processes, and organizational restructuring.

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

### Mission Statement
"The U.S. Department of the Interior protects and manages the Nation's natural resources and cultural heritage; provides scientific and other information about those resources; and honors its trust responsibilities or special commitments to American Indians, Alaska Natives, Native Hawaiians, and affiliated Island Communities."

### Major Bureaus and Agencies

| Bureau | Acronym | Key Functions |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| **Bureau of Land Management** | BLM | Multiple-use management of 245+ million acres of public lands; oil/gas leasing |
| **National Park Service** | NPS | Management of 424 national park units; ~20,000 employees; 300+ million annual visitors |
| **U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service** | USFWS | Migratory bird management, endangered species protection, wildlife refuge administration |
| **Bureau of Indian Affairs** | BIA | Trust responsibilities to 574 federally recognized tribes; administration of 55.7 million acres |
| **Bureau of Indian Education** | BIE | Education services for Native American communities |
| **U.S. Geological Survey** | USGS | Scientific research; earth science data; water resources; mapping and geology |
| **Bureau of Reclamation** | Reclamation | Western water management; dam operations; irrigation; hydroelectric power |
| **Bureau of Ocean Energy Management** | BOEM | Offshore oil/gas and renewable energy leasing |
| **Office of Surface Mining Reclamation and Enforcement** | OSMRE | Coal mining reclamation; surface mining enforcement |
| **Bureau of Safety and Environmental Enforcement** | BSEE | Worker safety and environmental compliance (offshore operations) |

**Personnel:** 60,634 employees nationwide (2020)

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS AND RESPONSIBILITIES

### Public Lands Management
- **Acreage Managed:** 500+ million surface acres; 700 million acres of subsurface minerals; 1.7 billion acres of Outer Continental Shelf

### National Parks and Conservation
- **National Park System:** 424 units; 300+ million annual visitors
- **National Wildlife Refuge System:** 700 species of birds; 220 mammal species; 250 reptile/amphibian species
- **Land and Water Conservation Fund (LWCF):** Since 1965, funded $4 billion in projects in every U.S. county

### Water Resources Management (Bureau of Reclamation)
- 475 dams across 17 western states
- Water supply to 31+ million people
- Irrigation water to 10 million acres of farmland
- 58 hydroelectric plants serving 3.5 million homes

### Scientific Research (USGS)
- Approximately 1.9 million water data collection sites
- ~10,000 science and support staff across 400+ science centers

### Indian Affairs and Trust Responsibilities
- 574 federally recognized tribes (~2 million indigenous Americans)
- 55.7+ million acres held in trust

### Energy Development on Federal Lands
- FY2024 Production: 14% of domestic oil; 2% of domestic natural gas; $7 billion federal revenues
- Offshore Resources: 68.79 billion barrels of oil; 229.03 trillion cubic feet of natural gas on OCS

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Energy Development vs Conservation

**Oil and Gas Leasing Streamlining (2025)**
- New BLM Instruction Memorandum to complete lease parcel reviews within 6 months (reduced from 8-15 months)

**Offshore Wind Energy Restrictions (2025)**
- Fish and Wildlife Service halted new Eagle Take Permits to wind projects
- BOEM eliminated requirement to publish five-year offshore wind lease sale schedule

**BLM Conservation Rule Rescission**
- 2024 rule established conservation as "use on par with other uses"
- 2025: Proposed rescission of the Conservation and Landscape Health Rule

### National Energy Emergency Measures (April 23, 2025)
- DOI invoked emergency authorities under NEPA, ESA, and NHPA to expedite energy project approvals

### Tribal Relations Controversies

**Indian Boarding School Legacy (1870s-1970s)**
- Federal Indian Boarding School Initiative documented 973 children died at boarding schools
- Schools prohibited indigenous languages, practices, and cultures

**Trust Fund Accounting Disputes**
- Ongoing disputes over proper accounting for American Indian Trusts
- Historical mismanagement and underfunding of tribal programs

**1972 Interior Building Occupation**
- November 3-9, 1972: Native American activists occupied DOI headquarters
- $700,000 in building damage; irreplaceable treaties and records lost/destroyed

### Major Personnel Reductions (2025)
- Elimination of 2,000+ positions across department
- Environmental Justice Office shuttered
- USGS, BLM, USFWS significant cuts
