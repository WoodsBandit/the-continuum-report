# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: NATIONAL SECURITY AGENCY

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The National Security Agency is the U.S. Department of Defense's primary signals intelligence (SIGINT) and information assurance agency, responsible for global monitoring, collection, and processing of communications intelligence. With over 20,000 employees at Fort Meade and a classified budget, the NSA operates as a central node in the Intelligence Community while commanding U.S. Cyber Command through a "dual-hat" arrangement.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** National Security Agency / Central Security Service (NSA/CSS)

**Founding Date:** November 4, 1952

**Current Director:** General Timothy D. Haugh

**Headquarters:** Fort G. Meade, Maryland

---

## 2. BUDGET INFORMATION

**Classification:** Part of National Intelligence Program (NIP) - classified
**FY2026 NIP Request:** $81.9 billion (total IC)
**FY2026 MIP Request:** $33.6 billion

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

### Primary Directorates
1. Directorate of Operations
2. Capabilities Directorate
3. Cybersecurity Directorate
4. Signals Intelligence Directorate

### Dual-Hat Command
- NSA Director also commands U.S. Cyber Command

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

- PRISM (Foreign Intelligence Surveillance)
- XKeyscore (Internet data analysis)
- MUSCULAR (Joint NSA-GCHQ program)
- Five Eyes Alliance coordination

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Snowden Disclosures (2013)
- Exposed PRISM, XKeyscore, Five Eyes operations
- Revealed mass surveillance capabilities

### Warrantless Wiretapping (2001-2007)
- Post-9/11 surveillance without FISA warrants
- 2020: Ninth Circuit ruled program unlawful

### FISA Section 702
- 125,000+ individuals under surveillance annually
- Incidental collection of U.S. citizen data

---

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- DOD: Combat support agency
- USCYBERCOM: Dual-hat command
- CIA: Joint Special Collection Service
- FBI: Counterintelligence coordination
- Five Eyes: GCHQ, CSEC, ASD, GCSB
