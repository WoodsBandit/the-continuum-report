# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: U.S. DEPARTMENT OF ENERGY

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) is a cabinet-level agency established in 1977 that consolidates responsibility for America's nuclear weapons complex, scientific research enterprise, energy policy, and environmental cleanup. It represents a unique fusion of defense and civilian energy missions.

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

### Major Components
1. **National Nuclear Security Administration (NNSA)** - Nuclear weapons stewardship
2. **Office of Science** - Largest U.S. funder of physical sciences research
3. **National Laboratories System** - 17 laboratories
4. **Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC)** - Independent regulatory agency
5. **Energy Information Administration (EIA)** - Statistical agency

### National Laboratories
- 3 under NNSA: Los Alamos, Lawrence Livermore, Sandia
- 10 under Office of Science

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

- Stockpile Stewardship and Management Program
- Advanced Reactor Demonstration Program ($2.477 billion)
- Fusion Energy Sciences ($763 million)
- Grid Security & Reliability

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Yucca Mountain Nuclear Waste Repository
- Site in seismically active region
- No designated long-term U.S. storage exists
- Federal government pays $300-500 million annually to utilities

### Solyndra Loan Guarantee Failure
- $535 million loan guarantee
- Company bankruptcy September 2011
- Program loss: $528 million

### Nuclear Weapons Lab Security Incidents
- 2004 Los Alamos security stand-down
- 2015 uranium inhalation incident
