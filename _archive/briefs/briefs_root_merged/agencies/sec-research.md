# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: SECURITIES AND EXCHANGE COMMISSION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The SEC is the primary federal regulator of U.S. securities markets, established June 6, 1934. With ~3,800 staff and FY2024 appropriation of ~$2.1B (fully offset by transaction fees), it oversees securities registration, market integrity, and investor protection.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** United States Securities and Exchange Commission
**Founding:** June 6, 1934 (Securities Exchange Act)
**Current Chair:** Paul S. Atkins (January 2025)
**Previous Chair:** Gary Gensler (April 2021 - January 2025)
**Headquarters:** Washington, D.C.
**Staff:** ~3,800, 11 regional offices

---

## 2. BUDGET

**FY2024:** ~$2.1 billion appropriated
**FY2025 Request:** $2.594 billion
**Funding:** Deficit-neutral through transaction fees

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**5 Commissioners:** Presidential appointees, 5-year terms, max 3 same party

**6 Divisions:**
1. Corporation Finance - Disclosure requirements
2. Enforcement - Investigations, actions
3. Trading and Markets - SRO oversight
4. Investment Management - Advisers, funds
5. Examinations - Inspection program
6. Economic and Risk Analysis - Data analysis

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

- Securities registration (Securities Act 1933)
- Market oversight and surveillance
- Investor protection initiatives
- Sarbanes-Oxley implementation

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Madoff Failure (2008)
- 10+ years of complaints ignored
- OIG found systemic investigative failures
- 8 employees disciplined, none fired

### 2008 Financial Crisis
- 2004 net capital rule change enabled excessive leverage
- Derivatives unregulated
- Investment bank collapses (Lehman, Bear Stearns)

### Crypto Enforcement (Gensler Era)
- 125 crypto enforcement actions (2021-2024)
- "Regulation-by-enforcement" criticized
- Court rebukes: "arbitrary and capricious"
- Ripple case loss

### Revolving Door
- 31% of SEC lawyers become "revolvers"
- Regulatory capture concerns

---

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- CFTC: Joint crypto/derivatives coordination
- DOJ: Enforcement coordination, parallel prosecutions
- State AGs: NASAA partnership
- FINRA: SRO oversight
