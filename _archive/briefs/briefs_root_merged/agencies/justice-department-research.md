## ANALYTICAL BRIEF: U.S. DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE

### OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** United States Department of Justice (DOJ)

**Founding Date:** June 22, 1870 (signed into law by President Ulysses S. Grant; operative July 1, 1870)
- *Historical Note:* While the DOJ was formally established in 1870, the Office of the Attorney General traces its origins to 1789 via the Judiciary Act.

**Current Attorney General:** Pam Bondi (87th Attorney General)
- Confirmed by Senate: February 4, 2025 (54-46 vote)
- Sworn in: February 5, 2025
- Background: Former Attorney General of Florida (2011-2019), over 18 years as prosecutor

**Headquarters:** Robert F. Kennedy Department of Justice Building
- Address: 950 Pennsylvania Avenue NW, Washington, D.C.
- Constructed: 1931-1934; completed 1935
- Size: 7 floors, 1.2 million square feet
- Renamed: November 20, 2001 (in honor of RFK, AG 1961-1964)
- Architectural Style: Classical Revival with Art Deco ornamentation

### ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**Leadership:** Attorney General reports directly to the President and serves as Cabinet member

**Major Law Enforcement Agencies:**
1. **Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI)** - Principal investigative agency; enforces federal law in 200+ areas including terrorism, cybercrime, organized crime, counterintelligence
2. **Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA)** - Lead federal agency for narcotics and controlled substances enforcement
3. **Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives (ATF)** - Protects public from crimes involving firearms, explosives, arson; regulates lawful commerce (transferred to DOJ from Treasury in 2003)
4. **U.S. Marshals Service** - Federal law enforcement with nationwide jurisdiction
5. **Federal Bureau of Prisons (BOP)** - Operates federal prison system
6. **94 U.S. Attorneys' Offices** - Organized by districts; located in every state and territory

**Major Litigating Divisions:**
- Criminal Division
- Civil Division
- Antitrust Division
- Civil Rights Division
- Tax Division
- Environment & Natural Resources Division
- Office of Legal Counsel (OLC) - Provides legal opinions to President and executive agencies
- Justice Management Division (JMD) - Administrative functions (budgeting, personnel)

**Workforce:** Over 115,000 employees; 40+ component organizations

### KEY PROGRAMS

**1. Federal Prosecution**
- U.S. Attorneys conduct most trial work for criminal cases and represent the federal government in civil litigation
- Three statutory responsibilities: criminal prosecution, civil litigation, and federal debt collection
- 94 district offices provide nationwide coverage

**2. Antitrust Division**
- Promotes competition through enforcement of Sherman Act (Sections 1 & 2) and Clayton Act
- Created permanent Civil Conduct Task Force (2024) to "energize and prioritize non-merger civil enforcement"
- Expanded compliance guidance to civil matters; offers whistleblower financial rewards

**3. Civil Rights Division**
- Pattern-or-practice investigations of police misconduct
- Hate crimes prosecution and voting rights enforcement
- Equitable access to justice initiatives
- Civil Rights Fraud Initiative (May 2025) - Uses False Claims Act to pursue federal funding recipients allegedly violating civil rights compliance certifications

**4. Office of Justice Programs (OJP)**
- Federal leadership in crime prevention and control
- Develops and evaluates criminal and juvenile justice programs
- Partnerships with federal, state, local, tribal, and nonprofit organizations
- Supports crime victims

**5. Whistleblower Programs**
- DOJ Antitrust Division partnered with U.S. Postal Service (July 2025) to offer financial rewards for antitrust violations reporting
- Criminal Division and U.S. Attorneys' Offices offer non-prosecution agreements incentives

### DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

#### 1. INSLAW/PROMIS SCANDAL (1980s-1990s)

**Background:**
Inslaw, Inc. developed PROMIS, a case management software system, under a $9.6 million DOJ contract awarded March 1982.

**Core Allegations:**
- DOJ allegedly converted (stole) Inslaw's Enhanced PROMIS software through "trickery, fraud and deceit"
- Unauthorized modifications introduced backdoor functionalities for covert surveillance and intelligence gathering
- Pirated software distributed to federal agencies and foreign intelligence services without authorization
- Software disseminated to 40+ countries without Inslaw's knowledge or permission

**Court Findings (Bankruptcy Court, 1988):**
- After 23 witnesses and 280+ exhibits, court ruled DOJ converted PROMIS "trickery and deceit"
- DOJ project manager Brewer found to have developed intense dislike for Inslaw
- Inslaw awarded $7 million in damages

**Later Legal Outcomes:**
- Settlement upheld by federal court; ordered retrial on appeal
- July 31, 1997: Judge Christine Miller ruled all PROMIS versions in public domain; government free to use as it wished
- Three-judge Review Panel upheld 1997 ruling
- 1998: Court of Federal Claims ruled against Inslaw

**Congressional & DOJ Response:**
- Multiple Justice Department internal reviews
- Two congressional investigations
- Special counsel appointment by Attorney General William P. Barr
- Review of special counsel report under Attorney General Janet Reno
- DOJ conclusions: no credible evidence of improper PROMIS distribution to foreign governments or document destruction

**International Intelligence Connections:**
- National Archives documents confirm PROMIS copy given to Israel
- Recipient identified as Israeli spymaster Rafi Eitan
- Evidence that software provided to CIA and international intelligence partners
- FBI eventually opened as "Major Case" (ranked with Osama bin Laden, 9/11, Anthrax investigations)

**The "Octopus" Connection:**
- Journalist Danny Casolaro attempted linking INSLAW with October Surprise allegations, Iran-Contra, BCCI scandal as unified conspiracy
- Casolaro died under suspicious circumstances in 1991; death ruled suicide despite questions
- 2024: Netflix released "American Conspiracy: The Octopus Murders" documentary
