# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: OCCUPATIONAL SAFETY AND HEALTH ADMINISTRATION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

OSHA is the federal workplace safety regulator, established December 29, 1970. With ~$632M budget and 900 compliance officers for 6M+ workplaces, it enforces safety standards. Faces 2025 deregulation rollback (heat rule, walkaround rule), inspector staffing shortages, OSHRC quorum crisis, and NIOSH research partner gutting (90% workforce cut).

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Occupational Safety and Health Administration
**Founding:** December 29, 1970 (Occupational Safety and Health Act, signed by Nixon)
- Took effect April 28, 1971
**Current Leadership:**
- Douglas L. Parker (November 2021 - January 2025, Biden administration)
- Trump nominee: David Keeling (former Amazon/UPS safety director)
**Headquarters:** Department of Labor, 200 Constitution Avenue NW, Washington, DC 20210
**Impact:** Since 1971, workplace fatalities cut 60%, injury/illness rates cut 40%

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Workplace Safety Standards Enforcement
- Authority over 6+ million workplaces
- ~900 compliance officers
- FY2024 Enforcement: 34,625 total inspections
- **Penalties (2025):** Serious violations: $16,550; Willful/repeat: $165,532

### Whistleblower Protection Program
- Enforces 20+ federal laws protecting employees from retaliation
- Covers: Safety hazards, injury reporting, inspection participation
- Environmental, financial reform, food safety, nuclear safety, pipeline safety
- Employees can refuse dangerous work if imminent death/serious harm risk

### Voluntary Protection Programs (VPP)
- Recognition for employers with exemplary safety records
- Minimum $3.5 million annual appropriation

### State Occupational Safety Plans
- 22 states: Both private sector and state/local government coverage
- 7 states: State/local government only
- 29 total states/territories with OSHA-approved plans
- OSHA provides up to 50% funding and oversight
- States must be "at least as effective" as federal OSHA

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Inspector Staffing Shortages
- ~900 compliance officers for 6+ million workplaces
- 2018-2020 (Trump first term): 10% drop in inspectors (878 to 790)
- Critics: Ratio prevents adequate coverage

### OSHRC Quorum Crisis
- Review Commission has lacked quorum since April 28, 2023
- Significant backlog of contested citations unresolved
- Commissioner term expired January 2025

### COVID-19 Emergency Temporary Standard (Struck Down)
- Supreme Court stayed ETS (January 2022)
- Court: COVID not "occupational" hazard but universal public health problem
- OSHA withdrew rule

### Heat Safety Rule Controversy (2024-2025)
- NPRM published August 30, 2024
- Would apply to indoor/outdoor work across all sectors
- Comment period extended to January 14, 2025
- **2025 Status:** Trump's "Regulatory Freeze Pending Review" paused rule
- Rep. Mark Messmer's Health Workforce Standards Act (November 2025) to repeal
- 23 Republican co-sponsors; 20+ industry organizations support

### 2025 Deregulation Initiative
- "Regulatory Freeze Pending Review" (January 20, 2025)
- "10-to-1" deregulation: Eliminate 10 regulations per new rule
- Target: 60+ workplace safety and wage regulations
- Major rules under review: Walkaround Rule, Heat Safety Rule
- Congressional Review Act eliminated 16 regulations in 2025
- Rep. Andy Biggs reintroduced Nullify OSHA Act (NOSHA) to abolish OSHA

### Withdrawn Infectious Disease Rulemaking
- Intended to address tuberculosis, chickenpox, MRSA, COVID
- Withdrawn per E.O. 14192 ("Unleashing Prosperity through Deregulation")

### NIOSH Impact on OSHA Research
- **Dr. John Howard removed** after 22-year tenure (April 1, 2025)
- **90% of NIOSH workforce (900+ employees) laid off**
- Staff reduced from ~1,400 to fewer than 150
- Partial reinstatement (328 employees) in May 2025
- National Personal Protective Technology Laboratory halted (N95/SCBA vetting)
- ~30 health hazard evaluations will never be completed

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

**Parent Agency - Department of Labor:**
- Reports to Secretary of Labor
- Assistant Secretary of Labor for Occupational Safety and Health leads OSHA

**Mine Safety and Health Administration (MSHA):**
- Separate DOL agency (Federal Mine Safety and Health Act of 1977)
- March 29, 1979 MOU delineates jurisdiction
- OSHA: General occupational safety; MSHA: Mining operations
- Both report to Secretary of Labor

**National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health (NIOSH) - CDC:**
- Established simultaneously with OSHA (OSH Act of 1970)
- Part of CDC under HHS (NOT part of NIH or OSHA)
- **NIOSH provides research; OSHA provides enforcement**
- 1974: NIOSH/OSHA Standards Completion Program resulted in 387 new OSHA standards
- NIOSH publishes Criteria for Recommended Standards to inform OSHA rulemaking

**State Occupational Safety Plans:**
- 21 states with complete plans (private + public sectors)
- 7 states with public-sector-only plans
- OSHA provides up to 50% funding
- California's Cal/OSHA example of stricter standards
