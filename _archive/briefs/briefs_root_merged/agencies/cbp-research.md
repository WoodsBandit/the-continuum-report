# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: CUSTOMS AND BORDER PROTECTION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

CBP is America's largest law enforcement agency (67,000+ employees), established March 1, 2003. With $19.6B+ budget, it operates 328 ports of entry, secures 6,000+ miles of borders, and processes $306M+ daily in duties. Faces 2025 enforcement expansion, border wall controversies, and child treatment concerns.

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Use of Force
- 31% of CBP deaths from fatal car chases
- 22% from fatal shootings
- 8% involve minors under 18

### Child Migrant Treatment
- ~1,360 children permanently separated
- Documented abuse: hunger, physical assault, inadequate care
- Flores Agreement violations
- 2025: Reversed protections for sponsor information

### Border Wall (2017-2021)
- 458 miles constructed
- 62% on federal public lands
- 31+ environmental laws waived
- GAO 2023: Destroyed cacti, blocked wildlife corridors, damaged sacred sites

### Corruption
- 80 Border Patrol agents arrested since 2004
- 127 CBP officers arrested
- 38% drug-related; 34% immigration-related
- Weak internal reporting culture

### 2025 Expansion
- Reversed sensitive location protections
- Expanded expedited removal nationwide
- CBP One asylum app disabled
- 42.5% increase in officer hiring
- 84% increase in Border Patrol hiring

### Missing Children Crisis
- 32,000 UACs failed to appear for court (FY2019-2023)
- 291,000 UACs not served with NTA
- August 2024: ICE unable to account for unaccompanied children
