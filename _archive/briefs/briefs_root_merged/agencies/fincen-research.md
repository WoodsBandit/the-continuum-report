# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: FINANCIAL CRIMES ENFORCEMENT NETWORK

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

FinCEN is the U.S. financial intelligence unit, established April 1990. With ~$216M budget request (FY2025) and Bank Secrecy Act enforcement authority, it combats money laundering, terrorist financing, and financial crimes. Faces FinCEN Files leak fallout, cryptocurrency enforcement challenges, and beneficial ownership implementation rollback.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN)
**Founding:** April 1990 (Treasury Order 105-08)
- Became independent bureau following USA PATRIOT Act of 2001
**Current Director:** Andrea Gacki (appointed September 2023)
- Former Director of OFAC
- Acting Under Secretary for Terrorism and Financial Intelligence (2021)
**Headquarters:** Washington, DC; Operations in Vienna, Virginia
**Parent Organization:** Department of the Treasury (under Under Secretary for Terrorism and Financial Intelligence)

---

## 2. BUDGET

**FY2024 Appropriations:**
- House proposed: $166 million (12.7% below FY2023's $190.2M)
- Senate proposed: $190 million
- Final below President's $229M request

**FY2025 Request:** $216 million
- $20.959M program increase for Beneficial Ownership Implementation
- Pay adjustments: 5.2% (2024) + 2.0% (2025)
- 15 additional FTE for beneficial ownership operations

**Key Challenge:** Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA) and Corporate Transparency Act (CTA) mandate ~40 rulemaking requirements

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**Leadership:** Director reports to Under Secretary for Terrorism and Financial Intelligence

**Six Main Divisions:**

1. **Intelligence Division** - Analysis and reporting support
2. **Enforcement Division:**
   - Office of Compliance and Enforcement (Depository Institutions, MSBs, Non-Bank FIs)
   - Office of Special Measures (Section 311 USA PATRIOT Act)
   - Office of Special Investigations (SAR disclosure violations)
3. **Regulatory Division** - Regulatory development
4. **Policy Division:**
   - BSA rule drafting, guidance, regulatory rulings
   - Strategic policy (AML/CFT compliance)
5. **Information Technology Division**
6. **Administration Division**

**Recent Reorganization:** Shifted from stakeholder-based to function-based organization

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) Enforcement
- Primary statutory authority
- 31 CFR Chapter X requirements
- Enforcement: targeting, examinations, investigations, civil monetary penalties

### Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)
- ~150,000 reports processed since inception
- $5,000+ threshold (domestic); $2,000+ (MSBs)
- Confidential; essential to law enforcement
- October 2025: Joint FAQs relaxed documentation requirements

### Beneficial Ownership Information (BOI) Reporting
**Major Change (March 2025):** Removed BOI requirements for U.S. domestic companies
- Now applies only to foreign reporting companies
- January 1, 2024: BOI IT Systems deployed on schedule
- Corporate Transparency Act (2021) originally required domestic reporting
- Database access: Federal agencies, state/local law enforcement (court orders), foreign authorities, financial institutions
- Legal challenge: National Small Business United v. Yellen (appeal pending)

### Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Programs
- 2024 rule: AML/CFT requirements for investment advisers (effective January 1, 2026)
- Mandatory risk-based programs
- Government-wide AML/CFT priorities integration

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### FinCEN Files Leak (2020)
- 2,657 leaked documents including 2,121 SARs
- $2+ trillion suspicious transactions (1999-2017)
- Released by BuzzFeed News to ICIJ and 108 media partners
- **Source:** Natalie Mayflower Sours Edwards (Treasury official) - pleaded guilty January 2020
- **Banks implicated:** JPMorgan Chase ($514B), Deutsche Bank ($1.3T)
- **Notable figures:** Paul Manafort, Jho Low, Reza Zarrab, Semion Mogilevich
- Contributed to bipartisan Corporate Transparency Act (2021)

### Cryptocurrency Enforcement
- **Binance (November 2023):** $3.4 billion settlement (with OFAC, IRS-CI)
- **BitMEX (August 2021):** $100 million penalty
- **Paxful (December 2025):** $3.5 million penalty; first action with "Compliance Considerations" guidance
- **Bitzlato:** First "primary money laundering concern" under Combating Russian Money Laundering Act
- **CVC Kiosk Scams:** 10,956 FBI complaints in 2024 (99% YoY increase); ~$246.7M losses
- **CVC Mixing:** October 2023 NPRM identifies as primary money laundering concern

### Beneficial Ownership Implementation Challenges
- Five-year delays post-FinCEN Files
- 2024 constitutional challenge limiting scope to foreign companies
- Transparency advocates concerned about policy rollbacks

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

**Treasury Department Structure:**
- Bureau under Office of Terrorism and Financial Intelligence (TFI)
- Under Secretary: John K. Hurley (confirmed 2025)
- TFI also includes: OFAC, OIA, TFFC, TEOAF

**Department of Justice:**
- Virtual Currency Emerging Threats Working Group (FBI-led, formed 2012)
- Includes FBI, DEA, U.S. Attorney's Offices, Criminal Division
- Joint enforcement actions

**FBI/IRS Coordination:**
- Information sharing through law enforcement partnerships
- IRS Criminal Investigation participates in major settlements
- Shared BSA/AML compliance responsibilities

**Banking Regulators (Federal Functional Regulators):**
- OCC, Federal Reserve, FDIC, NCUA
- MOUs (September 2004)
- FFIEC coordination for uniform standards
- FSOC participation for systemic risk

**Formal Advisory Bodies:**
- Bank Secrecy Act Advisory Group (BSAAG) - chaired by FinCEN Director
- PROTECT series (with DEA, HSI, CBP, Secret Service, FBI, DOJ, local law enforcement)

**International:**
- U.S. Financial Intelligence Unit (one of 147 FIUs)
- Member of Egmont Group of Financial Intelligence Units
