# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: BUREAU OF ALCOHOL, TOBACCO, FIREARMS AND EXPLOSIVES

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

ATF is a law enforcement bureau within DOJ focused on firearms, explosives, and arson investigation, established 1972 (Treasury) and transferred to DOJ in 2003. With ~$1.9B budget, it regulates firearms licensing, traces crime guns, and investigates explosives. Faces chronic director vacancies and controversial regulations.

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Firearms Regulation
- 9 types of Federal Firearms Licenses (FFLs)
- eTrace System: 2,000+ agencies, 17,000+ users
- NIBIN (ballistics information network)

### Explosives Regulation
- Federal Explosives Licensing Center
- Mandatory background checks
- Safe Explosives Act enforcement

### Arson Investigation
- Only federal agency with arson as core mission
- NCETR training facility (Redstone Arsenal)
- Accelerant Detection Canine Program

### Firearms Trafficking Enforcement
- Violent Crime Impact Teams
- Trafficking Strike Forces
- Anti-Firearms Trafficking Campaign (2021)

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Fast and Furious (2009-2011)
- Phoenix "gunwalking" operation
- ~2,000 firearms illegally purchased
- CBP Agent Brian Terry killed with operation firearms
- AG Holder held in contempt of Congress (2012)
- 14 ATF/DOJ employees found responsible

### Director Vacancy Crisis
- 2006: Senate confirmation requirement added
- Only two confirmed directors since 2006
- NRA effectively blocked nominations 15 years
- B. Todd Jones (2013-2015), Steven Dettelbach (2022-2025)

### Ghost Guns Rule (2022)
- Expanded "firearm" definition
- March 2025: Supreme Court upheld 7-2 (Bondi v. VanDerStok)
- Ghost gun seizures: 1,600 (2017) â†’ 27,000+ (2023)

### Pistol Brace Rule (2023-2025)
- January 2023: Final rule required registration
- Multiple circuit courts ruled unlawful
- July 2025: Trump DOJ dismissed appeal; rule vacated nationwide
