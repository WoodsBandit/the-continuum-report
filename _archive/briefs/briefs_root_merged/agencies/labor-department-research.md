# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: US DEPARTMENT OF LABOR

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The U.S. Department of Labor (DOL) is a Cabinet-level executive department responsible for administering over 180 federal labor laws covering approximately 10 million employers and 125 million workers. Established March 4, 1913, the DOL oversees workplace safety, wage enforcement, unemployment insurance, pension protection, and labor statistics.

## 4. MISSION STATEMENT

"To foster, promote, and develop the well-being of the wage earners, job seekers, and retirees of the United States; improve working conditions; advance opportunities for profitable employment; and assure work-related benefits and rights."

---

## 5. KEY PROGRAMS

### Unemployment Insurance
- Income support to displaced workers
- Short-Time Compensation programs

### Workplace Safety (OSHA)
- Standards setting and enforcement
- ~14 Americans die daily from work-related incidents
- 4.6 million workers seriously injured annually

### Wage Enforcement (WHD)
- Minimum wage, overtime pay requirements
- Investigates wage theft and misclassification

### Pension Protection (PBGC)
- Insurance on private sector defined benefit plans
- Maximum guarantee: $5,011.36/month (single-employer)
- Current surplus: $37.629 billion

---

## 6. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### OSHA Enforcement Issues
- Maximum criminal penalty: 6 months jail (misdemeanor)
- Most Permissible Exposure Limits date to 1970-1971
- 2025: Proposed rollback of general duty clause protections

### Wage Theft
- $3+ billion recovered 2017-2020
- WHD budget <$100 million; low detection rates
- Disproportionately affects low-wage, minority, immigrant workers

### Gig Economy Misclassification
- Widespread misclassification avoids minimum wage, benefits, taxes
- ~2/3 of gig companies use arbitration to limit class actions
- California AB 5, New York expanded enforcement

### Pension Concerns
- Historical PBGC deficit (now improved)
- GAO concerns about plan abuse detection
