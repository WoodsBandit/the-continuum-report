# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: COMMODITY FUTURES TRADING COMMISSION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The CFTC is an independent agency regulating derivatives markets (futures, options, swaps), established October 23, 1974. With ~$411M budget and jurisdiction over $400+ trillion notional swaps market, it faces chronic underfunding and crypto regulatory turf battles with SEC.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Commodity Futures Trading Commission
**Founding:** October 23, 1974 (Commodity Futures Trading Commission Act)
**Current Chair:** Michael S. Selig (16th Chairman)
**Headquarters:** 1155 21st Street NW, Washington, DC 20581
**Regional Offices:** Chicago, New York, Kansas City

---

## 2. BUDGET

**FY2024:** $411 million (12.6% increase over FY2023)
**FY2025 Request:** $374 million
**Staff:** 759 employees (FY2023)
**Jurisdiction:** $400+ trillion notional swaps market

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**5 Commissioners:** Presidential appointees, 5-year staggered terms, max 3 same party

**Core Divisions:**
- Division of Clearing and Risk
- Division of Enforcement

- Market Participants Division
- Division of Data
- Division of Administration

**Key Offices:**
- Office of the Chief Economist
- Office of International Affairs
- Office of Technology Innovation

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Derivatives Markets Regulation
- Exclusive jurisdiction over commodity futures, options, swaps
- Authority over derivatives clearing organizations
- Oversight of designated contract markets

### Cryptocurrency Jurisdiction
- Bitcoin/Ethereum classified as commodities
- Authority over crypto derivatives
- Fraud/manipulation authority in spot markets
- December 2025: New guidance on digital assets as margin collateral

### Notable Enforcement
- Binance: $3.4 billion settlement (largest crypto penalty)
- BitMEX: $100 million settlement
- FTX: $12.7 billion judgment

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### 2008 Financial Crisis Origins
- Brooksley Born (1996-1999) attempted OTC derivatives regulation
- Blocked by Treasury, Fed, SEC
- CFMA 2000 exempted OTC derivatives from regulation
- $60 trillion CDS market unmonitored during crisis
- Dodd-Frank (2010) brought swaps into regulatory framework

### SEC Crypto Turf War
- SEC claims most tokens are securities
- CFTC claims digital assets are commodities
- 2025: Acting Chair Pham announced turf war "over"
- Agencies now pursuing cooperative approach

### FTX Collapse (2022)
- CFTC charged Bankman-Fried, FTX, Alameda
- $8 billion customer deposit misappropriation
- August 2024: $12.7 billion consent order
- SBF sentenced to 25 years (March 2024)

### Chronic Underfunding
- ~$371-411 million budget vs $400+ trillion market
- Resource constraints limit enforcement capacity

---

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- SEC: MOUs on swaps/security-based swaps, Form PF data access, crypto coordination
- Federal Reserve: CDS clearing MOU, Treasury market coordination, FSOC collaboration
- Treasury: Data sharing with OFR, Treasury Market Working Group
- DOJ: Criminal referrals, technical assistance for prosecutions
