# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: U.S. DEPARTMENT OF HOUSING AND URBAN DEVELOPMENT (HUD)

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) is a Cabinet-level federal agency established in 1965 to coordinate federal activities affecting housing and urban development. With a current workforce of approximately 8,000 employees and an annual budget exceeding $67 billion, HUD administers the nation's largest rental assistance program and oversees fair housing enforcement.

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Housing Choice Voucher Program (Section 8)
- Serves over 2.3 million families
- Only 1 in 4 income-eligible households receives voucher

### FHA Mortgage Insurance
- MMI Fund capital ratio: 8.03% (FY2021)
- Economic value: $100.5 billion positive

### Public Housing
- Capital needs backlog: $40 billion
- Growing at $3.4 billion per year

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Fair Housing Enforcement Failures (2025)
- FHEO staffing cut 65%
- 66 fair-housing nonprofits received termination letters
- $30 million in FHIP grants eliminated

### Public Housing Capital Crisis
- $40 billion maintenance backlog
- Lead-based paint oversight failures

### Ben Carson Era (2017-2021)
- $31,000 dining room set scandal
- Family ethics violations investigated
