# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: U.S. FOREST SERVICE

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Forest Service manages 193 million acres of National Forests and Grasslands, established February 1, 1905. With multi-billion dollar budget and ~30,000 employees, it oversees timber, recreation, watershed, and wildfire management. Faces firefighter pay crisis, wildfire budget domination, and staffing shortages threatening fire prevention work.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** United States Forest Service (USFS)
**Founding:** February 1, 1905 (Transfer Act under Theodore Roosevelt)
**First Chief:** Gifford Pinchot (1905-1910)
**Current Chief:** Randy Moore (verify current status)
**Parent Agency:** U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA)
**Headquarters:** Washington, D.C.

## 2. BUDGET

**Major Accounts:**
- Wildland Fire Management (WFM): Largest share
- National Forest System (NFS)
- State, Private & Tribal Forestry (SPTF)
- Forest and Rangeland Research (FRR)

**Recent Allocations:**
- $478 million for active forest management
- $154 million for 26 Forest Legacy projects
- $23+ million in timber production grants

**Historical Context:**
- 1995: Wildfire was 16% of budget
- 2005: Exceeded 50% of budget
- Wildfire now dominates agency spending

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### National Forest Management
- 193 million acres managed
- Planning, recreation, grazing, timber, watershed, wildlife, law enforcement

### Wildfire Crisis Response
- Wildfire strategy (January 2022)
- Collaborative Wildfire Risk Reduction Program: 21 projects, 14 states
- Hazardous fuels treatment
- World's leading wildland fire research organization
- Research ROI: $7 per $1 spent on treatments

### Timber Programs
- Timber Production Expansion Guaranteed Loan Program
- Research on silvicultural efficiency
- Wood technology and innovation

### Conservation
- Forest Legacy Program
- Urban and Community Forestry
- Community Wildfire Defense Grants

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Wildfire Budget Crisis
- 1995: 16% of budget â†’ 2005: 50%+ of budget
- Borrowing from other programs became unsustainable
- 2018 disaster fund attempted fix
- Escalating costs from inflated pricing

### Firefighter Pay and Retention Crisis
- Base pay historically: $15/hour
- 50% permanent staff lost since 2020
- 30-50% of ~11,000 firefighters could resign
- Union accusations of "wage theft"
- Recent increases insufficient for retention
- Staffing down 27-29% (2025)

### Fire Prevention Work Backlog
- Prescribed burns, tree thinning slowed dramatically (2025)
- Some locations lost 40% non-fire staffing
- Firefighters pulled for suppression during prevention season
- Targets repeatedly reduced due to workforce depletion

### Logging vs. Conservation
- One Big Beautiful Bill Act: 250M board feet mandate through 2034
- Mature tree logging raises wildfire risk concerns
- Climate and ecosystem impact tensions
- Policy pressure vs. ecological evidence conflict
