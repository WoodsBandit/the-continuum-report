# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: FEDERAL EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT AGENCY

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

FEMA is the federal disaster response coordination agency, established April 1, 1979. With $22.5B+ in Disaster Relief Fund appropriations and 20,000+ employees across 10 regions, it coordinates federal emergency response and administers NFIP. Faces Katrina/Maria legacy failures, DRF funding crises, and 2025 staff reassignments to ICE.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA)
**Founding:** April 1, 1979 (Executive Order 12127)
**Parent Agency:** Department of Homeland Security (since March 2003)
**Headquarters:** Washington, D.C.
**Staff:** 20,000+ employees across 10 regional offices

---

## 2. BUDGET

**FY2025 DRF Appropriations:** $22.51 billion (+ $29B total with supplementals)
**FY2024 DRF:** $16 billion
**Historical Context (1992-2021):** DRF received $381 billion total; 75% from supplementals
**COVID-19:** 45% of DRF major disaster obligations (FY2022-2024)
**FY2026 Proposed:** Significant reduction

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Disaster Response
- Coordinates federal response to presidential declarations
- Two types: emergency and major disaster declarations
- 10 regional offices for deployment coordination

### National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP)
- 4.7 million policyholders
- $1.3 trillion in coverage
- Nation's largest single-line insurance program
- 90% of disasters involve flooding

### Grant Programs
- Public Assistance: Debris removal, infrastructure restoration
- Flood Mitigation Assistance (FMA)
- Building Resilient Infrastructure (BRIC): $1 billion FY2021
- Homeland Security Grant Program (HSGP)

### Preparedness
- Homeland Security National Training Program
- National Domestic Preparedness Consortium

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Hurricane Katrina (2005)
- Widely documented preparedness/response failures
- Established baseline for FEMA reform efforts

### Hurricane Maria Puerto Rico (2017)
- Emergency warehouses nearly empty pre-storm
- Only 695 generators available; 31 on island after 3 days
- 54% of deployed staff not rated "qualified"
- Lost visibility of 38% of shipments ($257M)
- Average 69 days for commodities to reach destinations
- Of $23B earmarked, only $1.8B spent by August 2022
- Trump Administration response "suffered from same flaws as Katrina"

### Disaster Relief Fund Crises
- DRF depends on supplemental appropriations (75% historically)
- Recurring cycles of projected shortfalls
- FY2024: $7.4 billion shortfall; August 2024 depletion
- "Immediate Needs Funding" restrictions used cyclically

### FEMA Staff Reassignments to ICE (August 2025)
- ~100+ FEMA staff reassigned to ICE
- Half of FEMA's HR department
- ~50 employees from security teams
- 90-day detail for hiring 10,000 new ICE officers
- Employees warned of removal for declining
- FEMA already down ~2,000 staffers
- Signals potential agency dismantling
