# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: GENERAL SERVICES ADMINISTRATION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

GSA is an independent agency managing federal real estate and procurement, established July 1, 1949. With ~$33B operating budget and 12,000 employees, it oversees 8,397 buildings, 215,000 vehicles, and $60B+ annual procurement. Headquarters for DOGE operations in 2025.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** General Services Administration
**Founding:** July 1, 1949 (Federal Property and Administrative Services Act)
**Current Administrator:** Edward C. Forst (December 24, 2025)
**Headquarters:** 1800 F Street NW, Washington, DC 20405

---

## 2. BUDGET

**Operating Budget:** ~$33 billion annually
**Procurement Volume:** $60+ billion annually
**Federal Buildings Fund (FBF):** Primary real estate funding mechanism
- Revenue: Rent from occupant agencies
- Chronic underfunding: ~$7B cumulative shortfall (FY2011-2024)

**FY2025 Request:** $3.5B for FBI Headquarters (rejected)

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Trump Hotel Lease (Old Post Office)
- Trump Old Post Office LLC lease since 2013
- GSA ruled no lease violation (Trump placed hotel in trust)
- 2019 OIG: GSA failed to consider Emoluments Clauses

### 18F Shutdown (March 2025)
- Deemed "non-critical" under DOGE
- 455 federal projects halted
- Included IRS Direct File, passport processing

### DOGE Role (2025)
- January 20, 2025: EO established DOGE
- Leadership: Elon Musk, Steve Davis, Thomas Shedd
- Occupied Level 6 of GSA headquarters (Feb-May 2025)
- Described as agency "choke point"
- Results: 9% workforce reduction; 350 leases terminated ($140M savings)
- November 2025: DOGE formally ended
- Musk: "Somewhat successful"; wouldn't repeat

### IT Modernization Failures
- 11 of 69 systems critically outdated
- Treasury systems: COBOL and Assembly Language
- 7 systems with known cybersecurity vulnerabilities
