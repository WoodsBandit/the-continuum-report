# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: FOOD AND DRUG ADMINISTRATION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The FDA is the federal regulatory agency for food safety, drugs, medical devices, and biologics, established 1906. With $7.2B budget (49% from industry user fees), it operates through six product centers and regulates $240B in domestic food plus pharmaceutical and device markets. Faces accelerated approval controversies, opioid crisis legacy, and RFK Jr. reform agenda.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Food and Drug Administration (FDA)
**Founding:** 1906 (Pure Food and Drugs Act)
**Current Commissioner:** Martin A. Makary, M.D. (27th Commissioner, April 1, 2025)
**Parent Agency:** Department of Health and Human Services (HHS)
**Headquarters:** White Oak Campus, Silver Spring, Maryland
**Staff:** 18,000+ employees
**Field Presence:** 223 field offices, 13 laboratories (50 states + international)

## 2. BUDGET

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**Leadership:**
- Commissioner (Martin Makary, 2025-present)
- Reports to HHS Secretary

**Six Product-Oriented Centers:**

1. **CDER - Center for Drug Evaluation and Research**
   - OTC and prescription drugs, biological therapeutics
   - Standard review: 10 months; priority: 6 months

2. **CBER - Center for Biologics Evaluation and Research**
   - Vaccines, blood devices, human tissues

3. **CDRH - Center for Devices and Radiological Health**
   - All medical devices; 700+ AI/ML devices authorized since 2020
   - 510(k), PMA, De Novo pathways

4. **CFSAN - Center for Food Safety and Applied Nutrition**
   - $240B domestic food, $15B imported food, $15B cosmetics
   - Reorganized into Human Foods Program (HFP) in 2025

5. **CVM - Center for Veterinary Medicine**
   - Animal drugs and food additives

6. **CTP - Center for Tobacco Products**
   - 2.6M applications reviewed; 99.5% high-market products authorized

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Drug Approval
- Four-phase clinical trial system
- 12-15 year typical development timeline
- Postmarket surveillance and pharmacovigilance

### Device Regulation
- 510(k) Premarket Notification
- Premarket Approval (PMA) for Class III
- Breakthrough device designation (2016)

### Food Safety
- HACCP, FSMA implementation
- Recall authority for adulterated products

### Tobacco Regulation (CTP 2024)
- 710+ warning letters to manufacturers
- Youth e-cigarette use declined 70% over five years
- 1.4% of youth currently smoke (25-year low)

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Accelerated Approval Pathway Debates
- 30.4% of U.S. oncology drugs use accelerated approval
- Companies often fail to conduct required confirmatory studies
- Aducanumab (Aduhelm) discontinued November 2024
- December 2024: FDA draft guidance strengthening requirements

### Opioid Crisis and OxyContin
- 1996: Approved OxyContin with label stating addiction "very rare"
- "Delayed absorption" statement reducing abuse liability came from FDA, not Purdue
- Two FDA reviewers later took positions at Purdue Pharma
- 2002 advisory committee: 8 of 10 members had pharmaceutical ties
- $7.4 billion settlement with Purdue/Sacklers (2024)

### Baby Formula Crisis (2022)
- October 2021: Whistleblower concerns raised; FDA inspected 4 months later
- February 2022: Abbott recall; 40% of U.S. supply affected
- Peak shortage: 70% out-of-stock rate (May 2022)
- Two infant deaths; four hospitalizations
- FDA audit: "Inadequate policies" to heed warnings

### RFK Jr. FDA Reform Agenda (2025)
- Eliminate industry user fees ($3.3-3.5B)
- 10,000+ HHS job reductions (March 2025)
- FDA nutrition department potential elimination target
- Expanded supplement industry health claims
- Began banning red dye #3 in food products

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- HHS: Parent agency; Commissioner reports to HHS Secretary
- USDA: FDA handles all food except meat/poultry/eggs
- CDC: Foodborne illness surveillance collaboration (IFORC)
- EPA: Pesticide tolerance enforcement
- FTC: 1971 MOU on consumer protection coordination
- 50+ interagency food safety agreements
