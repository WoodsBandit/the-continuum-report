# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: NATIONAL CREDIT UNION ADMINISTRATION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The NCUA is an independent agency regulating federal credit unions and administering share insurance, established March 10, 1970. Self-funded through NCUSIF ($24B assets) and operating fees, it insures 124M+ account holders up to $250,000. Facing significant credit union consolidation wave.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** National Credit Union Administration
**Founding:** March 10, 1970; reorganized November 10, 1978
**Current Chair:** Kyle S. Hauptman (January 20, 2025)
**Headquarters:** 1775 Duke Street, Alexandria, Virginia 22314

---

## 2. BUDGET/FUNDING

**2025-2026 Budget:** $316.2 million (20% decrease from 2025)
**Staff:** 967 salaried positions

**Funding Sources:**
- National Credit Union Share Insurance Fund (NCUSIF): ~$200M annually
- Operating fees from regulated credit unions
- Treasury securities investments

**NCUSIF (Q3 2025):**
- Assets: $24 billion
- Net income: $100.4 million
- Projected equity ratio: 1.30%

**Coverage:** $250,000 per account ownership category (Dodd-Frank permanent minimum)

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**3-Member Board:** Presidential appointees, max 2 same party, 6-year staggered terms

**Central Organization:**
- Central Office (Alexandria, VA)
- Five Regional Offices
- Asset Management and Assistance Center (Austin, TX)
- Community Development Revolving Loan Fund
- Central Liquidity Facility

**Staff:** ~950 employees across 16 offices and three regions

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Share Insurance
- Insures federal and state-chartered credit union deposits
- $250,000 per ownership category
- Backed by full faith and credit of U.S. government
- Covers 124+ million account holders

### Chartering
- Grants federal credit union charters
- Population threshold: 500+ potential members
- Field of membership reviews for community charters

### Supervision & Examination
- Examines federal credit unions
- Regulates state-chartered applying for share insurance
- FFIEC member

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Field of Membership Expansion
- NCUA expanded community charter eligibility
- Banks argue undercuts common bond requirement
- 2020: Amendments allow CSAs up to 2.5M population
- ABA: Expansions "contrary to congressional intent"
- NCUA received 11,380 comments on proposed rule

### Cannabis Banking
- State-legal cannabis businesses lack federal banking access
- Former Chair Hood: Credit unions serving compliant businesses won't face enforcement
- Live Life FCU (February 2021): First explicit enforcement for cannabis BSA/AML violations
- Legislative gap: SAFE Banking Act not enacted

### Credit Union Merger Wave
- Q1 2025: 35 mergers approved
- Q2 2025: 45 mergers ($5.6B combined assets)
- 2024 Total: 162 mergers ($15B acquired assets)
- Largest: Digital Federal CU + First Tech FCU (~$30B combined)
- 22 of 45 Q2 mergers involved negative-earnings credit unions
- Pressures: Leadership challenges, poor financial condition

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- State Regulators: Federal-state chartering coordination
