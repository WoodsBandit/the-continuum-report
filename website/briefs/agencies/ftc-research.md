# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: FEDERAL TRADE COMMISSION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The FTC is the primary federal antitrust and consumer protection agency, established September 26, 1914. With FY2024 budget of $450M and 5 commissioners, it enforces civil antitrust law and consumer protection statutes.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Federal Trade Commission
**Founding:** September 26, 1914 (FTC Act)
**Current Chair:** Andrew N. Ferguson (January 2025)
**Previous Chair:** Lina Khan (June 2021 - January 2025)
**Headquarters:** 600 Pennsylvania Avenue NW, Washington, DC

---

## 2. BUDGET

**FY2024:** $450 million
**FY2025 Request:** $535 million
**Consumer Recovery (FY2024):** $333+ million returned

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**5 Commissioners:** Max 3 from same party, 7-year terms

**3 Bureaus:**
1. Bureau of Competition - Antitrust, merger review
2. Bureau of Consumer Protection - Advertising, deceptive practices
3. Bureau of Economics - Analysis support

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

- Hart-Scott-Rodino merger review
- Antitrust enforcement (civil only)
- Consumer protection enforcement
- Click to Cancel rule
- Junk Fees rule

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Big Tech Antitrust Cases
- Google: Search monopoly ruling (August 2024)
- Meta/Facebook: Trial April 2025
- Amazon: Trial October 2026
- Apple: Lawsuit March 2024

### Khan's Aggressive Enforcement
**Wins:** Blocked Lockheed/Aerojet, Nvidia deal, Kroger/Albertsons
**Losses:** Microsoft/Activision approved

### Business Opposition
- Called "most feared in Silicon Valley"
- Reid Hoffman: "not helping America"
- Commissioner Wilson resigned in protest
- Employee morale declined (83% to 44%)

---

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- DOJ Antitrust: Concurrent jurisdiction, coordination
- State AGs: Parens patriae suits, joint enforcement
- FCC: Limited coordination
