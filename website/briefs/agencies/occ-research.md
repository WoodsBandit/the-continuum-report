# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: OFFICE OF THE COMPTROLLER OF THE CURRENCY

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The OCC is an independent bureau within Treasury regulating national banks, established February 25, 1863. Self-funded through bank assessments (~96%), it supervises 1,100+ national banks and federal savings associations. Faced criticism over 2023 bank failure supervision lapses.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Office of the Comptroller of the Currency
**Founding:** February 25, 1863 (National Currency Act)
**Current Comptroller:** Jonathan V. Gould (July 15, 2025)
**Headquarters:** Washington, DC (within Treasury Department)
**District Offices:** New York, Chicago, Dallas, Denver; London examination office

---

## 2. BUDGET/FUNDING

**Self-Funded Model:** No Congressional appropriations
**Assessment-Based:** ~96% from semiannual national bank assessments
**Remaining 4%:** Investment income in Treasury securities
**Net Position (Sept 2024):** $1,750.6 million

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**Leadership:**
- Comptroller of the Currency (5-year term)
- Senior Deputy Comptrollers

**2025 Reorganization (October 1, 2025):**
- Large and Global Financial Institutions ($500B+ assets)
- Regional and Midsize Financial Institutions
- Community Banks

**Treasury Relationship:** Independent bureau; Secretary has general oversight but cannot control budget, regulations, or supervisory matters

**Ex Officio Roles:** FDIC board, FSOC, FFIEC, Neighborhood Reinvestment Corporation

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### National Bank Charter
- Authority to charter, regulate, supervise national banks
- Jurisdiction: 1,100+ national banks, federal thrifts, federal branches of foreign banks

### Supervision & Examination
- Safety and soundness oversight
- Consumer protection compliance
- Fair access enforcement

### Fair Access Rule (2021)
- Requires individual customer risk assessments
- Applies to banks $100B+ assets
- Status uncertain under current administration

### Fintech Charter
- Special Purpose National Bank (SPNB) charter
- Accepting applications since July 2018

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Fintech "Valid When Made" Rule (2020)
- Established loan interest rates remain valid when transferred
- States alleged "rent-a-charter" schemes
- February 2022: Court upheld OCC rule

### CRA Modernization Controversy
- 2020: OCC issued unilateral rule without Fed/FDIC
- December 2021: Rule rescinded
- 2023: Joint interagency rule issued
- February 2024: Trade associations sued
- March 2024: Texas court preliminary injunction
- March 2025: Agencies announced intent to rescind; restore 1995 framework

### 2023 Bank Failure Oversight
- SVB: Examiners identified deficiencies but "did not fully appreciate vulnerabilities"
- Warning signs from 2019 (rapid growth, uninsured deposit reliance)
- Enforcement delayed until August 2022
- Congressional finding: Supervisors focused on "wrong issues (processes vs. material risks)"
- May 2023: Revised enforcement policies to authorize asset reduction orders

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- CFPB: Consumer protection consultation

**Joint Guidance:**
