# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: CENTERS FOR MEDICARE & MEDICAID SERVICES

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

CMS administers Medicare, Medicaid, and ACA marketplaces, established 1977 (as HCFA), renamed 2001. With $1.6 trillion budget authority and 6,000+ employees, it serves 160+ million Americans. Faces drug pricing implementation, Medicaid unwinding (25M disenrolled), prior authorization reform, and managed care oversight gaps.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS)
**Founding:** 1977 (as Health Care Financing Administration); renamed 2001
**Administrator:** Transitioned with 2025 administration (verify current)
**Parent Agency:** Department of Health and Human Services (HHS)
**Headquarters:** Woodlawn, Maryland (Baltimore area)
**Staff:** 6,000+ employees (~4,000 at HQ)

---

## 2. BUDGET

**FY2025 Budget Authority:** $1.6 trillion
**FY2025 Discretionary:** $4.3 billion (administrative)
**Medicare + Medicaid:** 85% of all HHS outlays
**State Medicaid Spending:** 30% of total state spending

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**Office of the Administrator**

**Major Operating Centers:**
- **Center for Medicare:** Fee-for-service programs
- **Center for Medicaid and CHIP Services (CMCS):** Medicaid and CHIP oversight
- **CCIIO:** Health insurance marketplaces, ACA implementation
- **Center for Clinical Standards and Quality:** Quality improvement
- **Center for Program Integrity:** Fraud prevention
- **CMMI:** Innovation and payment model testing

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Medicare (Title XVIII)
- 68 million beneficiaries
- Parts A (hospital), B (medical), C (Medicare Advantage), D (drugs)
- 2025 Part B Premium: $185/month
- 2025 Part B Deductible: $257

### Medicaid (Title XIX)
- 73-82 million beneficiaries (post-unwinding)
- Federal-state partnership
- Low-income individuals, families, elderly, disabled

### CHIP
- Children in families too high for Medicaid, too low for private insurance
- Combined with Medicaid: 82+ million Americans

### Health Insurance Marketplace
- HealthCare.gov and state exchanges
- ACA coverage for uninsured outside Medicare/Medicaid

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Drug Pricing
- August 2024: First 10 drugs negotiated (effective January 2026)
- December 2024: 15 additional drugs negotiated
- Combined savings: $12 billion (44% price reductions)
- December 2025: GLOBE, GUARD, GENEROUS models announced
- Controversy: Budget reconciliation reduced negotiation efficacy

### Managed Care Oversight Deficiencies
- $207.5 million paid for deceased enrollees
- MLR remittance tracking failures
- State directed payments: $124.3B → $144.6B projected
- Mental health parity failures (8 states)
- Managed care excluded from Recovery Audit program

### Medicaid Continuous Enrollment Unwinding (2023-2024)
- March 31, 2023: Continuous enrollment ended
- 25.2 million disenrolled (April 2023-June 2024)
- 69% retention rate for completed renewals
- Peak: 94 million → Current: 73-77 million

### Prior Authorization
- Average 43 requests weekly per physician
- 23% of physicians report serious adverse events from prior auth
- January 2024 CMS Rule: 72-hour expedited; 7-day standard
- 2025 WISeR Model: AI-powered reviews in 6 states; companies paid for denials
