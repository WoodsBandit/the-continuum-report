# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: US DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The U.S. Department of Transportation (USDOT or DOT) is the fourth-largest federal agency, tasked with ensuring safe, efficient, sustainable, and equitable movement of people and goods. Operating across 10 operating administrations and managing approximately 95,000 employees, the DOT oversees all major transportation modesâ€”aviation, highway, rail, transit, maritime, and pipeline safety. Led by Secretary Sean P. Duffy (confirmed January 2025), the agency faces significant challenges in oversight effectiveness, budget constraints, and evolving regulatory priorities under the Trump administration.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** United States Department of Transportation

**Founding:** Established by Congress on October 15, 1966; operational launch April 1, 1967

**Current Leadership:**
- Secretary: Sean P. Duffy (confirmed January 28, 2025; sworn in by Justice Clarence Thomas)
- Deputy Secretary: Steven G. Bradbury

**Headquarters:** 1200 New Jersey Avenue SE, Washington, DC 20590 (1.35 million sq ft facility, operational since 2007)

**Organizational Scale:** Approximately 95,000 federal employees managing national transportation infrastructure

---

## 2. MISSION STATEMENT

**Official Mission:** "To deliver the world's leading transportation system, serving the American people and economy through the safe, efficient, sustainable, and equitable movement of people and goods" (FY2022-2026 Strategic Plan).

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

The DOT operates through the Office of the Secretary (OST) and 10 Operating Administrations:

| Operating Administration | Primary Responsibility |
|--------------------------|----------------------|
| **Federal Aviation Administration (FAA)** | Civil aviation safety, air traffic control, airspace management |
| **Federal Highway Administration (FHWA)** | Federal-Aid Highway Program, National Highway System |
| **Federal Transit Administration (FTA)** | Public transportation funding |
| **Federal Railroad Administration (FRA)** | Rail safety, Amtrak oversight |
| **National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA)** | Vehicle safety standards |
| **Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA)** | Commercial truck and bus safety |
| **Pipeline and Hazardous Materials Safety Administration (PHMSA)** | Pipeline safety |
| **Maritime Administration (MARAD)** | Waterborne transportation |

## 5. KEY PROGRAMS AND AUTHORITIES

### Aviation Safety (FAA)
- Air traffic control modernization
- Aircraft certification and airworthiness
- Commercial space launch licensing

### Highway Infrastructure
- Federal-Aid Highway Program funding to states
- National Highway System maintenance

### National Defense Coordination
- Civil Reserve Air Fleet (CRAF)
- Strategic Highway Network (STRAHNET)

---

## 6. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Boeing 737 MAX Crashes (2018-2019)
- Lion Air Flight 610: October 29, 2018 (189 deaths)
- Ethiopian Airlines Flight 302: March 10, 2019 (157 deaths)
- Total: 346 deaths
- Congressional finding: "Grossly insufficient oversight by the FAA"
- Boeing settled January 2021 for $2.5 billion

### Infrastructure Spending Controversies
- Trump Administration Funding Freeze (January 2025)
- $125 billion in unobligated IIJA funding affected
- 17 state attorneys general filed lawsuit

## 7. INTERAGENCY RELATIONSHIPS
