# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: U.S. AGENCY FOR INTERNATIONAL DEVELOPMENT

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

USAID was the independent agency managing U.S. foreign development assistance, established November 3, 1961. With ~$35B budget (pre-2025), it was the largest bilateral aid agency globally. Dramatically dismantled in 2025 with 83% funding cuts and merger into State Department.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** U.S. Agency for International Development
**Founding:** November 3, 1961 (Executive Order by JFK)
**Headquarters:** 1300 Pennsylvania Avenue NW, Washington, DC 20004
**Status (2025):** Functions transferred to State Department; 15 statutory positions remain

---

## 2. BUDGET

**FY2024:** $35+ billion (pre-cuts)
- Largest bilateral aid agency globally
- One-third of SFOPS appropriations

**FY2025 (Pre-Cuts):**
- $12.2B for State/USAID combined
- $2.22B for administrative accounts

**FY2025 Rescissions:**
- $7.9B rescinded (July 2025)
- 83% overall funding reduction
- $75.9B in terminated programs

**FY2026 Proposal:** Cut from $54.4B to $28.4B (~50% reduction)

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**Pre-2025:**
- Administrator (presidential appointee)
- Two Deputy Administrators
- 12 central bureaus
- 60+ field missions worldwide
- ~10,000 employees

**Geographic Bureaus:**
- Africa
- Asia and Near East
- Europe and Eurasia
- Latin America and Caribbean

**Functional Bureaus:**
- Economic Growth, Education, Environment
- Humanitarian Assistance
- Global Health
- Democracy and Governance

**Post-2025:** Reduced to 15 statutory positions; functions at State

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### 2025 Dismantling
**Timeline:**
- January 20: Foreign aid review initiated
- January 30-31: Musk demanded communications shutdown; Jason Gray refused and was removed
- February 2025: 1,600+ layoffs; headquarters shuttered
- February 3: Musk labeled USAID "criminal organization"
- March 28: Merger into State announced
- July 1: State assumed programming
- September 2: Operations transferred/shut down

**Scope:**
- 5,341 programs terminated (86%)
- 898 programs remained
- 83% funding reduction

### Afghanistan Withdrawal (2021)
- Unclear evacuation roles
- Inadequate crisis staff
- 7-day delayed guidance to implementers
- Leadership evacuated August 15, leaving field staff unsupported
- Poor data tracking (SPOT platform issues through 2024)

### Efficiency Concerns
- ~50% of awards failed expected results; 100% paid
- 60% of funding to 25 organizations (2017)
- "Beltway bandits": Top contractors repeatedly underperform
- IRD, Louis Berger Group: Fraud charges while top contractors
- 90% to international partners; 10% to local groups
