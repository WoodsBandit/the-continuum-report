# ORIGIN STORY: EXPORT-IMPORT BANK OF THE UNITED STATES

## FOUNDING DATE
February 2, 1934

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Executive Order 6581 (issued pursuant to National Industrial Recovery Act)

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### FRANKLIN D. ROOSEVELT (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** January 30, 1882
- **Died:** April 12, 1945
- **Career:** NY Governor; 32nd President

#### Religion
- **Episcopalian**

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Philosophy:** New Deal; government intervention in economy

### GEORGE N. PEEK (First President)

#### Background
- **Born:** November 19, 1873, Polo, Illinois
- **Died:** December 17, 1943
- **Career:** John Deere executive; WWI War Industries Board; first AAA Administrator; first Ex-Im Bank President (1934-1935)

#### Religion
- Not documented

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican → Democrat (1920s) → Republican (1936)
- **Philosophy:** Pro-agriculture; anti-production quotas ("a form of socialism"); became strongly anti-New Deal

### Famous Statement (1936)
- "The AAA became a means of buying the farmer's birthright as a preliminary to breaking down the whole individualistic system of the country"

### R. WALTON MOORE (First Chairman)

#### Background
- **Born:** February 6, 1859, Fairfax, Virginia
- **Died:** February 8, 1941
- **Career:** Virginia State Senator; six-term Congressman; Assistant Secretary of State; Ex-Im Bank Chairman

#### Religion
- **Episcopalian** - Served on vestry of Truro Episcopal Church, Fairfax

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democrat (lifelong)
- **Philosophy:** One of few Virginia politicians to embrace New Deal

---

## CONTEXT: NEW DEAL TRADE POLICY

### Original Purpose
- Facilitate trade with Soviet Union (USSR had just been recognized)
- Second bank created for Cuba trade (March 1934)
- Banks merged in 1935-1936

### Made Independent (1945)
- Export-Import Bank Act of 1945
- Became fully independent agency

---

## GEORGE PEEK'S EVOLUTION

### Political Journey
- Started as Republican
- Switched to Democrat when GOP rejected farm price supports
- Returned to Republican Party (1936) over New Deal disputes
- Joined America First Committee (1940) - opposed WWII entry

### Break with FDR
- Opposed production quotas as "planned scarcity"
- Called New Deal officials "Communists" (to Hugh Johnson)
- Campaigned for Alf Landon against FDR (1936)

---

## MISSION

### Export Finance
- Supports U.S. exports through loans and guarantees
- Fills gaps private lenders won't cover
- Helps U.S. companies compete internationally

### Self-Sustaining
- Operates at no cost to taxpayers in most years
- Charges fees and interest on loans

### Scale
- Has supported over $700 billion in U.S. exports since 1934
