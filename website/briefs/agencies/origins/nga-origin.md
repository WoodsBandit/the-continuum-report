## CREATION OF NIMA/NGA

### Gulf War Catalyst
- 1991 Gulf War exposed problems with imagery and mapping coordination
- Multiple agencies producing conflicting geospatial products
- Intelligence community recognized need for consolidation

### The Letter That Started It
- Perry, Deutch, and Shalikashvili jointly sponsored proposal
- Stated "imagery intelligence and mapping products are of growing importance"
- Recognized diverse customer base across government needed unified source

### Initial Resistance
- Many in IC and DoD opposed consolidation
- Service intelligence units resisted losing autonomy
- Deutch championed concept despite opposition

### Consolidation
- October 1, 1996: NIMA created by combining:
  - Defense Mapping Agency (DMA)
  - National Photographic Interpretation Center (NPIC)
  - Central Imagery Office (CIO)
  - Defense Dissemination Program Office (DDPO)
  - Imagery elements from CIA, DIA, NRO

---

## RENAME TO NGA (2003)

### New Discipline: GEOINT
- 2003: Congress authorized name change to NGA
- Recognized new intelligence discipline: Geospatial Intelligence (GEOINT)
- More than name change; cemented new approach to intelligence
- Established National System for Geospatial Intelligence

## HISTORICAL ROOTS

### Deep American History
- **1803:** Lewis and Clark expedition - first U.S. geospatial intelligence effort
- **1830:** Navy Depot of Charts and Instruments established
- **1972:** Defense Mapping Agency organized (direct predecessor)

### Thomas Jefferson Connection
- Ordered Lewis and Clark to explore and map Louisiana Territory
- Makes geospatial intelligence one of oldest U.S. government functions

## MISSION

### Geospatial Intelligence
- Collects, analyzes, distributes GEOINT to support national security
- Provides imagery, maps, and geospatial data
- Supports military operations, disaster response, infrastructure analysis

### Current Status
- Member of U.S. Intelligence Community
- Combat support agency within DoD
- Headquarters: Fort Belvoir North Area, Springfield, Virginia (NGA Campus East)
