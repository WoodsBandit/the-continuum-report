# ORIGIN STORY: DEPARTMENT OF VETERANS AFFAIRS

## FOUNDING DATE
October 25, 1988 (Department of Veterans Affairs Act signed)
March 15, 1989 (First Secretary sworn in)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Department of Veterans Affairs Act of 1988

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### EDWARD J. DERWINSKI (First Secretary of Veterans Affairs)

#### Background
- **Born:** September 15, 1926, Chicago, Illinois
- **Died:** January 15, 2012
- **Parents:** Sophia Zmijewski and Casimir Ignatius Derwinski
- **Education:** Loyola University of Chicago
- **Military:** World War II Army Veteran; served in Pacific theater and post-war Japan

#### Heritage
- **Polish American:** "Proud of his Polish heritage"
- Served as emissary to events in Poland for President Bush
- "Fun-loving, caring family man, who enjoyed Polish food, sing-alongs, and the Polka"

#### Religion
- Polish Catholic background (implied from heritage and Chicago upbringing)

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Illinois House:** Two terms (1952-1956)
- **U.S. Congress:** Twelve terms (1958-1982)
- **Congressional Committees:** House Foreign Affairs, Post Office, Civil Service

---

## REAGAN ADMINISTRATION SERVICE

### After Congressional Defeat
- President Reagan appointed Derwinski Counselor to State Department
- 1987: Under Secretary of State for Security Assistance, Science and Technology
- Served until end of Reagan's term

---

## CREATION OF VA DEPARTMENT

### Reagan's Surprise Announcement
- Veterans Day eve, 1987: Reagan declared support for legislation elevating Veterans Administration to cabinet department
- Ended 58 years of Veterans Administration as independent agency (since 1930)

### The Bill
- October 25, 1988: Reagan signed Department of Veterans Affairs Act
- Elevated VA to cabinet-level status

---

## APPOINTMENT AS SECRETARY

### Bush's Selection
- January 20, 1989: Day of inauguration, George H.W. Bush named Derwinski to lead department
- Qualifications: WWII Veteran, twelve-term Congressman, final head of Veterans Administration

### Senate Confirmation
- March 9, 1989: Confirmed unanimously (94-0)
- March 15, 1989: Sworn in as first Secretary

---

## KEY DECISIONS AS SECRETARY

### Agent Orange
- For many years, VA did not provide benefits to veterans claiming harm from defoliant
- Siding with veterans, Derwinski reversed government's position
- Authorized payments to veterans who suffered rare cancer linked to Agent Orange

### VA vs DVA
- Retained "VA" initialism instead of "DVA"
- Reasoning: "VA" had been familiar phrase in American culture for nearly 60 years

---

## EARLIER HISTORY: VETERANS ADMINISTRATION

### 1930 Creation
- July 21, 1930: Veterans Administration created by executive order
- Consolidated various veterans' programs under one agency
- Led by Administrator (not Secretary) for 58 years

### Frank T. Hines
- Administrator of Veterans Administration from 1930
- Long-serving leader who shaped VA operations
