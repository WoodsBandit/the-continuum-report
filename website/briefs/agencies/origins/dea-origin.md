# ORIGIN STORY: DRUG ENFORCEMENT ADMINISTRATION

## FOUNDING DATE
July 1, 1973 (DEA established)
July 28, 1973 (Reorganization Plan signed)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Reorganization Plan No. 2 of 1973

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### RICHARD M. NIXON (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** January 9, 1913, Yorba Linda, California
- **Died:** April 22, 1994
- **Education:** Whittier College; Duke Law School
- **Career:** Navy Lieutenant Commander WWII; Congressman; Senator; Vice President; President

#### Religion
- **Quaker** - raised in devout Quaker household
- Mother was particularly religious; Nixon less observant as adult

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Philosophy:** Anti-communism; "law and order"; pragmatic foreign policy
- Declared "War on Drugs" as national priority

---

## CREATION OF DEA

### Nixon's "War on Drugs"
- Nixon declared drug abuse "public enemy number one"
- Executive order established DEA to battle "an all-out global war on the drug menace"
- Created single federal agency to enforce federal drug laws

### Consolidation of Agencies
DEA merged multiple predecessor agencies:
- Bureau of Narcotics and Dangerous Drugs (BNDD)
- Office of Drug Abuse Law Enforcement (ODALE)
- Approximately 600 Special Agents from Bureau of Customs
- Customs Agency Service
- Other federal offices

### Initial Resources
- 1,470 agents at founding
- Budget of $74.9 million
- Headquarters in Washington, D.C.

---

## FIRST ADMINISTRATOR

### JOHN R. BARTELS JR.

#### Background
- **Born:** November 27, 1934
- **Career:** American lawyer; federal prosecutor
- **Position:** Confirmed October 4, 1973; served until May 30, 1975

#### Why Bartels?
- Three candidates on short list (Finlator, Ambrose, Ingersoll) all quit
- Congress and Nixon wanted someone NOT from Customs or BNDD
- Both agencies had been in "constant interagency dispute"
- Bartels was compromise choice without appearance of favoritism

#### Two Goals as Administrator
1. Integrate narcotics agents and Customs agents into one effective force
2. Restore public faith and confidence in drug law enforcement

#### Departure
- May 30, 1975: Resigned amid scandal during Ford administration

---

## CONTEXT: 1970s DRUG CRISIS

### Why DEA Was Needed
- Rising heroin epidemic
- Drug smuggling increasing
- Multiple agencies with overlapping jurisdiction
- Coordination failures hampering enforcement

### Nixon's Broader Strategy
- Comprehensive Drug Abuse Prevention and Control Act (1970)
- Controlled Substances Act established drug schedules
- DEA created to enforce these new laws

---

## MISSION

### Drug Law Enforcement
- Enforce controlled substances laws
- Investigate drug trafficking organizations
- Coordinate with international law enforcement
- Manage national drug intelligence

### Evolution
- Now approximately 10,000 employees
- Operates in all 50 states and 70+ countries
- Budget over $3 billion
