# ORIGIN STORY: DEFENSE INTELLIGENCE AGENCY

## FOUNDING DATE
October 1, 1961 (DIA began operations)
August 1, 1961 (DoD Directive 5105.21 issued)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Department of Defense Directive 5105.21

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### ROBERT S. McNAMARA (Secretary of Defense)

#### Background
- **Born:** June 9, 1916, San Francisco, California
- **Died:** July 6, 2009
- **Education:** UC Berkeley (BA Economics), Harvard Business School (MBA)
- **Career:** Ford Motor Company President before Pentagon
- **Nickname:** "The Computer" for analytical approach

#### Religion
- **Presbyterian** - raised in this tradition

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican turned Democrat
- **Philosophy:** Systems analysis; quantitative management; "best and brightest" era
- Later became critic of Vietnam War he helped escalate

---

## CREATION OF DIA

### Problems Before DIA
- Army, Navy, and Air Force each maintained separate intelligence operations
- Duplication of effort; conflicting estimates given to Secretary of Defense
- No coordination mechanism worked effectively
- Defense Reorganization Act of 1958 failed to resolve issues

### Bay of Pigs Connection
- April 1961: CIA's Bay of Pigs invasion failed catastrophically
- Exposed weaknesses in intelligence coordination
- Foundation of DIA institutionalized revoking CIA's paramilitary duties
- Military intelligence capabilities needed consolidation

### McNamara's Decision
- February 1961: Informed Joint Chiefs of decision to create DIA
- Ordered Joint Chiefs to develop implementation plan
- Met strong resistance from military service intelligence units
- Services viewed DIA as "encroachment on their turf"

---

## FIRST DIRECTOR

### LIEUTENANT GENERAL JOSEPH F. CARROLL (U.S. Air Force)

#### Background
- Air Force three-star general
- Led planning and organization of DIA
- Former FBI agent before military career
- Served as first DIA Director 1961-1969 (8 years)

#### Religion
- **Roman Catholic**

#### Politics
- Military professional; Cold War national security focus

### Starting from Nothing
- October 1, 1961: DIA born with only 25 personnel
- Operated in borrowed Pentagon space
- Built organization during Berlin Wall crisis

---

## MISSION

### Intelligence Consolidation
- Primary producer of foreign military intelligence for DoD
- Integrated fragmented intelligence efforts from military services
- Provided consistent, reliable intelligence to DoD leadership
- Centralized military intelligence management

### Evolution
- Grew from 25 to thousands of personnel
- Now one of "Big Five" U.S. intelligence agencies
- Headquarters at DIA Headquarters Building, Joint Base Anacostia-Bolling

---

## CONTEXT: COLD WAR 1961

### Turbulent Year
- January: Kennedy inaugurated
- April: Bay of Pigs disaster
- August: Berlin Wall erected
- October: DIA created amid Berlin crisis
- Soviet-U.S. tensions at peak
