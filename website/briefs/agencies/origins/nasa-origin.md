# ORIGIN STORY: NATIONAL AERONAUTICS AND SPACE ADMINISTRATION

## FOUNDING DATE
July 29, 1958 (National Aeronautics and Space Act signed)
October 1, 1958 (NASA began operations)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
National Aeronautics and Space Act of 1958

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### T. KEITH GLENNAN (First Administrator)

#### Background
- **Born:** 1905
- **Died:** 1995
- **Position:** President of Case Institute of Technology (CIT), Cleveland, Ohio
- **Prior Government Service:** Member of Atomic Energy Commission (October 1950 - November 1952)
- **Character:** "Very active in national and civic affairs"

#### Selection
- Chosen by President Eisenhower to lead the new agency
- Met with Eisenhower at White House on August 8, 1958
- Agreed to accept position on condition that Hugh L. Dryden serve as deputy

#### Religion & Politics
- Not specifically documented in search results

---

### HUGH L. DRYDEN (First Deputy Administrator)

#### Background
- **Born:** 1898
- **Died:** 1965
- **Position:** Last Director of NACA (National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics)
- **Reputation:** "Highly respected aeronautical scientist"

#### Role in NASA
- Served as Deputy Administrator to maintain continuity through NACA-to-NASA transition
- Served as Acting Administrator briefly during Eisenhower-Kennedy transition

---

### WERNHER VON BRAUN (Technical Visionary)

#### Background
- **Born:** March 23, 1912, Wirsitz, German Empire (now Poland)
- **Died:** June 16, 1977
- **WWII Role:** German scientist who developed ballistic missiles including V-2 rocket for Nazi Germany
- **Immigration:** Brought to U.S. in Operation Paperclip (program to recruit German scientists)

#### Role at NASA
- Led Army Ballistic Missile Agency team
- Agency became Marshall Space Flight Center under NASA
- Chief architect of Saturn V rocket that took Apollo astronauts to Moon

#### Religion
- **Lutheran** (later became more devout Christian after moving to America)
- Wrote religious essays about space exploration and faith

#### Political/Ethical Controversy
- His work for Nazi Germany and use of concentration camp labor in V-2 production remains controversial
- Represented both the promise and moral complexity of Cold War science

---

### DWIGHT D. EISENHOWER (President)

#### Vision for NASA
- Insisted NASA be civilian-led agency, not military
- "As an experienced general himself, Eisenhower knew the importance of separating military from civilian life"
- Believed space exploration "holds promise of adding importantly to our knowledge of the earth, the solar system, and the universe"
- Saw civilian leadership as way to establish "open skies" precedent for military satellites

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Philosophy:** Moderate conservative; believed in limited government but supported scientific advancement

---

## POLITICAL CONTEXT

### The Space Race
- October 4, 1957: Soviet Union launched Sputnik
- Sputnik "crisis" demonstrated U.S. behind in space technology
- National security and prestige at stake

### Civilian vs. Military Control
- Eisenhower deliberately created civilian agency
- Reduced rivalry between military branches competing for space program
- Established precedent that space exploration was for peaceful purposes

### NACA Transformation
- NACA (founded 1915) was aeronautics research agency
- NASA absorbed NACA personnel, facilities, and research
- Added space mission to existing aeronautics expertise
