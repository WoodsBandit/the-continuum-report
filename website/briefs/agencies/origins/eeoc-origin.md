# ORIGIN STORY: EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY COMMISSION

## FOUNDING DATE
July 2, 1964 (Civil Rights Act signed)
July 2, 1965 (EEOC became operational)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### LYNDON B. JOHNSON (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** August 27, 1908, Stonewall, Texas
- **Died:** January 22, 1973
- **Career:** Teacher; Congressman; Senator; Vice President; President

#### Religion
- **Disciples of Christ** - raised in Christian Church tradition

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democratic
- **Philosophy:** New Deal liberal; civil rights champion; Great Society architect

---

## CONGRESSIONAL CHAMPIONS

### The Longest Debate in Senate History
- June 19, 1964: After 534 hours of debate, Senate passed Civil Rights Act 73-27
- Longest debate in Senate's nearly 180-year history
- 500 amendments made to bill
- July 2, 1964: House passed; LBJ signed same evening

---

## CREATION OF EEOC

### Title VII
- Prohibited employment discrimination based on:
  - Race
  - Color
  - Religion
  - Sex
  - National origin
- Created five-member bipartisan EEOC to enforce law

### Commission Structure
- Five commissioners appointed by President, confirmed by Senate
- Five-year staggered terms
- No more than three from same political party
- Independent agency

### EEOC Opens
- July 2, 1965: Exactly one year after Act signed
- Initial budget: $2.25 million
- Initial staff: approximately 100

## CONTEXT: CIVIL RIGHTS MOVEMENT

### Building Momentum
- 1954: Brown v. Board of Education
- 1963: March on Washington; "I Have a Dream"
- 1963: Birmingham church bombing; JFK assassination
- 1964: Civil Rights Act finally passed

### LBJ's Determination
- Took up JFK's civil rights agenda
- Used political skill to overcome Southern Democratic opposition
- Called it "moral issue"

---

## MISSION AND EVOLUTION

### Original Authority
- Receive and investigate discrimination complaints
- Attempt conciliation
- Limited enforcement power initially

### Expanded Authority
- 1972: Equal Employment Opportunity Act gave EEOC litigation authority
- Added age discrimination (ADEA)
- Added disability discrimination (ADA)
- Added genetic information discrimination (GINA)
