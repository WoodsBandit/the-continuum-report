## CONTEXT: 2008 FINANCIAL CRISIS

### Why FHFA Was Created
- Housing bubble collapse
- Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac facing insolvency
- Previous regulators (OFHEO) lacked authority
- Systemic risk to financial system

### Agencies Merged Into FHFA
- Office of Federal Housing Enterprise Oversight (OFHEO)
- Federal Housing Finance Board (FHFB)
- HUD's GSE mission team

### Conservatorship
- September 6, 2008: FHFA placed Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac in conservatorship
- Largest government intervention in housing market history
- Still in conservatorship as of 2026

---

## DODD-FRANK ACT

### Further Reform (2010)
- FHFA received additional authorities
- Part of broader financial regulatory overhaul
- Named for Sen. Dodd and Rep. Frank

---

## CONSTITUTIONAL CHALLENGE

### Collins v. Yellen (2021)
- Supreme Court ruled single-director structure violated separation of powers
- Directors must be removable by President at will
- Agency itself remains valid

## MISSION

### Regulatory Oversight
- Fannie Mae
- Freddie Mac
- Federal Home Loan Banks (11 regional banks)

### Goals
- Ensure safe and sound operation
- Support housing finance and affordable housing
- Maintain liquidity in mortgage markets
