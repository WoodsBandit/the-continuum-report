# ORIGIN STORY: INTERNAL REVENUE SERVICE

## FOUNDING DATE
July 1, 1862 (Revenue Act of 1862 signed)
July 17, 1862 (First Commissioner sworn in)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Revenue Act of 1862

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### GEORGE S. BOUTWELL (First Commissioner of Internal Revenue)

#### Background
- **Born:** January 28, 1818, Brookline, Massachusetts
- **Died:** February 27, 1905
- **Career:** Politician, lawyer, statesman from Massachusetts
- **Prior Office:** 20th Governor of Massachusetts
- **Later Career:** Secretary of the Treasury under President Ulysses S. Grant

#### Politics
- **Party Evolution:** Free-Soil Democrat before becoming Republican
- **Philosophy:** Abolitionist; champion of African American citizenship and suffrage
- **Key Role:** Instrumental in drafting and passage of Fourteenth and Fifteenth Amendments

#### Views on America / Slavery
- At "Great War Meeting" at U.S. Capitol (August 6, 1862), gave speech titled "Treason the Fruit of Slavery"
- Declared: "If not for slavery, there would have been no treason; and when slavery should cease to exist, there will be no traitors. That is the beginning and the end of the war, slavery in the beginning, freedom in the end"
- **Notable:** Only one of eleven speakers (including Lincoln) who mentioned slavery or emancipation at this meeting
- Speech before 10,000 people helped prepare public opinion for Emancipation Proclamation

#### Connection to Lincoln
- Hosted Republican party's Thomas Jefferson birthday dinner in Boston (April 1859)
- Event helped propel Abraham Lincoln to presidency
- Lincoln sent letter expressing conviction: "he who would be no slave, must consent to have no slave"

#### Building the IRS
- Summer 1862: Arrived in Washington when "Union morale was low" and McClellan was retreating
- Started with three clerks in basement office of Treasury building
- Worked "seven days a week, nights and Sabbaths"
- By spring 1863: Built operation of 3,000+ revenue assessors and collectors
- Brought in tens of millions of dollars for Union war effort

---

### ABRAHAM LINCOLN (President)

#### Role
- Signed Revenue Act of 1862 on July 1, 1862
- Created income tax to fund Civil War

#### Context
- Treasury Secretary Salmon P. Chase originally planned to fund war with loans
- Early Union losses threatened investor confidence
- Massive tax increase needed to persuade lenders of nation's solvency

---

### SALMON P. CHASE (Secretary of the Treasury)

#### Role
- Oversaw Treasury during Civil War
- Recognized need for tax revenue when borrowing became difficult
- Later became Chief Justice of Supreme Court

---

## HISTORICAL CONTEXT

### The First Income Tax
- First federal income tax in American history
- Created out of necessity to fund Civil War
- Intended as temporary (income tax later repealed after war)
- Progressive structure: Different rates for different income levels

### Evolution
- Civil War income tax ended in 1872
- 16th Amendment (1913) permanently authorized federal income tax
- IRS as modern agency evolved from this Civil War foundation
