# ORIGIN STORY: DEPARTMENT OF DEFENSE

## FOUNDING DATE
September 18, 1947 (National Security Act)
1949 (Renamed from "National Military Establishment" to "Department of Defense")

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
National Security Act of 1947

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### JAMES V. FORRESTAL (First Secretary of Defense)

#### Background
- **Born:** February 15, 1892, Matteawan, New York (now Beacon)
- **Died:** May 22, 1949 (suicide at Bethesda Naval Hospital)
- **Family:** Father James Forrestal was Irish immigrant who "dabbled in politics"; mother Mary Anne Toohey was daughter of another Irish immigrant

#### Education
- High school in New York
- Dartmouth College (1911)
- Princeton University (1912-1915) - left few credits short of degree due to academic and financial difficulties

#### Early Career
- Financial reporter
- Clerk for zinc company
- Bond salesman
- Made fortune on Wall Street
- Partner at Dillon, Read & Co. (1923)
- Vice president (1926)
- President of company (1937)

#### Religion
- **Background:** "Very strict middle-class Irish Catholic family"
- **Mother's Hopes:** Wanted him to become a priest
- **Personal Practice:** "Drifted away from the church" after leaving home
- **Torment:** Biographer Rogow noted Forrestal was "tormented by his Roman Catholic religion"

#### Politics
- **Party:** Usually voted Democratic but "did not support New Deal liberalism"
- **Local Activity:** Publicist for Democratic Party committee in Dutchess County, New York
- **Philosophy:** Cold War hardliner; intensely hostile to Soviet Union

---

## PATH TO DEFENSE SECRETARY

### Navy Service
- June 1940: Administrative assistant to President FDR
- August 1940: Under Secretary of the Navy
- May 1944: Secretary of the Navy (after Frank Knox's death)

### Opposition to Unification
- **Initially Opposed:** Truman's plan to unify Army and Navy
- **Concerns:**
  - Department would be too large for meaningful oversight
  - Secretary would rely too heavily on military advisors
  - Unification would shift defense focus from naval carriers to Air Force bombers

### The Compromise
- When Truman persisted, Forrestal enlisted Ferdinand Eberstadt to develop compromise plan
- Eberstadt's plan formed basis for National Security Act of 1947
- Created "National Military Establishment" (later renamed Department of Defense)
- Also established National Security Council and Central Intelligence Agency

### Appointment
- September 17, 1947: Sworn in as first Secretary of Defense
- Truman's first choice (Secretary of War Patterson) declined
- Forrestal's initial powers were limited; 1949 amendments strengthened position

---

## FOREIGN POLICY VIEWS

### Cold War Stance
- Intensely hostile to Soviet Union
- Feared Communist expansion in Europe and Middle East

### Israel Controversy
- Along with George Marshall, opposed U.S. support for State of Israel
- Feared alienating Arab nations needed for oil
- Favored federalization plan for Palestine instead of partition

### Defense Budget Tensions
- Wanted higher defense spending
- Truman insisted on balanced budget
- Source of significant friction between Forrestal and President

---

## TRAGIC END

- Resigned March 1949
- Hospitalized for severe depression
- May 22, 1949: Committed suicide at National Naval Medical Center, Bethesda
- Truman: "This able and devoted public servant was as truly a casualty of the war as if he had died on the firing line"
