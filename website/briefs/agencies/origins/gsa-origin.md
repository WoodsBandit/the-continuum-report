# ORIGIN STORY: GENERAL SERVICES ADMINISTRATION

## FOUNDING DATE
July 1, 1949

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Federal Property and Administrative Services Act of 1949

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### HARRY S. TRUMAN (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** May 8, 1884, Lamar, Missouri
- **Died:** December 26, 1972
- **Career:** Farmer; haberdasher; Senator; Vice President; President

#### Religion
- **Baptist**

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democratic
- **Philosophy:** Government efficiency; Hoover Commission supporter

### HERBERT HOOVER (Commission Chair)

#### Background
- **Born:** August 10, 1874, West Branch, Iowa
- **Died:** October 20, 1964
- **Career:** Mining engineer; humanitarian; Commerce Secretary; President (1929-1933)

#### Religion
- **Quaker**

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Philosophy:** Efficiency; rationalization; limited but effective government

### Hoover Commission
- 1947: Truman asked Hoover to lead commission on government reorganization
- Commission on Organization of the Executive Branch of the Government
- Recommendations led to GSA creation
- Bipartisan reform effort

---

## POST-WWII CHALLENGE

### The Problem
- After WWII: Government possessed vast surplus goods, properties, equipment
- Multiple agencies independently managed properties, acquisitions, supplies
- Fragmented system created inefficiencies
- Administrative bottlenecks hindered government operations

### Hoover Commission Solution
- Consolidate administrative functions
- Create central agency for supplies, property, records
- Eliminate duplication
- Apply business efficiency to government

---

## CREATION OF GSA

### Federal Property and Administrative Services Act
- July 1, 1949: Truman signed into law
- Created GSA as independent agency
- Consolidated multiple functions and agencies

### Agencies Merged Into GSA
- National Archives Establishment
- Federal Works Agency
- Public Buildings Administration
- Bureau of Federal Supply
- Office of Contract Settlement
- War Assets Administration

---

## FIRST ADMINISTRATOR

### JESS LARSON

#### Background
- Administrator of War Assets Administration
- Named first GSA Administrator
- Led agency through formative years

#### Religion
- Not documented in sources

#### Politics
- Truman appointee

---

## FIRST MAJOR PROJECT

### White House Renovation
- By 1949: White House in dangerous disrepair
- Inspector said it was standing "purely from habit"
- GSA's first major job: Complete renovation
- Worked closely with President and Mrs. Truman
- Completed 1952
- Demonstrated agency capability

---

## ORIGINAL MISSION

### Wide-Ranging Functions
- Dispose of war surplus goods
- Manage and store government records
- Handle emergency preparedness
- Stockpile strategic supplies
- Regulate office supplies to federal agencies
- Manage federal buildings

### Unusual Operations
- Hemp plantations in South America
- Strategic material stockpiling
- War surplus disposal globally

---

## EVOLUTION

### Modern GSA
- Federal buildings and real estate
- Government-wide procurement
- Technology services
- Motor vehicle fleet
- Travel and relocation services
- Sustainability initiatives

### Federal Marketplace
- GSA Schedules (procurement contracts)
- Serves as "landlord" for federal government
- Manages 8,600+ federal buildings
- Acquires $75 billion+ in goods/services annually
