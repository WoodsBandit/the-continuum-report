# ORIGIN STORY: UNITED STATES POSTAL SERVICE

## FOUNDING DATE
July 26, 1775 (Continental Congress established postal system)
February 20, 1792 (Postal Act made it permanent)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Continental Congress Resolution (1775); Postal Act of 1792

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### BENJAMIN FRANKLIN (First Postmaster General)

#### Background
- **Born:** January 17, 1706, Boston, Massachusetts
- **Died:** April 17, 1790, Philadelphia
- **Career by 1737:** Already prosperous as printer, shopkeeper, and publisher of The Pennsylvania Gazette

#### Postal Experience
- 1737: Appointed postmaster of Philadelphia (age 31) after predecessor failed to submit financial reports
- 1753: Became one of two joint postmasters general for the colonies
- Made numerous improvements to colonial mail system:
  - Set up new, more efficient colonial routes
  - Cut delivery time in half between Philadelphia and New York
  - Had weekly mail wagon travel day and night via relay teams

#### Religion
- **Deist** - Believed in God as creator but not in divine intervention or miracles
- One of most famous American Deists alongside Thomas Jefferson
- Pragmatic religious tolerance

#### Politics
- **Philosophy:** Practical problem-solver; believed in self-improvement and public service
- **Constitutional Role:** Attended Constitutional Convention in 1787; lived to see Constitution adoption in 1789
- **Revolutionary:** Founding Father; worked on Declaration of Independence committee (1776)

#### Views on America
- Helped create foundational American institutions
- Believed informed citizenry essential to democracy
- Saw postal system as tool for unifying colonies/states

#### First Postmaster General (1775)
- July 26, 1775: Continental Congress appointed Franklin first Postmaster General
- Annual salary: $1,000 plus $340 for secretary and comptroller
- Patriots saw robust mail system as critical to nation's welfare
- Postal network facilitated communication among army commanders and elected representatives

#### Later Role
- Fall 1776: Left for Paris to secure French support for war with England
- Entrusted General Post Office to son-in-law Richard Bache

---

## THE POSTAL ACT OF 1792

### Architects
- James Madison and others designed the 1792 system
- Saw how post could support "fledgling democracy by informing the electorate"

### Key Provisions
- High-priced postage for letters subsidized delivery of cheap newspapers
- Newspaper printers could send each other newspapers for FREE
- Facilitated spread of national and foreign news from seat of government
- Established permanence of Postal Service as federal institution

### Democratic Purpose
- News considered crucial to informed electorate
- Subsidized newspapers represented commitment to democratic discourse
- Explicitly designed to spread political information widely

---

## CONSTITUTIONAL BASIS

### Postal Clause
- U.S. Constitution (1787) Article I, Section 8: Congress empowered "To establish Post Offices and post Roads"
- Postal Act of 1792 exercised these powers

---

## LATER TRANSFORMATION

### Postal Reorganization Act of 1970
- Signed by President Richard Nixon
- Transformed Post Office Department into United States Postal Service
- Made USPS independent agency rather than cabinet department
- Postmaster General no longer cabinet member
