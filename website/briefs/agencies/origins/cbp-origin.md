# ORIGIN STORY: U.S. CUSTOMS AND BORDER PROTECTION

## FOUNDING DATE
March 1, 2003

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Homeland Security Act of 2002

---

## DEEP HISTORICAL ROOTS

### Fifth Act of First Congress (1789)
- July 31, 1789: Original Customs Service established
- One of first acts of new U.S. government
- Customs duties funded 80-95% of federal budget until Civil War

### Immigration Inspection (1891)
- March 3, 1891: Office of Superintendent of Immigration established
- Ellis Island era began

### Border Patrol (1924)
- May 28, 1924: Congress first authorized Border Patrol
- Initially focused on Mexican and Canadian borders

---

## KEY FOUNDER (MODERN CBP)

### ROBERT C. BONNER (First Commissioner)

#### Background
- **Born:** January 29, 1942
- **Education:** Georgetown University Law
- **Career:** Federal prosecutor; U.S. District Judge; DEA Administrator

#### Religion
- Not documented in sources

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- Appointed by both Republican and Democratic administrations

### Path to CBP
- June 24, 2001: Nominated as Commissioner of U.S. Customs Service
- September 19, 2001: Confirmed (8 days after 9/11 attacks)
- March 2003: Became first CBP Commissioner when agency created
- Served until December 2005

---

## CREATION OF CBP

### 9/11 Catalyst
- September 11, 2001 attacks exposed border security gaps
- National Commission on Terrorist Attacks (9/11 Commission) recommended reform
- President Bush proposed Department of Homeland Security (June 6, 2002)

### Four Legacy Agencies Merged
1. **U.S. Customs Service** (from Treasury)
2. **Immigration and Naturalization Service inspection functions** (from DOJ)
3. **Border Patrol** (from INS/DOJ)
4. **Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service** (from USDA)

### Largest DHS Merger
- Nearly 60,000 employees affected
- First unified border agency in U.S. history
- "Nation's first comprehensive border, travel and trade agency"

---

## BONNER'S INITIATIVES

### Security Innovations
- **National Targeting Center** - risk-based screening
- **Container Security Initiative (CSI)** - pre-screening at foreign ports
- **Customs-Trade Partnership Against Terrorism (C-TPAT)** - trusted shipper program

### Philosophy
- Balance security with trade facilitation
- Push borders outward through international cooperation
- Risk-based targeting rather than random inspection

---

## MISSION

### Border Security
- Secure U.S. borders (land, sea, air)
- Prevent terrorists and weapons from entering
- Apprehend individuals attempting illegal entry

### Trade and Travel
- Facilitate legitimate trade and travel
- Enforce trade laws and regulations
- Collect customs duties

### Agriculture Protection
- Prevent introduction of pests and diseases
- Protect American agriculture

---

## CURRENT STATUS

### Largest Law Enforcement Agency
- Over 60,000 employees
- 328 ports of entry
- 20 field offices
- Operates on all U.S. borders

### Components
- Office of Field Operations (ports of entry)
- U.S. Border Patrol
- Air and Marine Operations
- Office of Trade
