# ORIGIN STORY: DEPARTMENT OF LABOR

## FOUNDING DATE
March 4, 1913 (established as separate Cabinet department)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Bill signed by President William Howard Taft on his last day as President; first Secretary appointed by Woodrow Wilson

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### WILLIAM B. WILSON (First Secretary of Labor)

#### Background
- **Born:** April 2, 1862, Blantyre, Lanarkshire, Scotland
- **Died:** May 25, 1934
- **Family:** Third child of Adam Black Wilson and Helen Nelson (Bauchop) Wilson; first to survive early childhood
- **Father's Occupation:** Coal miner

#### Immigration Story
- February 1868: During mining strike, family evicted from company-owned home as company suppressed strike
- Father traveled Scotland unsuccessfully seeking work
- Ultimately emigrated to United States
- Settled in Arnot, Tioga County, Pennsylvania (bituminous coal region)

#### Labor Organizing
- **Age 12 (1874):** First labor organizing - attempted to launch union for boys who worked as "trappers" (manual ventilation operators in mines)
- When fledgling union threatened strike over wage reduction, he was paddled by foreman and strike broken
- Later became founder of United Mine Workers of America (UMWA)
- 1900: Elected secretary-treasurer of UMWA (served until 1908)

#### Religion
- **Not Catholic** but worked during Gilded Age and Progressive era alongside many Catholic labor leaders including T.V. Powderly, John Mitchell, John W. Hayes, and "Mother" Jones

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democratic
- **Philosophy:** Labor advocate; believed in workers' rights and union organizing
- Unsuccessful campaigns: Pennsylvania state legislature (1888), U.S. House (1892)
- 1906: Won U.S. House seat as Democrat in staunchly Republican district
- Known as "labor's best friend" during House tenure (1907-1913)

#### Congressional Record
- Chairman of: Committee on Labor, Committee on Mines, Committee on Merchant Marine and Fisheries
- Introduced legislation creating Bureau of Mines
- Introduced legislation establishing Department of Labor

#### As Secretary of Labor (1913-1921)
- Appointed by President Woodrow Wilson on his first day in office (March 5, 1913)
- Served entire Wilson administration through WWI
- Created United States Conciliation Service for labor dispute mediation
- Named to U.S. Department of Labor's Labor Hall of Fame (2007)

---

## WOODROW WILSON (President)

#### Role
- Appointed William B. Wilson (no relation) as first Secretary
- Supported labor movement as part of Progressive agenda

---

## HISTORICAL CONTEXT

### Path to Cabinet Status
- 1903: Department of Commerce and Labor created
- 1913: Split into separate departments
- Labor advocates had long sought independent Cabinet representation
