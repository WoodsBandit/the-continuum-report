## The Public Record

### Criminal Charges and Conviction

**United States v. Keith Raniere, Case No. 18-CR-204 (E.D.N.Y.):**

Keith Raniere and five co-defendants were indicted in 2018 and 2019 for racketeering, racketeering conspiracy, and related crimes. On June 19, 2019, Raniere was convicted of all seven counts and all eleven racketeering acts.

**Government Sentencing Memorandum (August 27, 2020):**

> "In 2018 and 2019, Keith Raniere and five co-defendants were indicted for racketeering, racketeering conspiracy and related crimes, including sex trafficking, forced labor, alien smuggling, identity theft and extortion."

### Categories of Criminal Conduct

The government identified the following categories of illegal conduct:

1. Sexual exploitation of Camila (Jane Doe 2) — a minor
2. Alien smuggling and visa fraud
3. Trafficking of Daniela (Jane Doe 4) for labor and services
4. Unlawful surveillance of individuals believed to be enemies
5. Obstruction of justice
6. Sex trafficking, wire fraud, and extortion related to DOS
7. Identity theft related to tax evasion

### The Enterprise Structure

**Government Sentencing Memorandum:**

> "The evidence presented at trial demonstrated that for over a decade, Raniere led a criminal enterprise ('the Enterprise') and relied on an 'inner circle' of individuals to carry out his orders."

> "Raniere and his co-conspirators recruited individuals into various purported self-help organizations that Raniere founded, including Nxivm and affiliated programs, and DOS."

### Control Mechanisms

The court record documented systematic control mechanisms:

> "Raniere and his co-conspirators maintained control over the Enterprise by, among other means, obtaining sensitive information about members and associates of the Enterprise; inducing shame and guilt in order to influence and control members and associates of the Enterprise; isolating associates and others from friends and family and making them dependent on the Enterprise for their financial well-being and legal status within the United States."

### DOS and "Collateral"

The government documented the "DOS" organization within NXIVM:

> "Members of the Enterprise recruited and groomed sexual partners for Raniere, both within and outside of DOS, and many were themselves in sexual relationships with Raniere that involved pledges of loyalty, penances for 'ethical breaches,' and collateral."

DOS required members to provide "collateral" — compromising photographs, damaging information about family members, or other material that could be used to coerce compliance.

### Specific Victim Accounts

**Regarding Camila (then age 15):**

> "In September 2005, the defendant began a sexual relationship with Camila, then a fifteen-year-old child."

> "On November 2, 2005 and again on November 24, 2005, the defendant took photographs of Camila constituting child pornography."

**Regarding Daniela (confined for nearly two years):**

> "Between March 2010 and April 2012, Raniere, Lauren Salzman, and others trafficked Daniela for labor and services by confining her to a room for nearly two years on the threat of being sent to Mexico and withholding her birth certificate."

### Co-Defendants

Five co-defendants pleaded guilty:
- **Allison Mack** — Actress, DOS first-line master
- **Lauren Salzman** — NXIVM executive, DOS first-line master
- **Nancy Salzman** — NXIVM co-founder ("Prefect")
- **Clare Bronfman** — NXIVM benefactor, Seagram's heiress
- **Kathy Russell** — NXIVM bookkeeper

### Sentence

Keith Raniere was sentenced to 120 years in federal prison on October 27, 2020.
