The settlement's explicit non-admission of liability supports this reading.

3. **Estate administration success interpretation:** Under Kahn's co-administration, the estate has distributed over $250 million to victims and government entities. One might view this as evidence of faithful fiduciary execution—transforming assets derived from criminal conduct into compensation for those harmed.

4. **Property market interpretation:** The discounted property sales may reflect market conditions, property stigma, and practical necessity rather than administrative failures. All major properties have been liquidated, generating substantial proceeds for victims.

5. **Timing coincidence interpretation:** The will signing two days before Epstein's death could reflect longstanding estate planning that happened to conclude shortly before an unexpected event, rather than foreknowledge or unusual circumstances.

6. **Beneficiary status interpretation:** Naming professional advisors as trust beneficiaries, while uncommon, is not illegal or inherently improper. This may reflect Epstein's personal wishes rather than any improper arrangement.

7. **Information limitation interpretation:** Kahn's claimed uncertainty about trust distribution intentions may be genuine. Complex estate documents created by others can present interpretation challenges for even sophisticated professionals tasked with administration.
