## The Documented Record

**The following contains only direct quotes and factual citations. No interpretation.**

### The INSLAW Affair Congressional Investigation

The House Judiciary Committee published "The INSLAW Affair" in September 1992, documenting a multi-year investigation into allegations that the Department of Justice misappropriated PROMIS (Prosecutor's Management Information System) software developed by INSLAW, Inc.

### Allegations Examined by Congress

The House Judiciary Committee report examined testimony and affidavits alleging that:

1. PROMIS software was modified to include covert surveillance capabilities
2. Modified versions were allegedly distributed to foreign intelligence services
3. Israeli intelligence (Mossad) was among the alleged recipients of modified PROMIS software

### Ari Ben-Menashe Testimony

The committee examined testimony from Ari Ben-Menashe, who claimed to be a former Israeli military intelligence officer. Ben-Menashe alleged that Israeli intelligence was involved in the distribution of modified PROMIS software to foreign governments.

The House Judiciary Committee report noted the examination of these allegations without reaching definitive conclusions regarding their accuracy.

### Congressional Findings

The September 1992 House Judiciary Committee report stated that the investigation encountered significant obstacles, including:
- Department of Justice non-cooperation
- Missing or unavailable witnesses
- Classified information restrictions

The committee found sufficient evidence to warrant further investigation but could not definitively confirm or refute the intelligence distribution allegations.
