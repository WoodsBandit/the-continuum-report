## The Documented Record

**The following contains only direct quotes and factual citations. No interpretation.**

### Source: United States v. Keith Raniere, Case No. 18-CR-204 (E.D.N.Y.)

Clare Bronfman was arrested on July 24, 2018, as part of the federal prosecution of NXIVM leadership. She was initially charged with racketeering conspiracy, conspiracy to commit identity theft, and money laundering.

The superseding indictment named Bronfman alongside Keith Raniere, Allison Mack, Nancy Salzman, Lauren Salzman, and Kathy Russell as co-defendants in the NXIVM prosecution.

On April 19, 2019, Bronfman pleaded guilty to two counts:
- Conspiracy to conceal and harbor illegal aliens for financial gain
- Fraudulent use of identification

The plea agreement resolved all pending charges against Bronfman without requiring her to testify against co-defendants.

Judge Nicholas G. Garaufis presided over Bronfman's sentencing on September 30, 2020. Prosecutors requested a sentence of 60 months. The defense requested a sentence of probation or home confinement.

Judge Garaufis sentenced Bronfman to 81 months in federal prison, exceeding the prosecution's recommendation. The court also imposed a $500,000 fine and forfeiture of $6 million.

During sentencing, Judge Garaufis stated that Bronfman remained loyal to Raniere despite overwhelming evidence of his crimes and showed no remorse for victims.

Bronfman reported to federal prison on February 1, 2021, and is incarcerated at FCI Dublin in California. Her projected release date is September 2026.
