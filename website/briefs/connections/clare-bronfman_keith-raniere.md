## The Documented Record

**The following contains only direct quotes and factual citations. No interpretation.**

### Source: United States v. Keith Raniere, Case No. 18-CR-204 (E.D.N.Y.)

Clare Bronfman, heiress to the Seagram liquor fortune, was a key financial backer of Keith Raniere and his organization NXIVM. Federal prosecutors established that Bronfman provided substantial financial resources that enabled NXIVM's operations over many years.

Bronfman joined NXIVM in approximately 2003 and became one of its most prominent and wealthy members. Court records indicate she contributed tens of millions of dollars to support Raniere's various enterprises and legal defense funds.

On April 19, 2019, Clare Bronfman pleaded guilty to conspiracy to conceal and harbor illegal aliens for financial gain and fraudulent use of identification. She was sentenced on September 30, 2020 to 81 months (6 years and 9 months) in federal prison.

Federal prosecutors documented that Bronfman funded litigation against perceived enemies of NXIVM and Raniere, using her wealth to pursue legal actions against former members and critics of the organization.

Keith Raniere was convicted on June 19, 2019 of racketeering, racketeering conspiracy, wire fraud conspiracy, forced labor conspiracy, sex trafficking, sex trafficking conspiracy, and attempted sex trafficking. He was sentenced to 120 years in federal prison.
