### Successful Prosecution vs. Remaining Questions

Maxwell's conviction represents successful FBI prosecution of an Epstein network participant. Unlike the 2005-2007 investigation that resulted in NPA and state plea, the 2019-2021 investigation resulted in federal conviction and substantial prison sentence.

**What Changed?**
- Different U.S. Attorney's Office (SDNY vs. SDFL)
- Different political context (post-Miami Herald reporting)
- Epstein's death eliminating primary defendant
- Media attention and public pressure

### The Remaining Co-Conspirators

Maxwell was the only Epstein associate federally prosecuted. The July 2019 FBI email identifying 10 co-conspirators raises questions:

**Why Only Maxwell?**
- Was Maxwell the only prosecutable co-conspirator?
- Did evidence against others not meet threshold?
- Were deals made with other co-conspirators?
- Did Maxwell's trial testimony implicate others?

The public record does not answer these questions.

### FBI 302 Reports

FBI 302 interview reports contain detailed witness statements. These documents:
- Record what witnesses told FBI
- Potentially name additional perpetrators
- Contain allegations not part of trial record

**Why Sealed?**
- Standard practice for ongoing investigations?
- Protection of witness privacy?
- Evidence of additional crimes?

The sealing of 302 reports limits public accountability review.
