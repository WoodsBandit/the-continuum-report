### Source: United States v. Keith Raniere, Case No. 18-CR-204 (E.D.N.Y.)

Allison Mack and Clare Bronfman were both named as co-defendants in the federal prosecution of NXIVM leadership. The superseding indictment filed in the Eastern District of New York charged both women alongside Keith Raniere, Nancy Salzman, Lauren Salzman, and Kathy Russell.

Both Mack and Bronfman were high-ranking members of NXIVM. Court records indicate they held leadership positions within the organization and were part of Keith Raniere's inner circle.

Mack joined NXIVM in approximately 2006, while Bronfman joined in approximately 2003. Both women remained active members until the federal investigation led to arrests in 2018.

Clare Bronfman's financial resources helped fund NXIVM's operations, including legal defense funds and litigation against critics. This funding supported the organization in which both women held leadership roles.

Both defendants entered guilty pleas in 2019:
- Allison Mack pleaded guilty on April 8, 2019 to racketeering and racketeering conspiracy
- Clare Bronfman pleaded guilty on April 19, 2019 to conspiracy to conceal and harbor illegal aliens and fraudulent use of identification

Neither defendant testified against the other or against Keith Raniere at his trial.

Mack received a sentence of three years in federal prison on June 30, 2021. Bronfman received a sentence of 81 months (6 years, 9 months) on September 30, 2020. The disparity in sentences reflected different charges, cooperation levels, and judicial assessments of remorse and rehabilitation.
