## Alternative Interpretations

Reasonable observers might interpret this evidence differently:

1. **Legitimate Business Tainted by Association:** MC2 may have functioned primarily as a legitimate modeling agency, with any criminal activity representing Brunel's personal conduct separate from the business operations. The major retail clients who worked with MC2 apparently conducted sufficient due diligence to continue business relationships.

2. **Investment vs. Control:** The $1-2 million from Epstein could be interpreted as a standard investment in a modeling agency by a wealthy financier, rather than evidence of operational control over trafficking activities. Many legitimate businesses receive investments from individuals later revealed to have committed crimes.

3. **Incomplete Evidence:** The documented record relies heavily on testimony from Virginia Giuffre and civil court filings, which represent allegations rather than proven facts. Brunel denied wrongdoing, and his death before trial means these allegations were never adjudicated.

4. **Industry-Wide Problems:** The issues attributed to MC2 may reflect broader problems in the modeling industry rather than a uniquely criminal enterprise. Multiple modeling agencies have faced similar allegations about exploitation, suggesting structural rather than individual causes.

5. **Post-Hoc Attribution:** The interpretation of "MC2" as symbolically representing Epstein may be a retrospective construction. The name could have legitimate alternative explanations, and assigning sinister meaning after the fact may represent confirmation bias.
