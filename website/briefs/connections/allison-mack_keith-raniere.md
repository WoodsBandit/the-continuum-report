## The Documented Record

**The following contains only direct quotes and factual citations. No interpretation.**

### Source: United States v. Keith Raniere, Case No. 18-CR-204 (E.D.N.Y.)

Allison Mack, an actress known for her role on the television series "Smallville," became a member of NXIVM in approximately 2006. Court records establish that she rose to become one of Keith Raniere's closest associates within the organization.

Federal prosecutors documented that Mack was a leader within DOS (Dominus Obsequious Sororium), a secret society within NXIVM that operated as a master-slave hierarchy. Raniere was identified as the sole "grandmaster" at the top of this hierarchy, with Mack serving as one of the "first-line masters" directly beneath him.

Court evidence established that Mack recruited women into DOS, obtained "collateral" from them (including compromising photographs and damaging information about family members), and directed their activities under Raniere's ultimate authority.

Mack was arrested on April 20, 2018. She was charged with sex trafficking, sex trafficking conspiracy, and forced labor conspiracy.

On April 8, 2019, Allison Mack pleaded guilty to racketeering and racketeering conspiracy charges. During her plea allocution, Mack stated that she had obtained "collateral" from DOS members and that she knew this was wrong.

Keith Raniere was convicted on June 19, 2019 of racketeering, sex trafficking, and other charges. He was sentenced to 120 years in federal prison on October 27, 2020.

Mack was sentenced on June 30, 2021 to three years in federal prison. She was released from prison on July 10, 2023 after completing her sentence.
