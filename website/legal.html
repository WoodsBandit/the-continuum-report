Editorial Standards

Our methodology, source standards, and legal notice

Mission Statement

The Continuum Report is an independent, open-source intelligence research project dedicated to aggregating, organizing, and analyzing publicly available documents concerning matters of significant public interest. Our work focuses on documented connections between institutions, individuals, and events as revealed through primary source materials.

"For there is nothing hidden that will not be disclosed, and nothing concealed that will not be known or brought out into the open." — Luke 8:17

We believe that public access to organized, well-sourced information strengthens democratic accountability and enables citizens to understand the systems that shape their world.

Nature of This Publication

The Continuum Report constitutes protected editorial and journalistic activity under the First Amendment to the United States Constitution. This project engages in:

  • Aggregation of publicly available documents including court filings, depositions, government records, FOIA releases, and published investigative journalism
  • Organization and indexing of information already in the public domain
  • Analysis and commentary on matters of public concern
  • Synthesis of documented connections across multiple sources

We do not conduct original investigations, surveillance, or evidence gathering. We organize and present what already exists in the public record.

Source Standards

Primary Sources

We prioritize primary source documentation:

  • Court filings and judicial records — complaints, motions, depositions, exhibits, and rulings from federal and state proceedings
  • Government documents — FOIA releases, congressional testimony, official reports, and declassified materials
  • Corporate filings — SEC disclosures, incorporation records, and financial statements
  • Official records — flight logs, property records, and other documented transactions

Secondary Sources

When primary sources are unavailable or require context, we reference:

  • Published investigative journalism from established outlets
  • Academic research and scholarly publications
  • Books by credentialed investigative authors

Attribution Standard

Every factual claim in our publications is attributed to its source. We do not present sourced information as original fact. The source is always identified.

Distinguishing Fact, Allegation, and Analysis

We maintain clear distinctions between categories of information:

Documented Facts — Events, transactions, relationships, or statements that are established in official records and not in dispute. Example: "Flight logs show [Person] traveled on [Date]."

Allegations — Claims made by parties in legal proceedings, journalistic reports, or other sources that have not been proven or adjudicated. Example: "The plaintiff alleged in court filings that..." We report allegations as allegations, not as established truth.

Analysis & Commentary — Our editorial interpretation of patterns, connections, or implications derived from documented sources. This represents opinion and synthesis, not factual assertion. Example: "The documented pattern suggests..." or "This raises questions about..."

Inclusion of any individual in our research does not imply guilt, wrongdoing, or criminal conduct. Many individuals appear in court documents as witnesses, associates, or tangentially mentioned parties. Presence in our database reflects presence in source documents, nothing more.

AI-Assisted Research

The Continuum Report utilizes artificial intelligence tools to assist with:

  • Organization and categorization of large document sets
  • Extraction of entities (names, dates, locations) from source materials
  • Synthesis of information across multiple documents
  • Drafting of analytical summaries for human review

AI Limitations Acknowledged: AI systems can make errors. All AI-assisted output is reviewed for accuracy against source materials. AI is used as a research tool, not as an original source of facts. Any specific factual claim in our publications traces back to a human-verifiable source document, not to AI generation.

Public Figures Standard

Much of our research concerns public figures — individuals who have assumed roles of public prominence through politics, business, or other public activity. Under established First Amendment jurisprudence (New York Times Co. v. Sullivan, 376 U.S. 254 (1964)), commentary on public figures regarding matters of public concern receives the highest level of constitutional protection.

We report on public figures' documented actions, associations, and statements as revealed in public records. We do not intrude into purely private matters unrelated to their public roles.

Fair Report Privilege

Under the fair report privilege recognized in American law, we may report on the contents of official proceedings and public records — including allegations made in court filings — without liability for the underlying truth of those allegations, provided our report is fair and accurate.

When we report that "Plaintiff alleged X in court filing Y," we are exercising this privilege. We are reporting what the document contains, which is verifiable fact, regardless of whether the allegation itself is true.

Corrections Policy

We are committed to accuracy. If we have made a factual error — misquoted a document, misattributed a claim, or materially misrepresented source material — we will correct it promptly upon verification.

To request a correction:

  • Email contact@thecontinuumreport.com
  • Identify the specific content at issue
  • Provide documentation supporting the correction

We will review all correction requests in good faith and respond within a reasonable timeframe. Corrections will be noted transparently in the affected content.

What does not warrant correction: Disagreement with our analysis, opinion, or editorial framing is not grounds for correction. We correct factual errors, not interpretive differences.

Legal Notice

First Amendment Protected Activity

The Continuum Report is a publication engaged in journalism, research, and commentary on matters of public concern. This activity is protected by the First Amendment to the United States Constitution and applicable state constitutional provisions.

This publication constitutes the aggregation and analysis of publicly available information. We make no accusations of criminal conduct. We report what public documents contain and offer analysis clearly labeled as such.

Notice to potential litigants: Florida Statute § 768.295 provides for recovery of attorney's fees and costs by defendants in actions arising from the exercise of constitutional free speech rights in connection with a public issue.

Independence

The Continuum Report is an independent project. We are not affiliated with any government agency, political organization, or commercial interest related to our subject matter. We do not accept payment or consideration from subjects of our research. Our analysis is conducted without favor.
