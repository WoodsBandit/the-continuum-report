## Alternative Interpretations

A reader reviewing these same documents might reasonably conclude:

- **Employee limitations:** Employees may not understand the full context of what they observe. Alessi's interpretations of events may be colored by subsequent knowledge he did not have during employment.

- **Retrospective testimony:** Testimony given years after events may be affected by memory limitations, media coverage, or knowledge gained after the fact.

- **Legal pressure:** Alessi may have cooperated with prosecutors to avoid potential liability, which could affect the nature of his testimony.

- **Observation vs. knowledge:** Observing visitors at a residence does not establish knowledge of criminal activity. Household staff may be deliberately kept unaware of certain activities.

- **Litigation context:** Deposition testimony is given in an adversarial context where attorney questions shape the narrative.
