## Editorial Analysis

**The following represents the opinions and interpretations of The Continuum Report.**

The Taylor-Kellen connection exists through their parallel positions in the Epstein network:

**Similar roles:** Both women occupied assistant/administrative positions in proximity to Epstein and Maxwell. Taylor was Maxwell's assistant; Kellen was described as part of Epstein's staff.

**Different legal statuses:** Kellen was designated an unindicted co-conspirator in the 2008 NPA; Taylor was not (at least not in public documents). This may reflect different assessments of their respective roles. Kellen invoked Fifth Amendment privileges during her deposition. These invocations reflect constitutionally protected rights and should not be interpreted as evidence of wrongdoing. Such invocations may be exercised for many reasons, including on advice of counsel.

**Grouped in discovery:** Both were named in discovery requests as relevant witnesses, indicating investigators saw both as having relevant knowledge.

**Neither charged:** Despite their proximity to the principal figures and inclusion in investigation, neither Taylor nor Kellen has been criminally charged.

In our assessment, Taylor and Kellen represent parallel figures—both in assistant/staff roles, both investigated, but with different legal designations that may reflect different levels of involvement or knowledge.
