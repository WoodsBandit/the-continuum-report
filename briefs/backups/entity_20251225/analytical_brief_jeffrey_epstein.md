In 2008, Epstein entered into a controversial non-prosecution agreement with federal prosecutors, pleading guilty only to state charges in Florida. ### Sarah Ransome Affidavit

**[ECF Doc. 1331-12](https://thecontinuumreport.com/sources/giuffre-v-maxwell/ecf-1331-12.pdf), filed 01/05/24:**

Sarah Ransome provided a sworn affidavit dated January 5, 2017, stating:

> "In the summer of 2006, when I was twenty-two years old and living in New York, I was introduced to Jeffrey Epstein by a girl I had met named Natalya Malyshov. Shortly after meeting Jeffrey he invited me to fly to his private island in the US Virgin Islands, which I did. After that first trip I traveled to the island several more times, usually on one of Jeffrey's private airplanes, and always at his direction... As it turned out, the primary purpose of those visits was to have me have sexual relations with Jeffrey, Nadia Macinkova, and various other girls and guests he brought to the island."

Ransome also testified regarding observations of minors:

> "Some of the girls appeared to be 18 or older, but many appeared to be young teenagers. I recall seeing a particularly young, thin girl who looked well under 18 and recall asking her her age."

### Alfredo Rodriguez Testimony

**Regarding Epstein's death:** Epstein's death in custody prevented a criminal trial on the 2019 federal charges. This means many questions about his activities, associates, and victims may never receive judicial resolution.

**Our editorial judgment:** The documentary record establishes Epstein as the central figure in what federal prosecutors, civil plaintiffs, and ultimately a criminal jury found to be a sex trafficking operation. His 2008 guilty plea (to lesser charges), the multiple civil lawsuits, and Maxwell's conviction all support this characterization. Questions remain about the full scope of his activities and whether all responsible parties have been held accountable.
