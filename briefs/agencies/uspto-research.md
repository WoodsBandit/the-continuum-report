# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: UNITED STATES PATENT AND TRADEMARK OFFICE

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

USPTO is the federal intellectual property agency, established 1790. Uniquely fee-funded (~$5B annual collections, zero appropriations), it examines patents and trademarks with 12,000+ employees. Faces 800,000+ patent application backlog, patent quality issues, patent troll enablement concerns, and 2025 IPR restrictions limiting public ability to challenge bad patents.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** United States Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO)
**Founding History:**
- Patent Office established: July 31, 1790 (first patent to Samuel Hopkins)
- Moved: State Department (1790) → Interior (1849) → Commerce (1925)
- Renamed Patent and Trademark Office: January 2, 1975
- Renamed USPTO: March 29, 2000
- Formal establishment: July 19, 1952 (35 U.S.C. 1)
**Current Director:** John A. Squires (appointed March 2025)
- Title: Under Secretary of Commerce for Intellectual Property
- Background: Former Partner, Dilworth Paxson LLP; adjunct professor, UPenn
**Deputy:** Coke Morgan Stewart
**Headquarters:** Alexandria, Virginia (relocated 2005)

## 2. BUDGET

**Fee-Funded Model:**
- Operates solely on user fees
- Zero appropriations required
- Unique among federal agencies

**FY2026 Estimated Fee Collections:** $4,996 million
- Patent fees: $4,326 million
- Trademark fees: $670 million

**Fee-Setting Authority:**
- 2011 Leahy-Smith America Invents Act (AIA)
- Extended by SUCCESS Act (2018)
- **Authority expires: September 15, 2026** (absent congressional action)

**Operating Reserves:**
- Separate Patent OR and Trademark OR
- Patent and Trademark Fee Reserve Fund (PTFRF)
- Follows GAO best practices

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Patent Quality Issues
- **Examination Backlog:** 800,000+ unexamined applications (FY2026)
- **Time Constraints:** Examiners average ~20 hours per patent
- **GAO Finding (April 2025):** "persistent examination and quality issues"; systemic "quantity over quality"
- **Missing Prior Art:** Insufficient time and limited database access
- **Result:** 300,000+ patents annually granted that shouldn't be issued

### Patent Trolls & Litigation Abuse
- Patent holders enforcing patents far beyond actual value
- Low-quality patents used to extort settlements from small companies
- **Court Shopping:** Trolls target pro-patent courts (Western District of Texas)
- **Congressional Response:** Patent Eligibility Restoration Act (PERA), PREVAIL Act

### Examination Delays
- Sharp uptick in Patent Term Adjustment (PTA) awards
- USPTO struggling to meet timeliness mandates
- Delays favor speedy issuance of invalid patents

### Inter Partes Review (IPR) & PTAB Restrictions
**February 28, 2025 Memo:** USPTO restricted IPR access
- Withdrew 2022 guidance on discretionary denials
- New rules restrict public ability to challenge invalid patents at USPTO
- **Impact:** Patent trolls gain protection; companies lose affordable challenge mechanism

### PTAB Constitutional & Oversight Concerns
- **United States v. Arthrex (2021):** Supreme Court ruled APJ appointments violated Appointments Clause
- **GAO Survey (2022):** 75% of PTAB judges felt oversight affected independence; 67% reported pressure to modify decisions
- **May 2022 Reforms:** Made Management Review optional; clarified Director not involved in pre-issuance decisions
- Congress debated abolishing PTAB or modifying IPR/PGR procedures

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

**Parent Agency - Department of Commerce:**
- USPTO is subordinate agency
- Director serves as Under Secretary of Commerce for Intellectual Property

**Office of U.S. Trade Representative (USTR):**
- Leads U.S. trade negotiations with USPTO input on IP issues
- Interagency committees (TPRG, TPSC) include USPTO
- Joint preparation of Special 301 Report on global IP protection
- Coordinates trade enforcement involving IP

**Department of Justice:**
- Co-hosted South Asia Judicial Conclave on IP (New Delhi)
- Coordination on patent litigation policy and enforcement

**Other Collaborating Agencies:**
- State Department (trade negotiations)
- Treasury (CFIUS foreign investment reviews)
- Homeland Security (trade enforcement)
- HHS (pharmaceutical/medical IP)
- ITA (International Trade Administration)
