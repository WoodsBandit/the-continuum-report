# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: FARM CREDIT ADMINISTRATION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

FCA is the independent regulator of the Farm Credit System, established March 27, 1933. Self-funded through assessments (no appropriations), it oversees $344B in agricultural loans (44% of all farm debt). Faces Young/Beginning/Small farmer lending gaps, competition fairness concerns with commercial banks, and diversity outreach limitations.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Farm Credit Administration
**Founding:** March 27, 1933 (Executive Order 6084 by FDR)
- First Governor: Henry Morgenthau Jr.
- Consolidated all agricultural credit agencies
**Current Chairman/CEO:** Jeffery S. Hall
- Appointed March 17, 2015
- Named Chairman/CEO January 20, 2025
**Headquarters:** McLean, Virginia
**Legal Authority:** Farm Credit Act of 1971, as amended
**Mission:** Ensure Farm Credit System institutions and Farmer Mac are safe, sound, dependable credit sources for agriculture and rural America

---

## 2. BUDGET

**Self-Funded Model:**
- Receives NO federal appropriation funding
- Entirely funded by assessments from regulated institutions
- Not taxpayer-supported

**Assessment Structure:**
- First 30% of budget spread across institutions based on risk-adjusted assets
- Remaining: Examination time/travel costs and indirect cost percentages
- Farmer Mac assessment (FY2024): $2.9 million
- Administrative expenses cap: $94.3 million (FY2025)

**Expense Composition:**
- ~97% operating expenses classified as administrative
- Personnel costs exceed 80% of administrative spending

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**FCA Board:**
- 3 full-time members (presidential appointees, Senate-confirmed)
- 6-year terms
- Maximum 2 members from same political party
- Chairman serves as CEO and chief spokesman

**Reporting Structure (under Chief of Staff, established April 2023):**

- Office of Information Technology

**Staff Size:** ~300 full- and part-time employees

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Farm Credit System (FCS) Oversight
- Regulates borrower-owned financial institutions
- 64 regional credit associations
- 4 large banks allocating funds
- **Total loan portfolio (2021):** $344 billion
- **Farm loans specifically:** $210+ billion (44% of all farm debt)
- FCS largest lender for farm real estate at 49%

### Federal Agricultural Mortgage Corporation (Farmer Mac)
- Established 1987 (Agricultural Credit Act)
- Secondary market for agricultural loans
- Purchases, pools, securitizes loans
- **Total business volume (December 2024):** ~$30 billion
- Outstanding program volume: $23.6B (2021), up from $14.0B (2013)
- Provides risk management tools to originating lenders
- Separate corporate entity from FCS with distinct liability

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Young, Beginning, and Small (YBS) Farmers Lending
- FCS required by law (amended 1980) to serve YBS farmers
- **Definitions:**
  - Young: ≤35 years old
  - Beginning: ≤10 years farming
  - Small: <$250K annual sales
- **Critical Gap:** YBS lending share has remained flat/declined despite mandate
- Small farm loans declining since 2004 (modest recent increases)
- Reporting requirement exists but NO lending quotas imposed

### Agricultural Lending Concentration
- FCS: 44% of farm credit (largest single source)
- Commercial banks: 36%
- Individuals/others: 10%
- Life insurance companies: 4%

### Competition & Fairness Concerns
- Direct competition with commercial banks for creditworthy borrowers
- FCS has tax advantages as GSE (lower cost of funds)
- Commercial banks assert unfair competitive advantages
- Adversarial policy relationship exists

### Diversity & Outreach Limitations
- FCA requires lenders to prepare marketing plans with diversity outreach
- NO specific lending goals/quotas required
- Limited transparency on SDFR (socially disadvantaged farmers/ranchers) access
- Impact of outreach efforts poorly documented

### Reporting Data Challenges
- FCS reports YBS categories separately (no combined summary)
- Significant overlap between young/beginning/small categories not captured
- Difficult to assess aggregate progress on YBS mandate

## 6. 2008 FINANCIAL CRISIS PERFORMANCE

**Relative Resilience:**
- FCS did NOT require federal bailout
- Entered 2008 in strong financial position (recovery from mid-1980s farm crisis)

**Contributing Factors:**
- FCA implemented stronger capital regulations (early 1990s)
- System institutions required to maintain sufficient surplus
- Farm commodity prices remained strong pre-2008
- Quality of FCS loan portfolio was strong

**Crisis Management:**
- Funding Corporation accessed capital markets (short-term maturities only)
- Avoided major operational disruptions
- All federal assistance from 1980s repaid with interest by June 2005

## 7. RELATIONSHIPS

**Federal Regulatory Coordination:**
- Collaborates with: Federal Reserve Board, FDIC, NCUA, OCC
- Primary focus: Flood insurance requirements

**Flood Insurance Role:**
- FCA flood insurance regulations: 12 CFR Part 614, Subpart S
- May 2022: Revised interagency guidance on premium escrow, detached structure exemptions, force placement, private insurer acceptance

**Key Distinction from FDIC:**
- FCA regulates non-bank financial institutions (FCS cooperatives)
- FDIC regulates commercial banks
- FCS institutions are NOT FDIC-insured
- Separate insurance: Farm Credit System Insurance Corporation

**USDA Relationship:**
- FCA and USDA coordinate on agricultural credit policy
- USDA provides broader farm support programs (FSA, etc.)
- Complementary rather than overlapping jurisdiction
