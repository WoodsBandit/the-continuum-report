# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: U.S. DEPARTMENT OF VETERANS AFFAIRS

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) is a cabinet-level agency established in 1989, providing comprehensive health care, benefits, and services to veterans. As the second-largest federal department, the VA manages a budget exceeding $300 billion and employs approximately 371,000 personnel serving 9 million enrolled veterans.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** United States Department of Veterans Affairs (VA)

**Founding Date:** March 15, 1989 (Cabinet status; VA existed since 1930)

**Current Secretary:** Douglas Allen Collins (sworn in February 5, 2025, 12th Secretary)

**Headquarters:** 810 Vermont Avenue, NW, Washington, DC 20420

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

### Three Primary Administrations
1. **Veterans Health Administration (VHA)** - 170 medical centers, 1,255 facilities
2. **Veterans Benefits Administration (VBA)** - Disability, education, home loans
3. **National Cemetery Administration (NCA)** - 155 national cemeteries

**Workforce:** ~371,000 employees

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

- Disability Compensation (tax-free benefits)
- GI Bill Education Benefits
- VA Home Loans (no down payment)
- Burial & Memorial Benefits
- PACT Act toxic exposure benefits

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### 2014 Wait Time Scandal
- Phoenix VA secret waitlists
- 6 veterans with "clinically significant delays" died
- 3,500+ veterans on unofficial waitlists
- 8 VA employees terminated

### Veteran Suicide Crisis
- 6,407 veteran suicides in 2022 (17.6 per day)
- 14 of 20 daily suicides not in VA contact

### Agent Orange & Burn Pit Exposures
- PACT Act: Largest VA expansion in history
- 20+ presumptive conditions added

## 6. INTERAGENCY RELATIONSHIPS

- HHS/CMS: Healthcare standards, fraud detection
