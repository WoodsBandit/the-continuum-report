# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: EXPORT-IMPORT BANK OF THE UNITED STATES

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

EXIM is an independent agency providing export credit financing, established February 2, 1934. Self-sustaining at no taxpayer cost, it has remitted $6.9B to Treasury since 1992. Faces "Bank of Boeing" criticism and China competition challenge; reauthorized through December 2026.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Export-Import Bank of the United States
**Founding:** February 2, 1934 (Executive Order 6581)
**Current Chair:** John Jovanovic (September 19, 2025)
**Headquarters:** 811 Vermont Ave NW, Washington, DC 20571

## 2. BUDGET/FUNDING

**Self-Sustaining Status:** No taxpayer cost since FY2008

**Financial Performance:**
- Since 1992: $6.9B more to Treasury than received in appropriations
- Since 2009: $3.8B contributed to taxpayers
- Negative subsidy rate: 1.68% (indicates profitability)
- FY2026 projected: $323.6M in negative subsidy receipts to Treasury
- Every $1B in lending generates ~$50M excess revenues

**Statutory Authority:** $135-140 billion loan/guarantee/insurance cap

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Direct Loans
- Fixed-rate up to 15 years (22 for renewable energy)
- 100% commercial and political risk coverage
- Up to 50% local costs

### Loan Guarantees (45% of portfolio)
- 85% guarantee coverage (15% buyer down payment)
- Fixed or floating rates
- USD or alternative currency

### Export Credit Insurance (9% of portfolio)
- Political and commercial risk protection
- For exporters and financial institutions

### Working Capital Guarantees (40% of portfolio)
- Short-term financing for U.S. exporters

### China Competition Programs
**CTEP (2019):** Targets AI, biotech, quantum, semiconductors, renewables
**Supply Chain Resiliency Initiative:** Critical minerals financing
**China Competition Committee (C3):** Advisory committee on competitiveness

**Portfolio (FY2024):** $34B total exposure (25% of $135B cap)

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### "Bank of Boeing" Criticism
- Boeing: Largest EXIM beneficiary
- Pre-2014: 35% of total aid, 68% of loan guarantees
- FY2014: $7.4B (68.3%) of long-term guarantees
- Small businesses: 25% of total authorizations
- Critics: "Corporate welfare"

### Political Opposition
- Conservative Republicans/Tea Party view as corporate welfare
- Free-market advocates: Large banks should finance independently
- Delta Air Lines protested EXIM financing for Emirates

### Reauthorization Battles
- Required every 4-5 years
- 2015: Brief quorum lapse; Senate refused confirmations
- December 2015: Reauthorized through 2019
- December 2019: Extended through December 2026

### Accounting Disputes
- Critics: Self-sustaining claims are "accounting illusion"
- CBO: Official rules understate loan program costs
- Heavy aircraft loan concentration creates vulnerability

### Recent Controversy (2025)
- $498M for Turkish Airlines
- $100M+ guarantee for Azerbaijan's Silk Way Airlines
- Armenian groups campaigning to block
