# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: THE SMITHSONIAN INSTITUTION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Smithsonian is the world's largest museum complex, established August 10, 1846. With ~$1.09B federal appropriation (62%) plus trust funds, it operates 21 museums, National Zoo, and 9 research centers serving 20M+ annual visitors. Faces 2025 Trump administration exhibit review, NAGPRA repatriation gaps, and Enola Gay controversy legacy.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** The Smithsonian Institution
**Founding:** August 10, 1846 (James Smithson bequest for "increase and diffusion of knowledge")
**Current Secretary:** Lonnie G. Bunch III (14th Secretary, appointed June 15, 2019)
- First African American and first historian to hold position
- Founded National Museum of African American History and Culture
**Headquarters:** Washington, D.C. (Castle building on National Mall)

---

## 2. BUDGET

**FY2024 Federal Appropriations:** $1.09 billion (4.7% decrease from FY2023's $1.14 billion)
- Represents ~62% of total funding
- Conserves national collections, sustains research, educates public

**Trust Funds (Private Revenue):**
- Donations, grants, contracts, investments, business activities
- Smithsonian Enterprises operates independently (retail, media, travel services)
- Funds innovative research, facility expansion, collection development

**Hybrid Funding Model:** Federal baseline + private flexibility

**Secretary's Office:**

**Physical Assets:**
- 21 museums (free admission)

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

- Free admission to all 21 museums

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### NAGPRA & Native American Repatriation
- Operates under National Museum of American Indian Act (1989), not NAGPRA
- Holds ~10,000 human remains (more than any other U.S. museum)
- Originally held 18,500 Native American remains (1989)
- Lacks independent appeals process for tribal disputes
- Only 48% of human remains under NAGPRA nationally have been returned

### Enola Gay Exhibit Controversy (1995)
- Planned "The Crossroads" exhibit for 50th anniversary of atomic bombing
- Air Force Association/American Legion claimed exhibit "hostile to military"
- January 30, 1995: Smithsonian conceded; exhibit reduced to fuselage + minimal context
- Director Martin Harwit resigned May 2, 1995
- Created template for conservative groups to weaponize historical exhibits

### 2025 Trump Administration Controversy
**Executive Order (March 27, 2025):** "Restoring Truth and Sanity to American History"
- Prohibit federal funding of exhibits that "degrade shared American values"
- VP J.D. Vance tasked with purging "improper ideology"

**White House Review (August 2025):**
- Comprehensive audit of 8 museums
- Review of online content, curatorial processes, exhibition planning
- Artist grants and exhibit messaging under scrutiny

**Specific Changes:**
- National Museum of American History: Modified placard on Trump's impeachments
- National Portrait Gallery: Artist Amy Sherald canceled exhibition over transgender subject

**Trump Statement:** "The Smithsonian is OUT OF CONTROL... everything discussed is how horrible our Country is"

**Reactions:**
- American Historical Association: "egregious misrepresentation"
- Congressional Black Caucus: "whitewashing our nation's history"
- Implementation deadline: Early 2026

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

**Federal Trust Instrumentality (Unique Classification):**
- Public-private hybrid institution
- Not an executive branch agency
- Governance includes all three branches

**Exemptions from Federal Statute:**
- NOT subject to: Privacy Act, Administrative Procedure Act, FOIA (partially)
- SUBJECT TO: Federal Property and Administrative Services Act

**Dual Employment Categories:**
- Federal employees (appropriated funds)
- Trust employees (non-federal funds)

**Interagency Relationships:**
- Office of Protection Services coordinates with U.S. Park Police
- Historical relationship with Department of Interior
- Maintains partnerships across agencies for research, conservation, education

**Federal Immunity:** Shares sovereign immunity of U.S. from state/local regulation
