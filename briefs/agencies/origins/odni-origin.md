# ORIGIN STORY: OFFICE OF THE DIRECTOR OF NATIONAL INTELLIGENCE

## FOUNDING DATE
December 17, 2004 (legislation signed)
April 22, 2005 (ODNI began operations)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Intelligence Reform and Terrorism Prevention Act of 2004

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### 9/11 COMMISSION (Recommended Creation)

#### Key Members
- **Thomas Kean** (Chair) - Former Republican Governor of New Jersey
- **Lee Hamilton** (Vice Chair) - Former Democratic Congressman from Indiana

#### Report
- July 22, 2004: 9/11 Commission Report released
- Identified major intelligence failures before September 11 attacks
- Recommended creating Director of National Intelligence position
- Called for unified leadership over fragmented intelligence community

### GEORGE W. BUSH (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** July 6, 1946, New Haven, Connecticut
- **Education:** Yale University (BA); Harvard Business School (MBA)
- **Career:** Oil business; Texas Rangers owner; Governor of Texas; President

#### Religion
- **Methodist** (converted from Episcopalian after struggle with alcohol)

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Philosophy:** "Compassionate conservatism"; strong national security focus post-9/11

---

## CREATION OF ODNI

### 9/11 Intelligence Failures
- Attackers entered U.S. legally; intelligence agencies failed to share information
- CIA and FBI did not coordinate effectively
- No single official responsible for all intelligence
- Commission found "failure of imagination" across government

### Legislative Response
- Intelligence Reform and Terrorism Prevention Act of 2004
- December 17, 2004: President Bush signed into law
- Created DNI position and ODNI as independent agency
- Most significant intelligence reorganization since 1947

### Purpose
- DNI serves as head of U.S. Intelligence Community
- Directs and oversees National Intelligence Program
- Coordinates 18 intelligence agencies
- Principal intelligence adviser to President

## FIRST DIRECTOR OF NATIONAL INTELLIGENCE

### JOHN D. NEGROPONTE

#### Background
- **Born:** July 21, 1939, London, England
- **Education:** Yale University (BA)
- **Career:** Career diplomat; Ambassador to Honduras, Mexico, Philippines, UN, Iraq

#### Religion
- **Roman Catholic** (Greek-American heritage)

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Philosophy:** Career foreign service; Cold War diplomat; controversial Honduras tenure

### Appointment
- February 17, 2005: Nominated by President Bush
- April 21, 2005: Confirmed by Senate 98-2
- Same day: Sworn in by President Bush
- April 22, 2005: ODNI began operations

### Qualifications
- Deep diplomatic experience
- Ambassador to Iraq (2004-2005) during critical transition
- First person to hold newly created position

## STRUCTURE

### Agencies Under DNI
All 18 IC agencies report to DNI:
- Central Intelligence Agency (CIA)
- Defense Intelligence Agency (DIA)
- National Security Agency (NSA)
- National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency (NGA)
- National Reconnaissance Office (NRO)
- Federal Bureau of Investigation (intelligence elements)
- Department of Homeland Security (intelligence elements)
- Military service intelligence agencies
- Others

### ODNI Staff
- Approximately 1,750 employees
- Headquarters: McLean, Virginia

## 20TH ANNIVERSARY REFLECTIONS (2025)

### Negroponte's Assessment
- ODNI successfully brought coherence to intelligence community
- Created mechanisms for information sharing that didn't exist pre-9/11
- Position evolved significantly since creation
- Continues adapting to new threats (cyber, AI, great power competition)

---

## CONTEXT: POST-9/11 REFORMS

### Other Changes
- Department of Homeland Security created (2002)
- USA PATRIOT Act (2001)
- Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court reforms
- Enhanced congressional oversight

### Largest IC Reorganization Since 1947
- 1947: National Security Act created CIA, NSC, unified military
- 2004: IRTPA created DNI, reorganized entire intelligence community
- Both responses to perceived existential threats (Cold War; terrorism)
