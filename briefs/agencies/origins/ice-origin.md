# ORIGIN STORY: U.S. IMMIGRATION AND CUSTOMS ENFORCEMENT

## FOUNDING DATE
March 1, 2003

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Homeland Security Act of 2002

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### GEORGE W. BUSH (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** July 6, 1946, New Haven, Connecticut
- **Education:** Yale University (BA); Harvard Business School (MBA)
- **Career:** Oil business; Texas Rangers owner; Governor of Texas; President

#### Religion
- **Methodist** (converted from Episcopalian)

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Philosophy:** "Compassionate conservatism"; strong national security post-9/11

### 9/11 COMMISSION & CONGRESS

#### Post-9/11 Reorganization
- Largest government reorganization since creation of Department of Defense
- 22 federal agencies absorbed into new Department of Homeland Security
- Immigration and customs functions consolidated

---

## CREATION OF ICE

### Predecessor Agencies
ICE absorbed functions from:
- **U.S. Customs Service** (Treasury Department) - founded 1789
- **Immigration and Naturalization Service** (DOJ) - dating to 1891

### March 1, 2003
- Bureau of Immigration and Customs Enforcement opened doors
- Now known as U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE)
- Part of new Department of Homeland Security

### Sister Agencies Created Same Day
- **Customs and Border Protection (CBP)** - border security
- **U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS)** - legal immigration

---

## FIRST DIRECTOR

### MICHAEL J. GARCIA (Assistant Secretary for ICE)

#### Background
- **Career:** Federal prosecutor; career government official
- Acting Commissioner of INS (December 2002 - February 2003)
- Previous experience overseeing immigration functions

#### Appointment
- March 2003: Appointed by President Bush
- November 2003: Unanimously confirmed by Senate
- Title: "Assistant Secretary for Immigration and Customs Enforcement"

#### Scope of Leadership
- Led second largest investigative agency in federal government
- Over 20,000 employees
- 6,000 investigators
- Budget exceeding $4 billion

#### Later Career
- Became U.S. Attorney for Southern District of New York
- New York State Court of Appeals Judge

---

## MISSION

### Dual Focus
1. **Immigration Enforcement**
   - Interior enforcement of immigration laws
   - Removal operations
   - Detention management

2. **Customs/Homeland Security Investigations**
   - Cross-border crime
   - Human trafficking
   - Drug smuggling
   - Financial crimes
   - Cyber crimes

### Operational Directorates
- **Homeland Security Investigations (HSI)** - criminal investigations
- **Enforcement and Removal Operations (ERO)** - immigration enforcement
- **Office of the Principal Legal Advisor (OPLA)** - legal proceedings

---

## CURRENT STATUS

### Size and Scope
- Over 20,000 law enforcement and support personnel
- 400+ offices in United States and worldwide
- Annual budget approximately $8 billion

### Controversy
- Immigration enforcement policies highly contested
- Detention conditions debated
- Family separation policies criticized
- "Abolish ICE" movement emerged 2018
