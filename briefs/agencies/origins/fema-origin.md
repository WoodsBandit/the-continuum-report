# ORIGIN STORY: FEDERAL EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT AGENCY

## FOUNDING DATE
April 1, 1979

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Presidential Reorganization Plan No. 3 of 1978
Executive Order 12127 (April 1, 1979)
Executive Order 12148 (July 20, 1979)

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### JIMMY CARTER (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** October 1, 1924, Plains, Georgia
- **Died:** December 29, 2024
- **Education:** U.S. Naval Academy; nuclear engineering training
- **Career:** Naval officer; peanut farmer; Governor of Georgia; President

#### Religion
- **Baptist** - devoutly religious; taught Sunday school into his 90s

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democratic
- **Philosophy:** Good government reformer; efficient administration

---

## THE PROBLEM BEFORE FEMA

### Fragmented System
- More than 100 separate agencies involved in disaster relief
- No coordination mechanism
- Agencies jockeying for control and jurisdiction
- Confusing for states, localities, and disaster victims
- Inefficient use of resources

### Historical Background
- 1803: First disaster legislation (aid to New Hampshire town after fire)
- Patchwork of programs grew over 175 years
- No unified approach to emergencies

---

## CREATION OF FEMA

### Carter's Reorganization
- Presidential Reorganization Plan No. 3 of 1978
- April 1, 1979: Executive Order 12127 established FEMA
- July 20, 1979: Executive Order 12148 defined dual mission

### Dual Mission
1. **Emergency Management** - natural disaster response
2. **Civil Defense** - nuclear attack preparedness (Cold War)

### Agencies Merged Into FEMA
- Federal Insurance Administration
- National Fire Prevention and Control Administration
- National Weather Service Community Preparedness Program
- Federal Preparedness Agency (from GSA)
- Federal Disaster Assistance Administration (from HUD)
- Defense Civil Preparedness Agency (from DOD)

## EVOLUTION

### Early Years (1979-2001)
- Initially independent agency
- Mixed record depending on administration
- Gained prominence after major disasters

### Post-9/11 (2003)
- Transferred to Department of Homeland Security
- Became DHS component agency
- Focus shifted toward terrorism response

### Hurricane Katrina (2005)
- Major test and failure
- Criticized for slow, inadequate response
- Led to reforms and renewed focus on natural disasters

---

## MISSION TODAY

### Core Functions
- Disaster response and recovery
- Hazard mitigation
- Emergency preparedness
- National Flood Insurance Program
- Training and education

### Current Status
- Component of Department of Homeland Security
- Headquarters: Washington, D.C.
- Regional offices across country
- Coordinates federal disaster response

---

## CARTER'S LEGACY

### Standard for Disaster Response
- Created unified federal approach
- Established framework still in use
- FEMA model adopted internationally
- Demonstrated government reorganization could improve outcomes
