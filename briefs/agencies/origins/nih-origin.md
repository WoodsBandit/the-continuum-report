# ORIGIN STORY: NATIONAL INSTITUTES OF HEALTH

## FOUNDING DATE
1887 (Hygienic Laboratory established)
May 26, 1930 (Ransdell Act - renamed NIH)
1948 (became plural "Institutes")

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Ransdell Act of 1930

---

## DEEP HISTORICAL ROOTS (1887)

### The Beginning
- 1887: One-room laboratory on Staten Island
- Created within Marine Hospital Service
- Purpose: Study cholera, yellow fever, infectious diseases
- Focused on diseases affecting returning U.S. sailors

### Evolution
- Laboratory moved to Washington, D.C. (1891)
- Became known as Hygienic Laboratory
- Conducted research on infectious diseases
- Grew in scope and importance

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### JOSEPH E. RANSDELL (Senator, Louisiana)

#### Background
- **Born:** October 7, 1858
- **Died:** July 27, 1954
- **Career:** Louisiana Congressman (1899-1913); Senator (1913-1931)

#### Religion
- Not documented in sources

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democratic
- **Philosophy:** Progressive; federal investment in science and health

### Ransdell's Campaign
- 1926: Began pushing for federal medical research institute
- Private philanthropy couldn't fund envisioned institute
- Turned to federal sponsorship
- Championed legislation that bore his name

---

## THE RANSDELL ACT (1930)

### WWI Connection
- 1918: Chemists from WWI Chemical Warfare Service sought peacetime application
- Wanted institute applying chemistry to medicine
- 1926: Joined with Senator Ransdell after failing to find private funding

### What the Act Did
- Renamed Hygienic Laboratory as "National Institute of Health"
- Authorized fellowships for basic biological and medical research
- Provided $750,000 to construct NIH buildings
- Established federal commitment to medical research

### Great Depression Impact
- Act passed in truncated form due economic crisis
- Reflected harsh realities of Depression
- Still marked historic shift toward federal funding of medical research

---

## SIGNING

### HERBERT HOOVER (President)

#### Background
- President 1929-1933
- Signed Ransdell Act May 26, 1930

#### Religion
- **Quaker**

#### Politics
- Republican; limited government philosophy
- Nonetheless signed research funding bill

---

## EVOLUTION TO "INSTITUTES" (PLURAL)

### 1948 Expansion
- National Heart Act language made name plural
- Research expanded beyond infectious diseases:
  - Mental health
  - Dental disease
  - Heart disease
  - Cancer
- Each area got its own "Institute"

### Modern Structure
- 27 separate Institutes and Centers
- Each focuses on specific diseases or research areas
- Umbrella organization: National Institutes of Health

---

## MISSION

### Biomedical Research
- World's largest public funder of biomedical research
- Supports research at universities and institutions nationwide
- Conducts intramural research at Bethesda campus
- Training future scientists

### Impact
- Discoveries that transformed medicine
- Vaccines, treatments, diagnostic tools
- Economic driver through innovation
- Model for government-funded research

---

## CONTEXT: ATTITUDE SHIFT

### From Private to Public
- Before 1930: Medical research mainly private/philanthropic
- Ransdell Act marked change in scientific community attitude
- Federal funding became acceptable, then essential
- NIH became backbone of American medical research
