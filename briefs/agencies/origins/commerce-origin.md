# ORIGIN STORY: DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE

## FOUNDING DATE
February 14, 1903 (Department of Commerce and Labor created)
1913 (Split into separate Commerce and Labor departments)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Act creating Department of Commerce and Labor (ninth cabinet department)

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### GEORGE B. CORTELYOU (First Secretary of Commerce and Labor)

#### Background
- **Born:** July 26, 1862, New York City
- **Died:** October 23, 1940
- **Family Heritage:** Member of old New Netherlandish family; immigrant ancestor Jacques Cortelyou arrived 1652
- **Parents:** Rose (n√©e Seary) and Peter Crolius Cortelyou Jr.

#### Education
- Public schools of Brooklyn
- Nazareth Hall Military Academy, Pennsylvania (Moravian institution)
- Hempstead Institute, Long Island
- Westfield Normal School (now Westfield State University) - BA at age 20
- George Washington University - law degree
- Georgetown University - law degree

#### Pre-Government Career
- Educator and stenographer before government service

#### Religion
- Attended Nazareth Hall Military Academy (Moravian)
- Personal religious affiliation not documented in sources

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican
- **Leadership:** Chairman of Republican National Committee (1904-1907)
- **1912 Split:** Publicly declared support for Taft over former boss Roosevelt during contentious 1912 nomination

---

## PATH TO CABINET

### Relationship with Roosevelt
- Served as Theodore Roosevelt's personal secretary
- Became "the President's most trusted and intimate advisor"
- Roosevelt appointed him first Secretary of Commerce and Labor

### Tenure
- February 18, 1903 to June 30, 1904
- Oversaw establishment of Bureau of Corporations (investigated business practices)

### Later Cabinet Positions
- Postmaster General (1905-1907)
- Secretary of the Treasury (1907-1909)

---

## THEODORE ROOSEVELT (President)

#### Role
- Signed bill creating Department of Commerce and Labor on February 14, 1903
- Nominated Cortelyou as first Secretary two days later (February 16, 1903)

#### Philosophy
- Trust-busting and business regulation
- Believed government needed tools to oversee expanding industrial economy

---

## THE 1913 SPLIT

- Department of Commerce and Labor split in 1913
- Separate Department of Labor created to focus on workers' issues
- Commerce Department retained business-focused functions
