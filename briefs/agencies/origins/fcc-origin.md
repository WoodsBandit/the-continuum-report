# ORIGIN STORY: FEDERAL COMMUNICATIONS COMMISSION

## FOUNDING DATE
June 19, 1934 (Communications Act signed)
July 11, 1934 (FCC first meeting)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Communications Act of 1934

---

## KEY FOUNDER

### FRANKLIN D. ROOSEVELT (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** January 30, 1882, Hyde Park, New York
- **Died:** April 12, 1945
- **Education:** Harvard; Columbia Law School (did not complete)
- **Career:** NY State Senator; Assistant Navy Secretary; Governor of NY; President

#### Religion
- **Episcopalian** - lifelong member; senior warden at St. James Church, Hyde Park

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democratic
- **Philosophy:** New Deal; active federal government; economic regulation

---

## CREATION OF FCC

### Predecessor Agency
- **Federal Radio Commission (FRC):** Created 1927 to regulate radio broadcasting
- FRC faced difficulties: licensing disputes, frequency allocation, unregulated broadcasters

### Roosevelt's Initiative
- 1933: FDR directed Commerce Secretary Daniel C. Roper to study radio broadcasting
- January 1934: Roper committee recommended consolidating communications regulation
- February 26, 1934: FDR sent message to Congress urging FCC creation

### Congressional Sponsors
- **Senator Clarence Dill** (D-Washington)
- **Representative Sam Rayburn** (D-Texas) - later Speaker of the House
- Introduced bills February 27, 1934

### Passage
- May 31, 1934: Congress passed Communications Act
- June 19, 1934: Roosevelt signed into law

---

## WHAT FCC CONSOLIDATED

### Agencies Merged
- Federal Radio Commission
- Interstate Commerce Commission (communications functions)
- Postmaster General (communications functions)
- Presidential communications authority

### Broad Mandate
- Created "rapid, efficient, Nation-wide, and world-wide wire and radio communication service"
- Cornerstone principle: public ownership of airwaves
- Incorporated virtually all of Radio Act of 1927

---

## INITIAL ORGANIZATION

### Commission Structure
- Seven-member bipartisan commission
- Appointed by President, confirmed by Senate
- Seven-year terms
- No more than four from same political party

### First Meeting
- July 11, 1934: First meeting in Washington
- Voted unanimously to continue FRC status quo initially

### Three Divisions (July 17, 1934)
1. Broadcasting Division
2. Telegraph Division
3. Telephone Division
- Each division led by two commissioners
- Chairman member of all three

### Resources
- Initial budget: $1,146,885
- Initial staff: 442
- Oversaw approximately 800 commercial and educational radio stations
