# ORIGIN STORY: NATIONAL PARK SERVICE

## FOUNDING DATE
August 25, 1916

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
National Park Service Organic Act

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### WOODROW WILSON (President)

#### Background
- **Born:** December 28, 1856, Staunton, Virginia
- **Died:** February 3, 1924
- **Education:** Princeton; University of Virginia Law; Johns Hopkins (PhD)
- **Career:** Professor; Princeton President; Governor; President

#### Religion
- **Presbyterian** - deeply devout; son of Presbyterian minister

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democratic
- **Philosophy:** Progressive; New Freedom; active federal government

### STEPHEN TYNG MATHER (First Director)

#### Background
- **Born:** July 4, 1867, San Francisco, California
- **Died:** January 22, 1930
- **Education:** University of California, Berkeley
- **Career:** Journalist; businessman (borax industry fortune)

#### Religion
- **Congregationalist** - New England Protestant tradition

#### Politics
- Republican (business background)
- Conservationist; transcended party on parks

### How Mather Got Involved
- 1914: Wrote complaint letter to Interior Secretary about park management
- Response: "If you don't like the way the national parks are being run, come on down to Washington and run them yourself"
- Mather accepted; became Assistant to Secretary of Interior
- Used personal wealth to fund park improvements and advocacy

### Horace Albright (Mather's Deputy)

#### Background
- Young lawyer; Mather's assistant
- Second NPS Director (1929-1933)
- Co-architect of National Park Service

---

## BEFORE THE NPS

### Scattered Management
- Yellowstone (1872) and other parks existed but managed inconsistently
- Different parks under different agencies
- No unified vision or standards
- Poaching, vandalism, development threats

### Campaign for NPS
- Conservationists: John Muir, Sierra Club
- Railroad companies: Wanted tourism
- Mather and Albright: Coordinated advocacy
- 1916: Congress finally acted

---

## CREATION OF NPS

### Organic Act
- August 25, 1916: Wilson signed into law
- Created National Park Service within Department of Interior
- Mission: "conserve the scenery and the natural and historic objects and the wild life therein and to provide for the enjoyment of the same in such manner and by such means as will leave them unimpaired for the enjoyment of future generations"

### Dual Mandate
- Conservation AND public enjoyment
- Balance that continues to define NPS mission
- "Unimpaired for future generations"

---

## FIRST DIRECTOR

### STEPHEN MATHER (1917-1929)

#### Leadership Style
- Used personal fortune to supplement government funding
- Hired talented staff; empowered subordinates
- Marketed parks to American public
- Built political support across parties
- Created "America's Best Idea" narrative

#### Religion's Influence
- Saw parks as "cathedrals" of nature
- Spiritual dimension to conservation
- Parks as places of renewal and transcendence

#### Health and Departure
- Suffered from bipolar disorder
- Multiple breakdowns during tenure
- Retired 1929; died 1930
- Plaque in every park: "There will never come an end to the good he has done"

---

## CONTEXT: CONSERVATION MOVEMENT

### Progressive Era Conservation
- Theodore Roosevelt championed federal land protection
- National monuments (Antiquities Act 1906)
- National forests (Forest Service under Agriculture)
- Parks needed dedicated agency

### American Identity
- Parks as alternative to European cathedrals and castles
- Democratic ideal: nature belongs to all Americans
- "America's Best Idea" (Ken Burns documentary term)

---

## NPS TODAY

### Scope
- 400+ units (parks, monuments, battlefields, seashores, etc.)
- 85+ million acres
- 300+ million visitors annually
- Approximately 20,000 employees
