# ORIGIN STORY: SECURITIES AND EXCHANGE COMMISSION

## FOUNDING DATE
June 6, 1934 (Securities Exchange Act signed)
June 30, 1934 (First commissioners appointed)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Securities Exchange Act of 1934

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### JOSEPH P. KENNEDY (First Chairman)

#### Background
- **Position:** First Chairman of the SEC (1934-1935)
- **Career:** Businessman, investor, stock market speculator
- **Family:** Father of President John F. Kennedy

#### Religion
- **Roman Catholic** - Leading member of Irish Catholic community
- FDR sent Kennedy and other prominent Irish Catholics to try to moderate Father Charles Coughlin, the prominent Roman Catholic spokesman on political/financial issues in 1930s

#### Politics
- **Party:** Democratic - Leading member of Democratic Party
- **Philosophy:** Pragmatist who could "conciliate different points of view"
- **Roosevelt advisor Raymond Moley:** Believed Kennedy was best choice because top contenders Landis and Pecora were "too ideologically inclined"

#### Selection Controversy
- Choice of Kennedy "scandalized liberals across the country"
- Most assumed James Landis would get the job
- FDR's brain trust recommended Kennedy: "best bet for Chairman because of executive ability, knowledge of habits and customs of business to be regulated and ability to moderate different points of view"
- Kennedy had the knowledge, connections, and stature to lead the agency

---

### FERDINAND PECORA (Commissioner)

#### Background
- **Born:** Sicily (Sicilian immigrant to America)
- **Raised:** New York
- **Character:** "Pugnacious... with a sharp mind and quick tongue"
- **Role:** Chief counsel to Senate Banking Committee before SEC

#### The Pecora Investigation
- Uncovered "preferred list" of highly placed Americans allowed by J.P. Morgan & Co. to buy low and sell high on insider information
- Succeeded in fanning public outrage, making it politically possible for FDR to deliver financial reform
- Named Commissioner by FDR
- Supervised trading division (investigative arm of SEC)
- Resigned after six months, tiring of being in the minority

---

### JAMES M. LANDIS (Commissioner)

#### Background
- **Position:** Harvard Law professor
- **Mentor:** Felix Frankfurter (disciple of Louis Brandeis)
- **Character:** "Determined and tense"

#### Role in SEC Creation
- Recruited by Frankfurter to draft securities legislation
- Worked with Benjamin Cohen (described as "brilliant but retiring lawyer")
- Kennedy won Landis over before appointment
- Landis realized only Kennedy had the experience to lead the agency
- Assigned supervision of studies/reports and writing of regulations/opinions

---

### BENJAMIN COHEN (Legislative Architect)

#### Background
- **Position:** Lawyer
- **Character:** "Brilliant but retiring"
- **Role:** Worked with Landis to draft securities legislation

---

## POLITICAL CONTEXT

### The New Deal Framework
- Securities Act of 1933 was first effort
- FDR turned to "Politicians and Professors" who tempered ideology with practicality
- Progressive reformers vs. economic conservatives battled in agencies like FTC
- SEC represented compromise between regulation and market function

### FDR's Strategy
- Appointed Kennedy (a former speculator) to reassure Wall Street
- Kennedy "built a strong, effective SEC and helped FDR safeguard his New Deal"

---

## FOUNDING EVENT

June 6, 1934: FDR signed Securities Exchange Act with Pecora, Cohen, Corcoran, and Landis all standing by.
