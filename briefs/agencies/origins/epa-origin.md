# ORIGIN STORY: ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION AGENCY

## FOUNDING DATE
December 2, 1970 (EPA opened doors)
December 4, 1970 (First Administrator sworn in)

## FOUNDING LEGISLATION
Reorganization Plan No. 3 of 1970 (executive reorganization approved by Congress)

---

## KEY FOUNDERS

### WILLIAM D. RUCKELSHAUS (First Administrator)

#### Background
- **Born:** July 24, 1932, Indianapolis, Indiana
- **Died:** November 27, 2019
- **Education:** Princeton University; Harvard Law School
- **Early Career:** At age 28, appointed Deputy State Attorney General of Indiana
- **Legislative Work:** Drafted Indiana Air Pollution Control Act of 1963
- **Political Office:** Won Republican seat in Indiana state House of Representatives (1967)
- **Federal Career:** Assistant Attorney General for Civil Division (1969-1970) before EPA

#### Religion
- Not specifically documented in search results

#### Politics
- **Party:** Republican (moderate)
- **Philosophy:** Pragmatic environmentalist; believed in "following the science"
- **Legacy Values:** "Respecting the law, following the science, and operating openly and transparently"

#### EPA Achievements (First Term 1970-1973)
- Advocated for and enacted ban on DDT insecticide
- Oversaw implementation of Clean Air Act extension of 1970
- Oversaw Federal Water Pollution Control Act Amendments of 1972

#### Later Career: The "Saturday Night Massacre"
- October 1973: As Deputy Attorney General, resigned rather than obey Nixon's order to fire Watergate special prosecutor
- 2015: Awarded Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Obama

---

### RICHARD NIXON (President)

#### Role in EPA Creation
- July 9, 1970: Proposed executive reorganization consolidating environmental responsibilities under one agency
- Merged pollution control programs from multiple departments
- November 25, 1970: EPA proposal approved by Congress
- EPA created 90 days before it had to operate

#### Politics and Environment
- "Nixon was largely indifferent to environmental issues but sensitive about his own popularity"
- "Succumbed to public pressure" for environmental action
- Strategic calculation: Co-opted environmental movement before Democrats could claim it

---

### RACHEL CARSON (Intellectual Catalyst)

#### Background
- **Position:** Marine biologist, author
- **Key Work:** "Silent Spring" (1962)

#### Influence
- "Silent Spring" was "a quietly shocking tale about the widespread pesticide poisoning of man and nature"
- Book "elicited a public outcry for direct government action to protect the wild"
- "In the process of transforming ecology from dispassionate science to activist creed, Carson unwittingly launched the modern idea of environmentalism"
- Following publication, Congress discussed possible solutions for years

---

## POLITICAL CONTEXT

### Environmental Movement
- 1960s saw growing awareness of pollution, environmental degradation
- April 22, 1970: First Earth Day - massive public demonstration
- Public pressure created political necessity for action

### Reorganization Strategy
- EPA consolidated programs scattered across government:
  - Federal Water Quality Administration (Interior)
  - National Air Pollution Control Administration (HEW)
  - Pesticides research (Interior, HEW, Agriculture)
  - Radiation standards (multiple agencies)
  - Solid waste management (HEW)

### Bipartisan Support
- Environmental protection had bipartisan appeal in early 1970s
- Nixon administration and Democratic Congress both supported EPA
- Clean Air Act and Clean Water Act passed with large majorities
