# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: CYBERSECURITY AND INFRASTRUCTURE SECURITY AGENCY

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

CISA is DHS's cybersecurity and critical infrastructure protection agency, established November 16, 2018. With ~$3B budget and ~3,700 employees (before 2025 cuts), it protects 16 critical infrastructure sectors and coordinates federal cyber defense. Faces 2025 restructuring with 30%+ workforce reductions and controversial election security role.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA)
**Founding:** November 16, 2018 (elevated from NPPD)
**Current Director:** Sean Plankey (nominated March 2025, awaiting confirmation); Madhu Gottumukkala (Acting)
**Parent Agency:** Department of Homeland Security
**Headquarters:** Arlington, VA (relocating to St. Elizabeths 2027)

---

## 2. BUDGET

**FY2024:** $2.8 billion (-$34M from FY2023)
**FY2025:** $3.01 billion (+4.7%)
**FY2026 Proposed:** $1.96 billion (32% cut from FY2025)
**Workforce (pre-cuts):** ~3,700 positions
**Workforce (proposed):** ~2,650 positions (1,000+ job cuts)

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**Leadership:**
- Director (presidentially appointed, Senate confirmed)
- Deputy Director
- Executive Director

**Mandatory Divisions (per 2018 Act):**

1. **Cybersecurity Division (CSD)**
   - National cyber defense
   - Threat fortification and long-term capacity building

2. **Infrastructure Security Division (ISD)**
   - Critical infrastructure from all hazards
   - Risk management and resilience

3. **Emergency Communications Division (ECD)**
   - Interoperable emergency communications
   - Priority telecommunications programs

**Additional Divisions:**
- Integrated Operations Division (10 regional offices)
- National Risk Management Center (NRMC)
- Stakeholder Engagement Division (SED) - decimated October 2025

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Critical Infrastructure Protection
- Protected Critical Infrastructure Information (PCII) Program
- National Infrastructure Protection Plan (NIPP)
- 16 critical infrastructure sectors oversight
- Sector Risk Management Agency (SRMA) for 8 sectors

### Election Security
- Designated election infrastructure as critical (January 2017)
- #PROTECT2024: 1,300+ physical security assessments
- 700+ cyber assessments; 200 tabletop exercises
- 2025 Changes: Funding cut; ~130 election security advisers eliminated

### Cyber Incident Response
- CISA Central: 24/7 situational awareness center
- National Cybersecurity Protection System (NCPS)
- National Cyber Incident Response Plan (NCIRP)

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Christopher Krebs Firing (November 2020)
- Fired via Twitter for calling 2020 election "most secure in American history"
- CISA "Rumor Control" addressed voter fraud conspiracy theories
- April 2025: Trump signed order revoking Krebs' security clearance

### Election Misinformation Role
- Monitored voting-related misinformation on social media
- House Judiciary: CISA was "nerve center" of federal social media censorship
- Supreme Court: CISA work did not constitute government suppression
- 2025: DHS Secretary Noem stated work was "far off-mission"

### 2025 Restructuring ("Gutting")
- White House proposed: 1,083 job cuts (3,732 â†’ 2,649)
- October 2025: Almost all 95 Stakeholder Engagement Division employees laid off
- Leadership exodus: Top leaders from 5 of 6 divisions departed
- "Secure by Design" leaders departed
- Slashed international, industry, academic partnerships
- Expert assessment: "Harming national security...destabilization"

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- DHS: Parent agency; independent operational component status
- NSA: Tightly integrated cyber defense coordination
- FBI: Joint ransomware response, threat intelligence sharing
- Joint Ransomware Task Force (JRTF): CISA, FBI, NSA, MS-ISAC
- SLTT Partners: State and Local Cybersecurity Grant Program ($1B over 4 years)
- Five Eyes: Densely connected with international allies
