# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: U.S. DEPARTMENT OF HOMELAND SECURITY

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS) is a Cabinet-level agency established in 2002-2003 following the September 11 terrorist attacks, consolidating 22 federal agencies into the third-largest U.S. government department. With approximately 260,000 employees and a FY2025 budget of $93.69 billion, DHS manages border security, immigration enforcement, transportation security, cybersecurity, and disaster response.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** United States Department of Homeland Security (DHS)

**Founding Date:** November 25, 2002 (Homeland Security Act); Operations began January 24, 2003

**Current Secretary:** Kristi Noem (8th Secretary, confirmed January 25, 2025)

**Headquarters:** Nebraska Avenue Complex (NAC), Washington, D.C.

**Personnel:** 260,000+ employees (third-largest Cabinet department)

---

## 2. BUDGET & APPROPRIATIONS

**FY2024:** $82.10 billion
**FY2025:** $93.69 billion (Continuing Resolution)
**FY2025 Supplemental:** $191.02 billion (largest single DHS supplemental)

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

### Seven Operational Components
1. **CBP** - U.S. Customs and Border Protection
2. **ICE** - U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement
3. **USCIS** - U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services
4. **TSA** - Transportation Security Administration
5. **FEMA** - Federal Emergency Management Agency
6. **USSS** - U.S. Secret Service
7. **USCG** - U.S. Coast Guard

### Additional Components
- CISA - Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency
- Office of Intelligence and Analysis (I&A)

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

- Border security and immigration enforcement
- Airport and transportation security (TSA)
- Disaster response (FEMA)
- Cybersecurity (CISA)
- Trusted Traveler Programs (PreCheck, Global Entry)

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Family Separation Crisis (2018)
- Zero Tolerance policy separated asylum-seeking families
- 5,400+ children separated; poor record-keeping hampered reunification
- DHS OIG found 41% of records contained separation information

### TSA Security Failures
- 2015: Failed 95% of security tests
- 2017: Failed 50-80% of tests
- Systematic screening failures documented

### FEMA Staff Reassignments (2025)
- Hundreds of FEMA employees reassigned to ICE during hurricane season

### Surveillance Overreach
- I&A monitored protesters, activists, journalists (2020)
- Social media surveillance programs

---

## 6. INTERAGENCY RELATIONSHIPS

- DOJ: Immigration enforcement, Joint Terrorism Task Forces
- DOD: Border security support
- State: Visa coordination
- Intelligence Community: I&A membership
