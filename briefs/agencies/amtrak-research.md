# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: AMTRAK

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Amtrak is the national passenger railroad, established May 1, 1971. With $2.4B+ annual appropriations plus $22B IIJA funding, it operates 300 trains daily across 46 states. Faces perpetual $1B annual operating losses, NEC infrastructure crisis, COVID ridership recovery, and state cost-sharing tensions.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Legal Name:** National Railroad Passenger Corporation (Amtrak)
**Founding:** May 1, 1971 (Rail Passenger Service Act of 1970)
**Current CEO:** Richard Anderson (since 2017)
**Headquarters:** Washington, D.C.
**Operations:**
- ~300 trains daily
- 500+ stations
- 46 states + D.C.
- 21,800 route miles

---

## 2. BUDGET

**FY2023 Appropriation:** $2.4 billion
- Northeast Corridor: $1.3 billion
- National Network: $1.2 billion

**IIJA Funding (FY2022-2026):** $22.0 billion
- NEC: $6.0 billion
- National Network: $16.0 billion
- Obligated (March 2024): $12.9 billion

**Historical Subsidies:** $45+ billion since 1971
**FY2024:** Record ridership (32.8M) and revenues

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**Board of Directors (10 voting members):**
- Secretary of Transportation (ex officio)
- CEO (ex officio)
- 8 presidentially-nominated, Senate-confirmed members

**Six Service Lines:**
1. Northeast Corridor (NEC) - Acela, Northeast Regional
2. State-Supported Routes - 28 routes, 17 states
3. Long-Distance Routes - 14 routes, 39 states
4. Northeast Regional
5. Acela Express
6. Fleet Modernization Program

**NEC Operations:**
- 2,200 daily trains (Amtrak + commuter + freight)
- Amtrak owns 2,600 track miles

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

### Northeast Corridor
- Boston-Washington-New York
- Busiest passenger rail in North America
- Top speeds: 160 mph on select segments
- Major infrastructure investment underway

### State-Supported Routes
- Routes under 750 miles
- Cost-sharing with state governments
- 28 routes, 17 states, 20 transit partners

### Long-Distance Routes
- Routes over 750 miles
- Federal funding only
- 14 corridors serving 300+ stations

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Chronic Operating Losses
- Perpetual ~$1 billion annual losses acknowledged
- Expected 79% cost recovery by FY2027
- Never profitable in 50+ year history
- Long-distance losses: $450M â†’ $889M projected by 2027

### COVID-19 Impact
- March 2020: Ridership dropped 97%
- FY2020: 50% decline, $800M+ losses
- FY2024: Record 32.8M trips (full recovery)
- Persistent loss of high-revenue business travelers

### NEC Infrastructure Crisis
- Aging electrical, signal, track systems
- May-June 2024: Multiple major service failures
- 9,500+ hardware replacements underway
- $112M Federal-State Partnership grants

### State Cost-Sharing Tensions
- Disputes over decision-making control
- Cost-calculation methodology issues
- ~1/3 of state partners report low trust
- Capital cost allocation deadlock
- FY2018-2020: States $175M vs. Amtrak $515M

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- DOT: Granting agency with withholding authority
- SAIPRC: State-Amtrak committee for cost-sharing
- State Governments: Cost-sharing partners for <750-mile routes
- Commuter Rail: Shared NEC infrastructure coordination
