# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: DEFENSE INTELLIGENCE AGENCY

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Defense Intelligence Agency is the Department of Defense's primary intelligence agency, providing military intelligence to warfighters, defense policymakers, and force planners. With approximately 16,500 employees and classified funding within the Military Intelligence Program, DIA produces approximately one-quarter of the President's Daily Brief content.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** Defense Intelligence Agency

**Founding Date:** October 1, 1961

**Current Director:** Position in transition (Lt. Gen. Jeffrey Kruse removed August 2025)

**Headquarters:** The Pentagon, Washington, D.C.

---

## 2. BUDGET INFORMATION

**Framework:** Military Intelligence Program (MIP) and National Intelligence Program (NIP)
**FY2026 MIP Request:** $33.6 billion (total)
**DIA Specific:** Classified

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

### Five Primary Directorates
1. Directorate of Analysis
2. Directorate of Operations (includes Defense Clandestine Service)
3. Directorate for Science and Technology
4. Directorate for Mission Services
5. Regional and Functional Centers

### Defense Clandestine Service (DCS)
- ~500 clandestine operatives
- Conducts HUMINT operations globally

### Defense Attaché System
- 100+ embassy locations worldwide

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

- Military intelligence production
- Defense attaché program
- Combatant command support (J2 operations)
- Counterintelligence

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### Iraq WMD Intelligence Failure
- Pre-war analysis proved fundamentally wrong
- DIA assessments more skeptical than CIA but still flawed
- Senate investigation documented failure

### Able Danger Program (9/11)
- Claims program identified al-Qaeda cells before 9/11
- Senate finding: "Did not identify Mohamed Atta"

### Intelligence Politicization (2025)
- Director removed after Iran strike assessment
- Assessment contradicted administration claims
