# ANALYTICAL BRIEF: SMALL BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The SBA is an independent federal agency providing support to small businesses through the "3 Cs": Capital, Contracts, and Counseling. Manages $500 billion portfolio; deploys ~$50 billion annually. Recent focus on pandemic fraud recovery.

---

## 1. OFFICIAL INFORMATION

**Full Name:** United States Small Business Administration

**Founding Date:** July 30, 1953

**Current Administrator:** Kelly Loeffler (28th Administrator, February 2025)

**Headquarters:** 409 Third Street SW, Washington, DC

---

## 2. BUDGET & APPROPRIATIONS

**FY2025:** $1.6 billion total
**Disaster Loans Admin:** $406 million
**SBDCs:** $140 million
**OIG:** $47 million

---

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

- 10 Regional Offices
- 68 District Offices
- ~900 Small Business Development Centers
- 110 Women's Business Centers
- 350 SCORE chapters

---

## 4. KEY PROGRAMS

- 7(a) Loan Guarantee Program (up to $5M)
- 504 Loan Program (fixed assets)
- Disaster Loans (direct lending)
- 8(a) Business Development (disadvantaged)
- HUBZone Program
- Federal Contracting (23% goal)

---

## 5. DOCUMENTED CONTROVERSIES

### PPP Fraud (~$200 billion estimated)
- $1.2 trillion in COVID funds disbursed
- Major fraud cases (Blueacorn: $65M)
- DOJ: 524 individuals charged

### 8(a) Program Scrutiny
- 2023 court ruling on racial presumptions
- 2025 comprehensive audit ordered
- DOJ bribery scheme ($500M+)

### EIDL Delays
- 600,000+ backlog at peak
- Program closed May 2022

---

## 6. RELATIONSHIPS

- Treasury: Contracting oversight, investigations
- Commerce: 8(a) contract administration
- Federal agencies: 23% contracting goal
