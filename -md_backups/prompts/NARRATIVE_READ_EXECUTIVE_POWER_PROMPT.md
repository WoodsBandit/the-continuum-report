## NARRATIVE STRUCTURE

### Opening Hook
Start with a visceral comparison — the Boston Tea Party vs. modern paycheck withholding. Draw the reader in with the absurdity of the contrast. Make them feel the weight of the question: "How did we get here?"

### Act I: The Constitution's Promise (Pre-1913)
- Limited federal government, enumerated powers
- Income tax attempted and struck down (Pollock 1895)
- The Supreme Court said NO — direct taxes must be apportioned
- Set the scene: Americans understood the Constitution protected them

### Act II: The Year Everything Changed (1913)
- 16th Amendment ratified (income tax)
- Federal Reserve Act passed
- Pujo Committee exposed the "Money Trust" — then Congress handed them the keys
- The irony: they investigated concentrated banking power, then created the Fed

### Act III: Emergency Powers Never End (1917-1945)
- Trading with the Enemy Act (1917) — wartime measure, never repealed
- FDR's gold confiscation (EO 6102, 1933) — "emergency" that lasted 41 years
- Emergency Banking Act — amended TWEA to apply to Americans in peacetime
- The pattern emerges: crisis → power grab → permanent expansion

### Act IV: The Courts Draw a Line... Sort Of (1952)
- Youngstown Steel Seizure — Truman overreached, got slapped down
- Justice Jackson's framework: when Congress says no, the President can't just do it anyway
- A rare moment of restraint — but note what survived

### Act V: Nixon Shock & The Final Gold Betrayal (1971)
- Bretton Woods promised dollars backed by gold
- Nixon closed the gold window — by executive decree
- No vote. No constitutional amendment. Just done.
- Connect to EO 6102: the 1933 confiscation made this possible

### Act VI: The Permanent Emergency State (1976-Present)
- National Emergencies Act was supposed to limit emergencies — instead it codified them
- IEEPA gave presidents vast economic powers
- Count the active emergencies today (dozens, spanning decades)

### Act VII: The Administrative Fourth Branch
- APA (1946) created the regulatory state
- Chevron (1984) told courts to defer to agencies
- Agencies now write rules, enforce rules, and judge violations of rules
- Loper Bright (2024) — a crack in the edifice?

### Act VIII: Surveillance as Governance (1978-2001)
- FISA created secret courts
- PATRIOT Act expanded everything
- The government that couldn't tax income in 1895 now monitors your communications

### Closing: The Question We Must Answer
- Not conspiracy — documented history
- Not Left vs. Right — a ratchet that only turns one direction
- The tea was 3%. What percentage are you paying now?
- End with a call to awareness, not despair
